267. WHAT [CREATURES] OF THE LAND AND SEA ARE [PERMITTED] TO BE EATEN

812. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no good in anything in the water except for fish.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

813. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Eat of that which water ebbs from [leaving it behind so that it dies] and what it casts forth, but do not eat that which floats [on top of the water having died of natural causes].’”2056

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

814. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Eat all fish except for that which floats [on top of the water having died a natural death].’”2057

815. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, ‘I wish I had one or two small baskets2058 of locusts.’”2059

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. The floating fish (ṭāfī) is that which dies in the sea and then floats on the surface of the water without sinking into it. According to us it is disapproved to eat floating fish, but the three Imams said there is no harm in it because of the general sense of his statement , “In the sea’s water is purity, and that which is dead in it is ḥalāl.” The answer to that is that what is meant by “that which is dead in” the sea is the fish the cause of whose death was the sea itself in that the sea cast it up on the seashore or withdrew to leave it stranded, not the one that died in the sea of natural causes and then floats up on the surface of the water, which is proved by the prohibition narrated of eating floating fish that have died, as is narrated by Abū Dāwūd, “That which dies within it and floats, do not eat it.” ↩
  2. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.4, p.254. ↩
  3. A qafʿah is something resembling a basket of palm leaves (zabīl) without handle and not being large. ↩
  4. An-Nawawī narrated that there is unanimous agreement that it is ḥalāl to eat locusts. ↩
