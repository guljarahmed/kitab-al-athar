276. DRINKING FROM VESSELS, RECEPTACLES, AND CERAMIC POTS ETC.

841. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAlqamah ibn Marthad narrated to us from Ibn Buraydah from his father that the Prophet said, “I had forbidden you to visit graves but now visit them but do not say foul things, because permission has been granted Muḥammad to visit the grave of his mother. [I had forbidden you] to keep the meat of Aḍḥā sacrifices for more than three days but now keep it as long as you wish and take it as travelling provision, because I only forbade you in order that your wealthy people would be generous to your poor. [I had forbidden you] to drink nabīdh from gourds,2114 ceramic pots,2115 and jars smeared with pitch2116 but now drink from every receptacle because the receptacle makes nothing ḥalāl or ḥarām, but do not drink intoxicants.”’”2117 2118

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

842. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Isḥāq ibn Thābit narrated to us from his father from ʿAlī ibn Ḥusayn that the Prophet went on the military expedition to Tabūk and passed by people behaving indecently.2119 He said to them, “What is wrong with these people?” They have been overcome by a drink of theirs.” He asked, “What receptacles do they use?” They said, “Gourds, ceramic pots, and jars smeared with pitch,” and so he forbade them to drink from them. When he passed by them on the return journey from his military expedition they complained to him of the indigestion they were experiencing and so he permitted them to drink from them and forbade them to drink strong pressed date-juice.’”2120

Muḥammad said, “It is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

843. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘”That of which a lot intoxicates, a little of is ḥarām,” is a mistake on the part of people. They only meant that intoxication from any drink is ḥarām.’”2121 2122

Muḥammad said, “It is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

844. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Sālim al-Afṭas narrated to us from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr that Ibn ʿUmar drank from a waterskin while standing.’”2123 2124

“We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

  2. Red or, some say, green earthenware jars. ↩
  3. The prohibition of preparing nabīdh in these vessels was at the very beginning of Islam and was then later abrogated and that is the position according to us. Mālik and Aḥmad took the position that the prohibition is still in effect. ↩

  5. Muslim narrated it under “funerals” in the chapter on “the Prophet seeking permission from his Lord to visit his mother’s grave.” ↩
  6. Speaking indecently and behaving ignorantly with laughter and mockery. There is in the Āthār of Abū Yūsuf that they were dancing because of their intoxication. ↩
  7. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on pp.225-6. ↩

However it is particularly about wine. The sound position in general is that intoxication is ḥarām from every drink whether or not it is wine. ↩
