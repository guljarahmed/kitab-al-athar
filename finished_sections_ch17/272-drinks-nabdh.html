272. DRINKS, NABĪDH DRINKS, DRINKING STANDING, AND THOSE DRINKS WHICH ARE ABHORENT

829. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Sulaymān ash-Shaybānī that Ibn Ziyād2087 broke his fast with ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar and he [ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar] gave him [Ibn Ziyād] some of his drink and it was as if it had overwhelmed him (akhadhahu fīhi). When he woke up in the morning he asked, ‘What was this drink? I could hardly find my way to my house.’ ʿAbdullāh said, ‘We gave you nothing more than [an infusion of steeped] ʿajwah [dates] and raisins.’”2088

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

830. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Nāfiʿ from Ibn ʿUmar that he [Nāfiʿ] used to prepare a nabīdh infusion of raisins for him [Ibn ʿUmar] and he did not find it wholesome2089 so he said to his slave girl, ‘Throw some dried dates in it.’”2090

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

831. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that he said, ‘There is no harm in drinking nabīdh infusions of dates and raisins if you mix them. They were only disapproved because of the severity of their living situation in the first age just as fat and meat were disapproved of. As for when Allah, exalted is He, has been generous to the Muslims, there is no harm in these two.’”2091

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
