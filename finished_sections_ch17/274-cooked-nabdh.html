274. COOKED NABĪDH AND PRESSED FRUIT JUICE

836. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If pressed fruit juice2103 is cooked and two-thirds of it evaporates leaving a third before it ferments,2104 there is no harm in it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

837. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm used to drink cooked pressed juice of grapes (ṭalā’) when two-thirds of it had evaporated2105 and a third remained and nabīdh would be made out of it for him. Then he would leave it until it became strong, when he would drink it and would not see anything wrong in that.”2106 2107

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

838. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Walīd ibn Sarīʿ (the freed slave of ʿAmr ibn Ḥarīth) narrated to us that Anas ibn Mālik used to drink cooked pressed grape juice [when it had been cooked so that] a half [remained].”2108 2109

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this. One ought not to drink any cooked pressed grape-juice except that of which two-thirds have evaporated and a third remains, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. If the pressed juice of grapes is cooked until two thirds of it has gone and one third remains then it is ḥalāl even if it is strong. This is according to Abū Ḥanīfah and Abū Yūsuf. According to Muḥammad, ash-Shāfiʿī and Mālik it is ḥarām. This is the difference of opinion if one intends by it to strengthen oneself, but if one intends by it to entertain oneself it is not ḥalāl and that is agreed upon unanimously. ↩
  2. If it ferments and becomes strong it is also permissible according to Abū Ḥanīfah and Abū Yūsuf. ↩
  3. “When two thirds of it had evaporated” is the explanation of what ṭalā’ is. ↩
  4. By this it is established that strong nabīdh which is not intoxicating is permissible, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad. [Ash-Shāfiʿī, and Mālik considered it ḥarām. However, if it ferments it is ḥarām according to everyone. – Ed.] ↩
  5. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated the like of it in the Muṣannaf, vol.5, p.89. ↩
  6. And it is that of which a half has evaporated by cooking and pressed fruit juice when it is cooked and less than a two-thirds evaporates, and it is ḥarām if it ferments and becomes strong and is cast into the zabad according to the Imam and if it becomes strong according to the two of them [Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad]. ↩
