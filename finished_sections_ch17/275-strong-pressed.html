275. STRONG PRESSED DATE-JUICE2110 AND WINE

839. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham from Ibn Masʿūd that a man2111 with jaundice2112 came to him and asked him about strong pressed date-juice, and he forbade him [to drink it].”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

840. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Ibn Masʿūd said, ‘Your children are born in the natural state (fiṭrah) so do not treat them medically with wine nor nourish them with it; Allah has not made impurity a healing. Their [the children’s] wrong action will only be the liability of those who give it to them to drink.’”2113

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. as-sakar is pressed date-juice when it becomes strong. ↩
  2. Nuʿaym ibn al-ʿAwwām. ↩
  3. ṣafar is jaundice, but also hunger. The word might equally be ṣufr or ṣifr, but I have chosen the meaning of jaundice since it seems to indicate that the man thought that he might have a medical excuse for drinking the strong date-juice. – Trans. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.227. ↩
