118. WHAT MARRIAGE IS FORBIDDEN, AND CONSULTING A VIRGIN

404. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr narrated to us from one of the men1243 of Shām from the Prophet saying, “A man came to him1244 and said, ‘Messenger of Allah, should I marry so-and-so?’ And he forbade him to do so. Later he came to him three times, but he forbade him and then he said, ‘A prolifically fertile black woman is preferable to me over a sterile1245 but extremely beautiful woman. I want to contend1246 with the other nations in terms of your plentiful numbers. Even the miscarried child1247 will continue to be angry1248 when it is said to him, “Enter the Garden!” and so he will say, “No! not until my parents enter.”’”1249

405. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A virgin is not to be married until her consent is sought,1250 and [the sign of] her agreement is her silence.’ He said, ‘She knows herself best; perhaps she may have a defect so that men may not be able to endure her along with it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We do not think that a virgin who has reached puberty should be married without her consent, whether her parent or anyone else marries her off, and [the sign of] her agreement is her silence. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
