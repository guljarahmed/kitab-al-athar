126. SOMEONE MARRIES AND THEN EITHER OF THE COUPLE COMMITS ADULTERY

430. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, ‘If a man married a woman but had not yet consummated the marriage when he subsequently committed adultery, he is to be flogged1301 and he retains his wife. If she committed adultery and he had not yet consummated the marriage so that the ḥadd1302 punishment be inflicted on her,1303 they are separated.’”

Muḥammad said, “As for the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and what people in general hold to, she is his wife in every case. If he wishes he may divorce her and if he wishes he may retain her, and that is our verdict.”

431. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A man came to ʿAlqamah ibn Qays and said, “Can a man who fornicated with a woman marry her?” He said, “Yes,” and then he recited this āyah, “It is He who accepts tawbah from His slaves and pardons evil acts and knows what you do” (Sūrat ash-Shūrā: 23).’”1304

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  3. The text seems mistakenly to have ʿalayhi – “inflicted upon him” instead of ʿalayhā – “inflicted on her.” – Trans. ↩
