123. WHOEVER MARRIES A JEWISH OR CHRISTIAN WOMAN, SHE DOES NOT CONFER THE STATUS OF BEING MUḤṢAN1272

414. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no harm in marrying a Jewish or Christian woman when already married to a free woman.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

415. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Ḥudhayfah ibn al-Yamān that he married a Jewish woman in Madā’in, and so ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb wrote to him, ‘Let her go.’ He wrote back to him, ‘Is she forbidden, Amīr al-Mu’minin?’ He wrote to him, ‘I stress the great urgency in your1273 not putting down my letter before letting her go, because I fear that the Muslims will model themselves on you, and so choose the women of the people of the dhimmah contract because of their beauty, and that is sufficient as a trial for Muslim women.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We do not think1274 that it is forbidden, but we think that one should choose Muslim women in preference to them and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

416. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said “A Muslim is not made muḥṣan1275 by [marriage to] a Jewish woman nor a Christian woman. He is only made muḥṣan by [marriage to] a free Muslim woman.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. muḥṣan comprises being married or having been married, such that if the person commits adultery they would be liable to the punishment of stoning rather than the lesser punishment of flogging. ↩
  2. Contrary to our original text which had aʿẓimu ʿalayka but in agreement with the text prepared for us, Ḥāfiẓ Riyāḍ Aḥmad al-Multānī has aʿzimu ʿalayka and says it means, “I adjure you.” – Trans. ↩
  3. But it is more appropriate that one not marry women of the People of the Book and that their meat not be eaten. ↩
  4. i.e., marriage to a kāfir woman of the People of the Book does not make a Muslim muḥṣan [such that if he committed adultery he would be stoned]. Abū Yūsuf said in his al-Kharāj, “And the best of what we have heard respecting that is that the matter of being muḥṣan, and Allah knows best, is that a free Muslim only becomes muḥṣan by marriage to a free woman, and if he is married to a woman of the People of the Book, he becomes muḥṣan but she does not become muḥṣanah by him.” ↩
