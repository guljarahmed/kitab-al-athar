129. THE MARRIAGE OF AN INEBRIATED PERSON

439. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the inebriated person who marries, ‘Everything which he does is legally binding upon him.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this except in one respect: if his intellect was absent because of drunkenness and he reneged1321 on Islam and then later recovered from his drunkenness and remembered that that had issued from him unconsciously, then that is accepted from him, and his wife is not finally divorced from him. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Because kufr and apostasy are in the category of belief, and belief is not a reality when intoxicated. This is a ruling of juridical discretion. Analogically she becomes finally divorced from him, and it is thus in al-Mabsūṭ. ↩
