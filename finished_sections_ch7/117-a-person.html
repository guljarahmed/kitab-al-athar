117. A PERSON, MALE OR FEMALE, WHO MARRIES HAVING A DEFECT

401. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who marries while healthy or who marries while suffering an affliction, “[if he is afflicted] his wife is not given the choice [of her freedom] nor her family. She is his wife forever and he is not compelled to divorce her.” He said, “If he marries her and she is thus,1234 then she has the same standing.”’”1235

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. But our verdict is that if the wife has a defect, then the verdict is that of Abū Ḥanīfah. If the man is the one with the defect but the defect is bearable then the verdict is as Abū Ḥanīfah said, but if the defect is unbearable1236 then he is in the position of someone who is completely castrated1237 or an impotent person,1238 and his wife is given the choice [of her freedom]. So if she wishes she can stay with him, and if she wishes she can separate from him.”

402. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who marries a woman who has a defect or an illness, ‘She is his wife whom he may divorce or retain. She is not in this case in the same position as slave women whom he may return because of a defect.’ He said, ‘Do you think that if the man had a defect, she should be able to return him?’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because divorce is in the hands of the husband, and if he wishes he may divorce and if he wishes he may retain her. Do you not see that if he had discovered her to be physically incapable of intercourse1239 he would not have the choice [of returning her] since divorce is in his hands. However, if she had discovered him to be completely castrated, she would have the choice [of her freedom] since divorce is not in her hands. It would be similar if she had discovered him to be mad and subject to insane promptings1240 and one feared for her sake lest he kill her, or if she had discovered him to have leprosy (judhām)1241 to such a degree that he was losing bodily parts and she was unable to approach him, and other similar cases of unbearable defects, then all of these are more serious than impotence and castration, and it has been narrated concerning impotence that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, ‘She is set the term of a year and then she is given the choice [of her freedom].’ It has also been narrated from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb concerning the person subject to insane promptings that he set a time for her and then later gave her the choice [of her freedom]. It is similar with defects that are unbearable; they are worse than castration and impotence.”

403. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who marries a woman and discovers that she has judhām leprosy or leuce leprosy,1242 ‘She is his wife, and if he wishes he may divorce her or if he wishes he may retain her.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this since divorce is in his hands.”
