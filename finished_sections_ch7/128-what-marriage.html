128. WHAT MARRIAGE IS FORBIDDEN TO MEN

436. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Ḥakam ibn ʿUtaybah narrated to us from ʿIrāk ibn Mālik that Aflaḥ ibn Abī Quʿays1313 sought permission to visit ʿĀ’ishah and she veiled herself from him.1314 He said, “Do you veil yourself from me when I am your paternal uncle1315?” She said, “From where?1316” He said, “You were suckled1317 by the milk [of the wife] of my brother.” When the Prophet came to visit, she mentioned that to him and he said, “That which is unmarriageable1318 because of blood relationship is also unmarriageable because of suckling.”’”1319

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

437.1320 Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibrāhīm ibn Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir narrated to us from his father from Masrūq who said, “Sell this slave girl of mine. As for me, I did nothing with her but that fondling or looking which would make her unmarriageable to my own son.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, except that we do not regard looking as anything significant unless he looked at her pudendum with sexual appetite. If he had looked at it with sexual appetite she would become ḥarām to his father and to his son, and her mother and daughter would become unmarriageable to him, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

438. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm who said, ‘If a man kisses his mother-in-law or touches her with appetite his wife becomes ḥarām for him.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. As-Sanbahlī said, “The correct position is that it was Aflaḥ the brother of Abū Quʿays. ↩
  2. After [the revelation of] the āyah on veiling. ↩
  3. By suckling. ↩
  4. i.e., “how can it be established that you are a paternal uncle?” ↩
  5. In another narration it is, “My brother’s wife suckled you with my brother’s milk.” ↩
  6. ḥarām means here unmarriageable. It has not only the sense of prohibition, but of admitting to the inner sanctum of the family. ↩
  7. Al-Bukhārī narrated it under “marriage” in the chapter on “what consummation is permissible,” and Muslim under “breast-feeding” in the chapter on “breast-feeding renders [someone] a maḥram.” ↩
  8. Know that they agree that whoever has sexual intercourse with a woman through his ownership, then she is ḥarām to his sons. They differed concerning embrace and touching with sexual appetite and gazing. Ash-Shāfiʿī said, “It is not clearly established that the sanctity of being an in-law comes about by passionate glances or by sexual caresses.” Abū Ḥanīfah said that such sanctity does take place. ↩
