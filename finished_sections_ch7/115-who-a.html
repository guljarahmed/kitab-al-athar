115. WHO A SLAVE IS PERMITTED TO MARRY

393. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A slave may only marry two free women or two slave women.’”1222

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

394. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A slave is not permitted to keep slave girls,1223 and intercourse is only permitted him in a marriage which his master expedites.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

395. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ismāʿīl ibn Umayyah al-Makkī narrated to us from Saʿīd ibn Abī Saʿīd al-Maqburī that Ibn ʿUmar said, “No one is permitted to have sexual intercourse with a slave woman except the purchaser,1224 someone who is given [a slave woman] as a gift or as a ṣadaqah, or if he is set free; then it is permitted, meaning by that1225 the slave.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. It means that the slave is only permitted sexual intercourse in marriage, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

396. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘It is not permitted for a slave to take a concubine,’1226 and then he read this āyah, “... except from their wives or those they own as slaves” (Sūrat al-Mu’minūn: 61227 and Sūrat al-Maʿārij: 30) and she is not his wife nor his property.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

397. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said that when a master marries his slave to someone, the slave has the right to divorce, but if a slave marries without his master’s permission,1228 the master has the right to declare the divorce and to take back from the woman what she accepted from his slave.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

398. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a slave marries without his master’s permission, his marriage is invalid, but if he [the master] gives permission after he had already married, his marriage is valid.’”1229

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. What he meant by his saying, ‘if he gives permission after he had already married’ is that he is really saying that if he endorses what the slave had already done, it is valid, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
