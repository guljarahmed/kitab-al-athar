125. A MAN MARRIES A SLAVE WOMAN AND THEN PURCHASES HER OR SHE IS SET FREE

422. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who marries a slave woman1283 and then divorces her one time and then purchases her,1284 ‘He may have sexual intercourse with her. If he1285 sets her free he1286 is allowed to marry her. If he divorces her twice1287 and then later purchases her, she is not permissible to him until she marries a husband other than him [who consummates the marriage and subsequently divorces her].’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

423. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said that if a free man divorces a slave woman she becomes finally divorced1288 by two divorces1289 and her ʿiddah is two menstrual periods if she is menstruating, but if she is not menstruating, then it is a month and a half, and she is not permissible to him until she has married another husband [who consummates the marriage and subsequently divorces her]. If a slave divorces his wife and she is a free woman she becomes finally separated from him by three [divorces] and her ʿiddah is three menstrual periods if she has a menstrual period, but if she does not have a menstrual period then her ʿiddah is three months.”1290

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. Divorce is according to the women and the ʿiddah is according to the women. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

424. Muḥammad said, “Ibrāhīm ibn Yazīd al-Makkī informed us saying, ‘I heard ʿAṭā’ ibn Abī Rabāḥ say, “ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, ‘Divorce is according to the women as is the ʿiddah.’”’”

“We adhere to this. We say that if the wife is a free woman then her divorce is three divorces and her ʿiddah is three menstruations whether her husband is free or a slave. If she is a slave woman her divorce is twice and her ʿiddah is two menstrual periods whether her husband is free or a slave.”

425. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning a man who marries a slave woman who is subsequently set free. He said, ‘She is given the choice, and if she chooses her husband then she is his wife. However, if she chooses herself, he has no way to her. If he dies and she had chosen him, then her ʿiddah is four months and ten days1291 and she inherits. However, if he dies and she had chosen herself, then her ʿiddah is three menstruations1292 and she has no inheritance.’”1293

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

426. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a slave woman is set free and she has a husband, then she is given the choice. If she chooses her husband, they remain in their marriage. If he had consummated the marriage, she has the dowry for her master.1294 If she chooses her self and he had not consummated the marriage, they are separated, and neither she nor her master have a dowry since the separation comes from her. Her separation is not a divorce and she is allowed to marry on the spot if she wishes.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

427. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a slave woman whose husband dies and then she is set free during her ʿiddah, ‘She observes the ʿiddah of a slave woman and she does not inherit. If he divorced her twice1295 and then she was set free, she observes the ʿiddah of a slave woman.’”1296

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

428. Muḥammad and Asad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Salamah ibn Kuhayl from al-Mustawrid ibn al-Aḥnaf from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd that a man came to him and said, ‘I married a slave woman belonging to my paternal uncle and she bore me a daughter, and now my uncle wants to sell her.’ He said, ‘He lies. He has no right to do that.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. He does not have the right to sell. Whoever owns1297 a near relative1298 who is forbidden to him in marriage,1299 then he is free.”1300

429. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘When a slave woman is divorced by her husband with a divorce in which he retains the right to take her back and she is subsequently set free, her ʿiddah is that of a freewoman. But if the husband did not have the right to take her back and she is set free, her ʿiddah is that of a slave woman.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
