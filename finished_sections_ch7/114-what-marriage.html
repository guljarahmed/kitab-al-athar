MARRIAGE

114. WHAT MARRIAGE IS PERMITTED TO A FREE MAN

390. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Qays ibn Muslim al-Jadalī narrated to us from al-Ḥasan ibn Muḥammad ibn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib concerning the words of Allah, “And also married women,1216 except for those you have taken in war as slaves” (Sūrat an-Nisā’: 24) that he said, “He used to say, ‘... then marry other permissible women, two, three or four’ and he said, ‘Four are permitted to you, and “Ḥarām for you are: your mothers ...” to the end of the āyah (Sūrat an-Nisā’: 23).’ He said, ‘Married women are fobidden for you except for those you have taken in war as slaves,1217 besides the four.’”’”

391. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man weds a slave woman when already married to a free woman, then the marriage to the slave woman is invalid,1218 but if he marries a free woman when already married to a slave woman then he retains both of them, and he appoints two nights to the free woman and a single night to the slave woman.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

392. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A free man may marry four slave women,1219 or three, two or one.’”1220

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. He may marry the same number of slave women as the number of free women he is able to marry,1221 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Following on from His words, exalted is He, “...your mothers...” (Sūrat an-Nisā’: 23) i.e., “Ḥarām for you are: your mothers and your daughters and your sisters ... and ḥarām for you are married women.” However, this is not unqualifiedly so, but on the contrary forbidden after the four, which is shown by His words, exalted is He, at the beginning of the surah, “...then marry other permissible women.” (Sūrat an-Nisā’: 3) The meaning of muḥṣanāt according to the well-known position is those possessing husbands. ↩
  2. Although the āyah literally says, “Those whom your right hands own,” it refers here to slaves taken in war, since a man may not have sexual relations with one of his slave women who is in a legitimate marriage. Wives of combatants captured in war are automatically divorced and are thus not under this restriction. ↩
  3. Because of the hadith of Jābir , “One should not marry a slave woman when already married to a free woman.” Al-Bayhaqī narrated from al-Ḥasan that the Prophet forbade someone already married to a free woman to marry a slave woman. ↩
  4. And ash-Shāfiʿī said that he may only marry one. ↩
  5. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.131. ↩
  6. In total four, not eight comprising four free women and four slave women. ↩
