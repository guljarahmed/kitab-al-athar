119. SOMEONE WHO MARRIES WITHOUT SETTING AN AMOUNT OF HIS WIFE’S DOWRY BEFORE HE DIES

406. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd that someone came to him and asked him about a man who married a woman without setting a dowry1251 for her, and without consummating the marriage before dying. He said, ‘Nothing has reached me about this from the Messenger of Allah .’ He said, ‘So speak about it according to your own view.’ He said, ‘I think that she should receive the full dowry,1252 that she inherits and that she has to observe the ʿiddah period [for a widow].’ One of those men who sat with him said, ‘You have passed judgement – by the One by Whom oaths are sworn!1253 – with the ruling of the Messenger of Allah in [the case of] Birwaʿ1254 bint Wāshiq al-Ashjaʿiyyah.’” He said, “ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd rejoiced to such an extent as he had never done before because of the concordance of his view with the verdict of the Messenger of Allah .”1255

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The inheritance and ʿiddah period are not obligatory until there has been a dowry before that, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Muḥammad said, “The man who said what he said to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd was Maʿqil ibn Yasār al-Ashjaʿī1256 who was one of the Companions of the Messenger of Allah .”
