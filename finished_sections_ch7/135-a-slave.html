135. A SLAVE WOMAN WHO IS SOLD OR GIVEN AS A GIFT WHILE HAVING A HUSBAND

458. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Ibn Masʿūd said concerning the slave woman who is sold while having a husband, ‘Her sale is her divorce.’”1349

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but we adhere to the hadith of the Messenger of Allah when ʿĀ’ishah bought Barīrah and then set her free, so the Messenger of Allah gave her the choice between staying with her husband or choosing her self. If her sale had been her divorce, he would not have given her any choice.”1350

459. “And it has reached us from ʿUmar, ʿAlī, ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf, Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ and Ḥudhayfah that they did not regard her sale as her divorce, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

460. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham who said, ‘A slave girl was given as a gift to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib and she had a husband who worked for him. So he wrote to her original owner, “You have sent me a slave girl who is taken.”’”1351

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this: neither her sale nor her being given as a gift is equivalent to her divorce,1352 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

461. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū’l-ʿAṭūf1353 narrated to us from az-Zuhrī that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd bought a slave girl1354 from his wife Zaynab ath-Thaqafiyyah.1355 She laid as a condition on him1356 that if he was in no need of her she would have more right to her [and could purchase her] for the same price. He met ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and mentioned that to him and he said, “It does not please me1357 that you should approach her while there is a condition on her,”1358 and so ʿAbdullāh went back and returned her.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Every condition laid on a sale, which is not part of the sale itself1359 but in which there is some benefit for the seller, or the purchaser or the slave girl, corrupts the sale, as in this case and similar ones, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
