120. SOMEONE WHO MARRIES A WOMAN DURING HER ʿIDDAH AND THEN LATER DIVORCES HER

407. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who marries a woman during her ʿiddah, ‘His divorcing her does not affect her,1257 and if he accuses her of sexual impropriety he is not to be flogged1258 and does not have to declare the liʿān1259 type of divorce.’”1260

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

408. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a woman who married during her ʿiddah and gave birth, ‘If the former [husband] lays claim to it, it is his child. If the former repudiates it and the other [husband] lays claim to it then it is his [the latter’s] child. If they are both in doubt, it is the child of both of them and inherits from both of them and they both inherit from it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but rather we think that if he divorces her and someone else marries her during her ʿiddah and consummates the marriage with her, that if she then gives birth to a child anything up to two years after the latter’s consummation of the marriage then it is the child of the first husband, but if the birth is more than two years later then it is the child of the other.” He said, “Abū Ḥanīfah also used to give a similar verdict to that concerning a final (bā’in1261) divorce.”

409. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said concerning a woman who marries during her ʿiddah, ‘She and her second husband are separated and she has a dowry inasmuch as he consummated the marriage1262 and she must complete what remains of her ʿiddah from the former, and then observe a second independent ʿiddah from the other husband. Then the other may marry her if he wishes.’”1263

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this except that we say that she completes her ʿiddah from the first and calculates1264 what amount of time has passed of the ʿiddah from the other in her completing the ʿiddah from the first and then she observes what remains of the ʿiddah from the other husband.” [See next hadith]

410. Muḥammad said, “Saʿīd ibn Abī ʿArūbah informed us from Abū Maʿshar1265 from Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī who said, ‘If one ʿiddah enters into another then they are a single ʿiddah,’ and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and it is the clarification of our verdict in the first hadith.”
