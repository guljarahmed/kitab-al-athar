131. MARRIAGE OF EQUALS AND THE HUSBAND’S RIGHTS OVER THE WIFE

442. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from a man from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb who said, ‘I will prevent access to the pudenda of women of some standing, to all but their peers.’”1323

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If a woman marries a man who is not her peer and her guardian brings it to the attention of the ruler or judge (imam) then they are separated, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

443. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Ḥakam ibn Ziyād narrated to us, ascribing it to the Prophet , that a woman received a proposal of marriage via her father and she said, “I will not marry until I meet the Prophet and ask him what are the rights of a husband over his wife.” So she came to him and said, “Messenger of Allah, what are the rights of a husband over his wife?” He said, “If she goes out of her house without his permission, Allah continually curses her as do the angels, the Trustworthy Spirit, the custodians of mercy and the custodians of punishment, until she returns.” She said, “Messenger of Allah, what are the rights of a husband over his wife?” He said, “If he asks her for herself while she is on the point of giving birth (ʿalā dhahri qatab),1324 she may not refuse him.” She said, “Messenger of Allah, what are the rights of a husband over his wife?” He said, “If he is angry, she should content him.” A man said, “Even if he is in the wrong?” He said, “Yes, even if he is in the wrong.” She said, “I am not going to marry after what I have heard.”’”1325

444. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ayyūb ibn ʿĀ’idh aṭ-Ṭā’ī narrated to us from Mujāhid who said, “A woman came to the Prophet bringing along with her an infant son who was still breastfeeding and a son whom she had taken by the hand, and she was pregnant. Anything she asked him for he gave her out of mercy for her. When she departed, he said, ‘Pregnant, giving birth, breastfeeding and showing mercy to their children; if it had not been for how they overpower1326 their husbands those of them who pray would enter the Garden.”’”1327
