122. SOMEONE WHO MARRIES A WOMAN WHO WAS DIVORCED AT HER OWN REQUEST FOR SOME COMPENSATION PAID BY HER (KHULʿ) OR WHO WAS DIVORCED

412. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The husband of the woman whom he divorces by taking an oath to abstain from sexual intercourse for more than four months (īlā’) and his sticking to that oath, and of the woman divorced at her own request for some compensation paid by her (khulʿ) may not take her back except in a new marriage. If either of them dies, the other does not inherit, because the divorce was final.1267 However, he [may] divorce as long as she is still within the ʿiddah.’”1268

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

413. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man marries1269 [his] wife who was previously divorced at her own request for a compensation that she paid, or [marries] a woman whom he divorced having sworn an oath to deliberately abstain from sexual intercourse for more than four months, or [marries] a [slave] woman he set free,1270 during her ʿiddah, and then divorces her before consummating the marriage, she [in all these cases] is owed the dowry.’”

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. His verdict was the same with respect to every woman who was observing an ʿiddah in her divorce from a man in a valid or an invalid marriage or in other cases such as the ʿiddah of the umm walad, and then someone marries her during her ʿiddah from him and then divorces her before consummating the marriage, that he has to pay the complete dowry. The divorce is such that he possesses the right to take her back and the ʿiddah commences from the day on which he divorces her.”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but [we think] if he divorces her before consummating the marriage with her, he owes her half of the dowry,1271 and he does not possess the right to take her back and she completes what remains of her ʿiddah [from her former husband]. That is the verdict of al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī, ʿAṭā’ ibn Abī Rabāḥ, the people of the Ḥijāz, and some of them also narrated it of ash-Shaʿbī.”
