116. A MAN WHO MARRIES AWAY HIS UMM WALAD1230

399. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If an umm walad gives birth to a child1231 to someone other than her master while [she is] still an umm walad, then he is of the same status as her.1232’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

400. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning a man who marries his umm walad to a slave and she gives birth to children and then [the master] dies, and said, ‘She is free and her children are free, and she has the choice:1233 if she wishes, she remains with the slave and if she wishes, she does not.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. She also has the choice if she has been married to a free man.”

Footnotes

  1. An umm walad is a slave woman who gives birth to her master’s children, and who thus has a different status from other slave women. ↩
  2. When her master marries off his umm walad and she gives birth to a child from someone other than her master. ↩
  3. The child has the same status as his mother, meaning that if the master dies they are both freed from out of the total of the wealth because the right to freedom is transmitted to the child just as in the case of a slave who is to be set free on the death of his master because the child of a slave woman in this situation is also to be set free on the master’s death. ↩
  4. If she is set free and if her husband was a slave, then she has the choice by unanimous agreement, but if he was free, then she has no choice according to the three Imams. Abū Ḥanīfah said that she has the choice, the source for which is the hadith of Barīrah whom the Messenger of Allah gave the choice when she was set free, and according to some narrations her husband was a slave and according to others he was free. ↩
