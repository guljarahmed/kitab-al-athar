130. MARRYING A WOMAN AND FINDING THAT SHE IS NOT A VIRGIN

440. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham from ʿĀ’ishah that she married a freed slave girl of hers to a man who found her not to be a virgin. The man went out because of that, grieving so intensely that it was seen in his face. It was brought to the attention of ʿĀ’ishah and she said, ‘What has he to grieve over? Virginity can be taken away by menstruation or a finger or wuḍū’ or a leap.’”

441. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man says about a woman whom he had married, “I did not find her a virgin,” then there is no punishment [for slander]1322 to be imposed on him.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

  1. Because he did not clearly slander her. ↩
