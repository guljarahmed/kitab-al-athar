ZAKĀH

85. ZAKĀH ON GOLD AND SILVER AND ORPHANS’ PROPERTY

295. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no zakāh on less than twenty mithqāls920 of gold. When the gold reaches twenty mithqāls then one owes half of a mithqāl from it. Whatever is above that921 is reckoned by the same calculation. There is no zakāh on less than two hundred dirhams. When the silver reaches two hundred dirhams then five dirhams is owed on it and whatever is above that is reckoned by the same calculation.’”922

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to all of it except in one respect: whatever is over two hundred dirhams then there is nothing owed on it923 until it amounts to forty dirhams when one owes a single dirham on it. Whatever is over and above the twenty mithqāls of gold, then there is nothing owing on it until it reaches four mithqāls,924 and there will be [zakāh] owing on it according to the same calculation.”

296. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no zakāh on the property of orphans925 and they are not obliged to pay zakāh until they are obliged to perform the prayer.’”926

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

297. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Layth ibn Abī Sulaym narrated to us from Mujāhid that Ibn Masʿūd said, “There is no zakāh on the property of orphans.”’”927

298. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Bakr narrated to us that ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān used to say when the month of Ramaḍān came, “People, this month of your zakāh has come, so whoever owes a debt928 should pay it and then pay the zakāh on what remains.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. One owes zakāh after settling one’s debts.”

299. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us from Ibn Sīrīn that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, “When you are owed a debt by people and you are repaid, then pay the zakāh on it for the time that has elapsed.”’”929

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

300. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who lent another a thousand dirhams, ‘Its zakāh is owed by the one who uses it and benefits from it.’”930

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but we adhere to the verdict of ʿAlī that its owner [who lent the money] pays its zakāh, and that when he receives it he pays the zakāh on it for the time which has elapsed.”
