94. A MAN WHO GIVES ALL HIS WEALTH FOR THE BEREFT

322. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man gives all his wealth to the bereft, then he has to investigate what will suffice him and his dependents, keep that, and give what is beyond that as ṣadaqah. If it is easier, he may give as ṣadaqah an amount similar to what he retains.’”998

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. He is only obliged to give ṣadaqah from his property with the forms of wealth upon which zakāh is due: gold, silver, goods held for trade, camels, oxen and pasturing sheep and goats. As for household goods, slaves, dwellings and other things that are not held for trade, he does not have to give them as ṣadaqah unless he had intended it in his oath.”
