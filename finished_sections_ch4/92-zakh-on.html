92. ZAKĀH ON SHEEP AND GOATS

319. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘There is no zakāh on less than forty sheep and goats, and when there are forty, one ewe is due, up until there are one hundred and twenty. When they increase by one up until there are two hundred, two ewes are due. When they increase by one over the two hundred up until there are three hundred, three ewes are due.987 When then are many sheep, a ewe is due on every hundred.’”988

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

320. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAṭā’ ibn as-Sā’ib narrated to us from al-Ḥasan that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb sent Saʿd – or Saʿīd – ibn Mālik989 as a zakāh collector, but he came to ʿUmar seeking his permission to go on jihad, so he said to him, “Are you not on jihad?990” He said, “From where? People claim that I wrong them.” He said, “For what reason?” He said, “They say, ‘You reckon the lambs and kids among the total.’” He said, “Reckon it so, even if the shepherd comes with it on his shoulder. Do you not leave for them those pregnant ones on the point of giving birth (mākhiḍ),991 those which are suckling (rubbā),992 the athīlah, and he-goats?”’”993 994

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The mākhiḍ is the one in whose womb is a lamb or kid, the rubbā is the one raising and suckling its young, and the athīlah is the one being fattened for eating. The zakāh collector ought only to take middling kinds of sheep and goats, leaving both good quality and poor quality creatures, and taking clearly middling creatures and upwards.”
