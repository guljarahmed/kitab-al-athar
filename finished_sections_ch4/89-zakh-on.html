89. ZAKĀH ON CROPS AND THE TENTH

312. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘On everything957 which the earth produces of that which the sky waters or is watered by naturally-occurring running water there is a tenth [due in zakāh]. On everything watered by using large leather buckets or irrigated using waterwheels958 there is a half of a tenth.’”959

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to this, but our verdict is that there is no zakāh on soft tender greens960 which include annual herbs, vegetables and juicy fruits961 which have no enduring fruit,962 such as water melon, and cucumbers. On whatever there is of wheat, barley, dried dates, raisins and their like, there is no zakāh until they amount to five wasqs963 and a wasq is sixty ṣāʿ, and the ṣāʿ is the qafīz of al-Ḥajjāj [ibn Yūsuf] and a quarter of the Hāshimī, and it is eight raṭl.”964

313. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning His words, exalted is He, ‘and pay their due on the day of their harvest’ (Sūrat al-Anʿām: 142), ‘They are abrogated.’”965

314. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Abū Ṣakhrah al-Muḥāribī966 that Ziyād ibn Ḥudayr said that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb sent him to ʿAyn at-Tamr967 to collect the zakāh and told him to take a quarter of a tenth from the wealth of those who prayed [the Muslims], and a half of a tenth from the wealth of the People [of the Book] who had made the dhimmah treaty when they came and went there for trade, and a tenth from the wealth of the people who had come from the Abode of War [outside the rule of Islam].”968

315. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us from Anas ibn Sīrīn that Anas ibn Mālik said that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb had sent Anas ibn Mālik as a zakāh collector to the people of Basra, and he [Anas ibn Sīrīn] said, “And he [Anas ibn Mālik] wanted me to act as his zakāh agent, but I said, ‘No, not until you write down for me the contract that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb wrote for you.’ So he wrote for me that I should take a quarter of a tenth from the wealth of the Muslims, and a half of a tenth from the wealth of the People [of the Book] who had made the dhimmah treaty when they came and went there for trade, and a tenth from the wealth of the people who had come from the Abode of War [outside the rule of Islam].”’”969

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. As for what is taken from the Muslims, that is zakāh and it is put to the use of zakāh for the needy and the bereft and those Allah has named in His Book. What is taken from the people who have made the dhimmah treaty and from the people at war [with Muslims] is put along with the kharāj970 in the bayt al-māl for combat.”971
