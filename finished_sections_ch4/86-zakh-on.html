86. ZAKĀH ON JEWELLERY

301. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd that a woman931 said to him, “I have jewellery, so do I have to pay zakāh on it?” He said to her, “Yes.” She said, “I have my brother’s two orphaned sons in my care. Would it be acceptable932 for to me to give it to them?” He said, “Yes.”’”933

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and there is no harm in giving the zakāh to any close relative except for a child,934 parent, grandchild, grandfather and grandmother even if they are among one’s dependents. And a wife may not be given zakāh.935 Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘The husband is not to be given either.’ But as for us, we see no harm936 in the husband being given zakāh, nor do we think that there is any zakāh to be paid on jewellery except on gold and silver.937 As for precious stones and pearls, there is no zakāh on them except if they are for the purpose of trade.”

302. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no zakāh on precious stones and pearls if they are not being kept for the purpose of trade.’”938

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
