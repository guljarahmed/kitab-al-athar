91. THE ZAKĀH OF CAMELS

317. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘From five979 up to nine camels a ewe is due. When they increase by one up until there are fourteen camels, two ewes are due. If they increase by one, up until there are nineteen camels, three ewes are due. If they increase by one, up until there are twenty-four camels, four ewes are due. If they increase by one up until there are thirty-five then a young female camel in its second year980 is due. If they increase by one, up until there are forty-five, a young female camel in its third year981 is due. If they increase by one, up until there are sixty, a female camel in its fourth year982 is due. If they increase by one, up until they are seventy-five, then a female camel in its fifth year983 is due. If they increase by one, up until they are ninety, then two young female camels in their third years are due. If they increase by one, up until they are one hundred and twenty, then two female camels in their fifth year are due. Then the cycle restarts,984 so that when there are many camels, out of every fifty, a female camel in its fifth year is due.’”985

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

318. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘From one hundred and twenty-five camels, two female camels in their fifth year and a ewe are due. From one hundred and thirty camels, two female camels in their fifth year and two ewes are due. From one hundred and thirty-five camels, two female camels in their fifth year and three ewes are due. From one hundred and forty camels, two female camels in their fifth year and four ewes are due. From one hundred and forty-five camels, two female camels in their fifth year and a female camel in its second year are due. On one hundred and fifty, three female camels in their fourth year are due.’”986

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. Then also the cycle restarts, so that when they reach another fifty a camel in its fourth year is due, and then the cycle restarts. All of this is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. On less than five there is no zakāh, but when it amounts to five camels and a year passes [then zakāh is due]. ↩
  2. ibnata makhāḍ: literally daughter of one pregnant, thus named after its mother because its mother became pregnant with another, and so this camel is one which has entered into its second year. ↩
  3. ibnata labūn: literally daughter of a milk camel, thus named after its mother because its mother is suckling another, and so this camel is one which has entered into its third year. ↩
  4. ḥiqqah: literally fit to be laden, thus named because it is fit to be mounted and to be laden, and it is the one which has entered into its fourth year. ↩
  5. jadhʿah, thus named because its milk teeth have come out, and it is the one which has entered in its fifth year. ↩
