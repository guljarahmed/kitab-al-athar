93. ZAKĀH ON OXEN

321. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is nothing on less than thirty oxen, but if they are thirty oxen up until they are forty, a male or female that has just begun its second year (tābiʿ)995 is due. If they are forty, a three-year old female (musinnah) is due. Then as much as they increase in number it is reckoned according to that.’”996

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to all of this, but as for our verdict there is nothing due in any increase over997 forty until they amount to sixty oxen. When there are sixty then two male or female calves that have just entered their second year (tābiʿ) are due. The tābiʿ is the yearling, and the musinnah is one in the third year and upwards.”

Footnotes

  1. The tābiʿ “follower”, is the one which has entered into its second year and is so named because it follows (tabaʿa) its mother around. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.86. ↩
  3. Because of his words to Muʿādh, “Do not take anything from the fractional amounts (awqāṣ) of oxen,” which they explained as referring to what is between forty up to sixty, but some have said that what are meant here are the small [or young] ones. ↩
