90. HOW ZAKĀH IS GIVEN

316. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Jubayr narrated to us from Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī that a man wished to give972 his zakāh as four hundred dirhams and he went to Ibrāhīm to show him. He had given the members of a household973 ten dirhams [each], and so Ibrāhīm said, ‘If it was me, I would prefer to enrich974 the members of a Muslim household by it.’”975

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. He is given up to two hundred of the zakāh976 but it should not amount to two hundred977 unless the person is in debt,978 in which case he should be given the amount of his debt and the remains of two hundred dirhams except for a little, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. A man wanted to give Ibrāhīm the zakāh on his wealth in the form of four hundred dirhams, but he refused to accept it, and so Ibrāhīm went with him to show him, as is in the Āthār of Abū Yūsuf. ↩
  2. He had given to each member of the household ten dirhams. ↩
  3. To enrich them so as to have no need to beg, because to enrich someone so much that they come to possess the amount which would render them liable for zakāh is disapproved. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.91. ↩
  5. In al-Hidāyah there is, “It is disapproved to pay two hundred dirhams or more to a single person, but it is valid if one does it.” ↩
  6. Two hundred dirhams is the amount on which zakāh becomes due in itself, so the amount given should not reach that. ↩
  7. i.e., unless the indebted person’s debt is more than or equal to his entire wealth. ↩
