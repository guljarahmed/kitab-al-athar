IMĀN

111. ĪMĀN

373. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī Ḥabībah narrated to us saying, “I heard Abū’d-Dardā’ the Companion of the Messenger of Allah saying, ‘While I was riding behind the Messenger of Allah he said, “Abū’d-Dardā’, whoever witnesses that there is no god but Allah and that I am the Messenger of Allah must necessarily have the Garden.”’1109 He said, ‘I said to him, “Even if he commits adultery and steals?”1110 He was silent1111 towards me, and he travelled for a time and then said, “Whoever witnesses that there is no god but Allah and that I am the Messenger of Allah1112 must necessarily1113 have the Garden.” I said, “Even if he commits adultery and steals?” He said, “Even if he commits adultery and steals and even if it puts the nose of Abū’d-Dardā’ out of joint.”’”1114 He [ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī Ḥabībah] said, “It is as if I am looking at the index finger of Abū’d-Dardā’ with which he indicated the tip of his nose.”’”1115

374. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd al-Karīm ibn Abī’l-Mukhāriq narrated to us that Ṭāwus said, “A man came to Ibn ʿUmar and asked, ‘Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān,1116 what is your view of these people who steal our padlocks and open our doors, are they kuffār?’ He said, ‘No.’1117 He asked, ‘What is your view of these people who make interpretations of the Qur’ān1118 and bear witness that we are kuffār, and consider it legal to spill our blood, are they kuffār?’ He said, ‘No1119 (so how [can they be kuffār] when he said no) not until they couple a partner with Allah.’” Ṭāwus said, “It is as if I am looking at Ibn ʿUmar’s finger while he was moving it.”’”

375. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAlqamah ibn Marthad narrated to us from Ibn Buraydah al-Aslamī that his father1120 said, “We were seated with the Messenger of Allah and he said, ‘Let us go and visit1121 this Jewish neighbour of ours.’” He said, “So we went to him1122 and said, ‘How are you? and how...?’ He questioned him1123 and then said, ‘So-and-so, witness1124 that there is no god but Allah and that I am the Messenger of Allah.’ The man looked at his father1125 who would not say anything to him1126 and so he was silent. Then he said, ‘So-and-so, witness that there is no god but Allah and that I am the Messenger of Allah.’ His father said to him, ‘Bear witness to it,’1127 so he said, ‘I witness that there is no god but Allah and that you are the Messenger of Allah.’ The Messenger of Allah said,1128 ‘Praise belongs to Allah Who by means of me has freed a soul1129 from the Fire.’”’”1130

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We see no harm in visiting the sick of the Jews, Christians and Zoroastrians.”

376. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Qays ibn Muslim al-Jadalī narrated to us that Ṭāriq ibn Shihāb al-Aḥmasī1131 said, “A Jew came to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and said, ‘What do you think of His statement, “Race each other to forgiveness from your Lord and a Garden as wide as the heavens and the earth,” so where is the Fire?’ ʿUmar said to the Companions of Muḥammad , ‘Answer him,’ but none of them had anything about it for him,1132 so ʿUmar said, ‘What do you think of the day: when it comes does it not fill the heavens and the earth?’ He said, ‘Of course.’ ‘So where is the night?’ He said, ‘It is where Allah wills.’ ʿUmar said, ‘And the Fire is where Allah wills.’ The Jew said, ‘By the One in Whose hand is your self, Amīr al-Mu’minin, it is in the revealed Book1133 of Allah just as you say.’”’”

377. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘While I was with ʿAṭā’ ibn Abī Rabāḥ, ʿAlqamah ibn Marthad al-Ḥaḍramī asked him saying, “In our land there are right acting people who say, ‘We testify that we are mu’minūn1134 and we testify that we are people of the Garden.’”1135 He said, “So say, ‘You are mu’minūn, but do not say that “We are the people of the Garden,” for by Allah! there is no angel who is brought close nor prophet sent as a messenger nor right-acting slave but that Allah has a way against him and an argument against him. As for the angel who obeyed Allah well, Allah bestowed that obedience on him and he falls short in showing gratitude for it. As for a prophet sent as a messenger or right-acting slave, they might have wrong actions,1136 and so Allah has a way against them and an argument against them.’”’”

378. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAṭā’ ibn Abī Rabāḥ narrated to us that ʿAbdullāh ibn Rawāḥah specified that a sheep among his flocks was for the Messenger of Allah and he charged a slave girl of his who was with the flocks1137 with responsibility for it. And he returned repeatedly to it and watched it whenever he went to the flocks until it fattened and was hale and hearty. Then he came one day and could not find it among his flocks, and so he asked her about it, and she said, “It is lost,”1138 and he slapped her face. When that had departed from him,1139 he went to the Prophet and told him the story and said, “I could not restrain myself from slapping her.” The Prophet regarded it as a weighty matter, and said, “She might be a mu’minah.” He said, “Messenger of Allah, she is black.”1140 He said, “Bring her here.” When he brought her the Prophet asked her, “Are you a mu’minah?” and she said, “Yes.” He asked, “So where is Allah?1141” and she said, “In the heaven.”1142 He asked, “Who am I?” She said, “You are the Messenger of Allah.” So the Messenger of Allah said, “She is a mu’minah.”’1143 He said, ‘So ʿAbdullāh ibn Rawāḥah said, “Then she is free, Messenger of Allah.”’”
