113. AFFIRMATION OF THE DECREE

385. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū’z-Zubayr narrated to us from Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Anṣārī from the Prophet . [Jābir] said, “Surāqah ibn Mālik ibn Juʿshum al-Mudlijī asked him, ‘Messenger of Allah, tell us about this ʿUmrah of ours, is it for this year of ours or is it forever?’ And he said, ‘It is forever.’ He said, ‘Tell us about this dīn of ours,1172 which is as if we were created for it; for what purpose is action?1173 Is it for something which the pens have already written1174 and for which the decrees are already fixed or is it for something we have begun to act towards?’ He answered, ‘It is for something which the pens have already written and for which the decrees are already fixed.’ He asked, ‘Then for what purpose is our action, Messenger of Allah?’ He said, ‘Act,1175 for each one who acts will find his way made easy.1176 Whoever is one of the people of the Garden will find the actions of the people of the Garden1177 made easy. Whoever is one of the people of the Fire will find the actions of the people of the Fire made easy.’ Then he recited this āyah, ‘As for him who gives out and has taqwā and confirms the Good, We will ease his way to Ease. But as for him who is stingy and self-satisfied, and denies the Good, We will ease his way to Difficulty’ (Sūrat al-Layl: 5-10).”’”1178

386. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Rufayʿ from Muṣʿab ibn Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ from his father from the Prophet who said, ‘There is no self1179 but that Allah has written its entrance1180 and its exit1181 and what it will meet.’1182 A man of the Anṣār said,1183 ‘Then for what purpose is action, Messenger of Allah?’ He said, ‘Everyone who is of the people of the Garden will find his way eased to the actions of the people of the Garden. Whoever is one of the people of the Fire will find his way eased to the actions of the people of the people of the Fire.’ The Anṣārī man said, ‘Now action is genuine.’”1184

387. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAlqamah ibn Marthad al-Ḥaḍramī narrated to us from Yaḥyā ibn Yaʿmur who said, “While we were in the mosque of the Messenger of Allah I saw Ibn ʿUmar sitting to one side. So I said to my companion, ‘Would you like to go to Ibn ʿUmar and ask him about the Decree1185?’ He said, ‘Yes,’ so I said, ‘Allow me to be the one who asks him, for I will be more courteous1186 towards him than you.’ We went and sat down with him, and I said to him, ‘Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān, we1187 are some people who travel here and there in these lands and sometimes we come to a place in which there are people who say, “There is no decree.”’1188 He said, ‘Convey to them that I am quit of them, and that if I could find supporters I would wage jihad on them.’1189 Then he set to narrating to us1190 and he said, ‘While we were with the Messenger of Allah among some of his Companions, a handsome youth, with good hair reaching to his shoulders, and with a fragrance came, wearing white clothing, and he said, “Peace be upon you,1191 Messenger of Allah, and peace be upon all of you.”1192 The Prophet returned his greeting as did we. Then he said, “I will approach, Messenger of Allah.” He said, “Approach!” and he approached a step or two and then he stood out of respect for him. Then he said, “I will approach, Messenger of Allah.” He said, “Approach!” and he approached a step or two and then he stood out of respect for him. Then he said, “I will approach, Messenger of Allah.” He said, “Approach!” and he approached a step or two and then he stood out of respect for him. Then he said, “I will approach, Messenger of Allah.” He said, “Approach!” until he sat down with his knees touching the knees1193 of the Messenger of Allah . Then he said, “Tell me about īmān; what is it1194?” He said, “It is īmān1195 in Allah and His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day and that the Decree, both the good of it and the evil of it1196 is from Allah.”1197 He said, “You tell the truth,” and we were astonished at his saying, “You tell the truth,” as if he already knew1198. He said, “So tell me about the laws of Islam;1199 what are they?” He said, “Establishment of the prayer,1200 payment of zakāh, Hajj of the House, fasting the month of Ramaḍān, and performing ghusl after sexual relations (janābah).” He said, “You tell the truth,” and we were astonished at his saying, “You tell the truth,” as if he already knew. He said, “Tell me about excellence (iḥsān1201); what is it?” He said, “That you act1202 for the sake of Allah as if you see Him, for if you do not see Him, He sees you.” He said, “You tell the truth,” and we were astonished at his saying, “You tell the truth,” as if he already knew. He said, “Tell me about the coming of the Hour;1203 when will that be?” He said, “The one questioned about it1204 knows no more than the questioner.” He said, “You tell the truth,” and we were astonished at his saying, “You tell the truth,” as if he already knew. Then he departed while we were watching him. Then the Prophet said, “Bring me the man,”1205 and we went off in his footsteps, but we didn’t know where he had taken himself to, and we saw nothing of him. So we mentioned that to the Prophet and he said, “This was Jibrīl who came to you to teach you the waymarks of your dīn.1206 He has never come to me in a form1207 without me recognising him in it, before this form.”’”’”1208

388. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAbd al-Aʿlā at-Taymī from his father that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said while delivering the khuṭbah to people at al-Jābiyah1209 when he said in his khuṭbah, ‘Allah misleads whomever He wills and guides whomever He wills.’ One of those priests1210 said, ‘What does the Amīr al-Mu’minīn say?’ They said, ‘He says, “Allah misleads whomever He wills and guides whomever He wills.”’ He said, ‘That is upside-down! Allah is too just to mislead anyone!’ That saying of his reached ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and he said, ‘On the contrary, Allah has misled you. By Allah! if it were not for your covenant1211 I would have struck off your head.’”1212

389. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Yazīd ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān narrated to us from Abū Wāthilah or Ibn Wāthilah (Muḥammad was in doubt) that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘The drop [of sperm] is in the womb for forty days, and then it is a clinging blood clot1213 for forty days, then it is a morsel of flesh1214 for forty days, and then its outward form is originated, so he [the angel] says, “Is it male or female? Miserable or fortunate? What is its provision?”’”1215

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The one who is [ultimately] miserable is the one who is miserable in his mother’s womb, and the fortunate person is the one who draws admonition from others’ [experiences].”
