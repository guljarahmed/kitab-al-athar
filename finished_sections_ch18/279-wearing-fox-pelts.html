279. WEARING FOX-PELTS AND TANNING HIDES

854. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that he had seen Ibrāhīm wearing a cap of a fox-pelt and that he had seen no harm in leopard skins.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”2141

855. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that ʿUmar said, ‘The sacrifice (dhakāh)2142 of every hide2143 is by tanning it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

856. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Everything which prevents a hide from decomposition is [considered] tanning.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.231. ↩
  2. dhakāh is slaughter, but with z [i.e., zakāh] it means purification. [Varying texts give both readings, but it seems that the former is the more sound as it is identical to Abū Yūsuf’s transmission of the same hadith from Abū Ḥanīfah. – Trans.] ↩
  3. mask is a hide. ↩
