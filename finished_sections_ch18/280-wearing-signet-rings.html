280. WEARING SIGNET-RINGS OF GOLD, IRON AND OTHER MATERIALS, AND HAVING ENGRAVINGS ON A SIGNET-RING

857. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād who said, ‘The engraving on Ibrāhīm’s signet-ring was, “Allah is the Guardian Friend of Ibrāhīm.” He said, “The signet-ring which Ibrāhīm2144 had was of iron.”’”2145

Muḥammad said, “Wearing signet rings of gold and iron, or any other type of jewellery, does not please us, except for silver for men. As for women, there is no harm in them wearing gold, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

858. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibrāhīm ibn Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir narrated to us from his father that the engraving on Masrūq’s signet ring was “In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful.”’ He said, ‘The engraving on Ḥammād’s signet ring was “There is no god but Allah.”’”2146

Muḥammad said, “We see no harm in engraving signet rings with dhikr of Allah as long as it is not a complete āyah, because such2147 ought not to be on one’s hand when one is in need of ghusl because of sexual intercourse or ejaculation, or in need of wuḍū’. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
