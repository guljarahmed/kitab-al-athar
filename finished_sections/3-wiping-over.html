3. WIPING OVER LEATHER SOCKS39

8. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Bakr ibn ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī Jahm narrated to us that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar said, “I went to Iraq on the military expedition of Jalūlā’,40 and I saw Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ wiping over leather socks, so I said, ‘What is this,41 Saʿd?’ He answered, ‘When you meet42 Amīr al-Mu’minīn ʿUmar , ask him.’” He said, “So I met ʿUmar43 and informed him of what Saʿd had done. ʿUmar said, ‘Saʿd spoke the truth. We saw the Messenger of Allah doing it, so we did it.’”’”44

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it.”

9. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm from Ḥanẓalah ibn Nabātah45 al-Juʿfī that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, “Wiping over leather socks is permitted to the resident for a day and a night, and to the traveller for three days46 and their nights, provided that you put them on47 while you are in a state of purity.”’”48

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it.”

10. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Sālim ibn ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar said, “ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar and Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ differed49 about wiping50 over leather socks, and Saʿd said, ‘I wipe51 [over leather socks],’ and ʿAbdullāh said, ‘It doesn’t please me.’ So they came to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and told him the story, and ʿUmar said, ‘Your paternal uncle has greater understanding (fiqh) than you.’”’”52

11. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from ash-Shaʿbī from Ibrāhīm ibn Abī Mūsā al-Ashʿarī from al-Mughīrah ibn Abī53 Shuʿbah, that he went out with the Messenger of Allah on a journey,54 and the Messenger of Allah went off to relieve himself. Then he came back, and he was wearing a long Byzantine55 outer garment with tight sleeves,56 which the Messenger of Allah rolled up57 because of the tightness of its sleeves. Al-Mughīrah said, ‘So I began to pour water for him from a clay vessel58 which I had. He made his wuḍū’ for the prayer and wiped over his leather socks without taking them off, and then he stepped forward and prayed.’”59

12. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from someone who saw60 Jarīr ibn ʿAbdullāh doing wuḍū’ one day and wiping over his leather socks, and so someone asked him about that and he said, ‘I saw the Messenger of Allah do it, and I only accompanied him after61 Sūrat al-Mā’idah was revealed.’”62

13. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Muḥammad ibn ʿAmr ibn al-Ḥārith that ʿAmr ibn al-Ḥārith ibn Abī Ḍirār accompanied Ibn Masʿūd on a journey,63 and three days and nights would pass without him taking his leather socks off.”64 65

14. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm used to wipe over his leather galoshes (jurmūq).”66 67

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it.”

15. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you have been wiping [over leather socks] and you are in the state of wuḍū’, but then you take off your leather socks, then wash your feet.’”68 69

Muḥammad said, “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it.”

It is narrated of al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī that he said, “I saw seventy of the people of Badr from among the Companions all of whom held the view that one wipes over leather socks.” It is for this reason that Abū Ḥanīfah held the view that it is one of the preconditions of [being one of] the people of the Sunnah and the Community (Ahl as-sunnah wa’l-Jamāʿah) and he said, “We give preference to the two Shaykhs (Abū Bakr and ʿUmar, preferring them over all the other Companions) and we love the two who were related through their daughters [to the Prophet , and they were Abū Bakr and ʿUmar], and we hold the view that one wipes over leather socks.” ↩
