22. THE TIMES OF THE PRAYER

65. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that a man came to the Prophet to ask him248 about the time of the prayer, and he told him to attend the prayers249 with the Messenger of Allah . Then he told Bilāl to be early250 with the prayers [at the beginnings of their times]. On the second day, he told him to delay all of the prayers [to the later parts of their times]. Then he said, ‘Where is the person who asked about the time of the prayer? What is in between these two is the time.’”251

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Maghrib and the other prayers are the same in this respect in our view, except that we disapprove252 of delaying it when the sun has set, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

66. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, ‘Delay Ẓuhr until the coolness253 following the intense heat of Jahannam.’”254 255

Muḥammad said, “Ẓuhr is delayed in the summer until it is cooler, but in winter it is prayed when the sun declines from the meridian [at the beginning of its time], and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

67. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Ibn Masʿūd looked at the sun when it set and said, “This is its decline [dulūk].”256 257
