17. THE GHUSL OF A WOMAN WITH CHRONIC MENSTRUAL BLEEDING199 AND OF THE WOMAN WHO IS MENSTRUATING

49. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a woman with chronic menstrual bleeding (mustaḥāḍah), ‘She leaves200 Ẓuhr until the end of its time, takes a ghusl and prays Ẓuhr, and then prays ʿAṣr.201 Then she waits until when the time of Maghrib comes but does not pray until the end of its time, takes a ghusl and prays Maghrib and ʿIshā’, [doing that] until she is over [her chronic menstrual bleeding].’”202

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but we adhere to the other hadith203 that she makes wuḍū’ for the time of every prayer and she may pray up until the next [prayer] time.204 In our view, she has only205 one ghusl to make when the [customary number of] days of her period have passed, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

50. Muḥammad said, “Ayyūb ibn ʿUtbah the Qāḍī of al-Yamāmah informed us from Yaḥyā ibn Abī Kathīr from Abū Salamah ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf that Umm Ḥabībah bint Abī Sufyān206 asked the Messenger of Allah about the situation of a woman with chronic menstrual bleeding, and he said, ‘She should take a ghusl207 when the [customary number of] days of her period have passed and then do wuḍū’ for every prayer and pray.’”208 209

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this hadith.”
