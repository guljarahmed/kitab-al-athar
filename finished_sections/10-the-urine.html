10. THE URINE OF ANIMALS139 AND OTHERS

34. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us, ‘A man140 from Basra narrated to us that al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī141 said, “There is no harm142 in the urine of any ruminant.”’”143 144

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to disapprove of it,145 and he used to say, ‘If it falls in water used for wuḍū’, it invalidates wuḍū’ [done with that water], and if a great deal146 of it falls on clothing in which someone then prays, he must repeat147 the prayer.’”

Muḥammad said, “I see no harm in it,148 and it does not render water, wuḍū’ water or clothing impure.”

35. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah narrated to us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man upon whose clothing a [male] infant’s urine149 falls, ‘If he hasn’t eaten [solid food] and drunk [something other than his mother’s milk], then it suffices if you pour150 some water.’”151

Muḥammad said, “The more pleasing of that is that one washes it,152 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

36. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who urinates standing153 while he has dirhams [in his hand] on which is writing, meaning some Qur’ān, and he disapproved of it and said, “It would be better if they were in a pouch or a pouch whose neck is tied.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we disapprove of him grasping them in his hand when there is some Qur’ān on them, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

37. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm154 said concerning a man who urinates standing, ‘The Prophet went to the rubbish tip of some people with his Companions and he spread his legs wide apart and then urinated standing.155 One of his Companions said, “So much so that we thought that his spreading his legs wide apart was for fear of the urine.”’”156
