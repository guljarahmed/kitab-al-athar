11. CLEANSING THE PRIVATE PARTS AFTER GOING TO THE TOILET

38. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that those who associate partners with Allah (mushrikūn) at the time of the Messenger of Allah met the Muslims and said, “We see that your companion157 teaches you how you should go to the toilet,” making fun of them. So the Muslims said, “Yes.”158 They [the mushrikūn] asked them [about that] and they [the Companions] said, “He told us159 not to face the qiblah with our private parts, not to cleanse our private parts with our right hands,160 not to cleanse our private parts with bones or dung, and that we should cleanse ourselves with three161 stones.”’”162

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but washing with water in the act of cleansing the private parts is preferable163 to us, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

Footnotes

  1. Meaning, the Messenger of Allah . ↩
  2. They answered wisely and didn’t get involved in their mockery. ↩
  3. Our people argued by means of the hadith that it is not permissible to face the qiblah or turn one’s back on it for urination or defecation whether one is in the desert [out in the country] or within buildings. ↩
  4. The ʿulamā’ are agreed that it is forbidden to clean the private parts with the right hand. ↩
  5. Three is mentioned as a customary and average number, because, in the main, the point is to attain cleanliness with them, or it may be that [three] is mentioned by way of recommendation (mustaḥabb). ↩
  6. Muslim narrated it in the book of purification in the chapter on “istiṭābah” (istinjā’, i.e., cleaning the anus and penis with a piece of clay or a dry stone) in a connected manner from Sufyān from al-Aʿmash and Manṣūr from Ibrāhīm from ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn Yazīd from Salmān. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.8. ↩
  7. Preferable to us than only using stones. Nevertheless sufficing oneself with using stones is also a sunnah, but washing afterwards with water is better. It can be interpreted that he meant that washing oneself with water initially is better than purifying oneself with stones. ↩
