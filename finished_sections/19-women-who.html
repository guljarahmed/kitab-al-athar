19. WOMEN WHO HAVE GIVEN BIRTH AND PREGNANT WOMEN WHO EXPERIENCE BLEEDING

54. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “If the woman who has just given birth hasn’t got a definite time215 [when her bleeding ordinarily stops], then let her wait for the number of days [which are usual] for women216 [of her family or of her time].”’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this,217 but she is counted as being in this condition for up to218 forty days. If [her bleeding] goes beyond that, then she takes a ghusl and performs wuḍū’ for every prayer and she prays, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

55. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a pregnant woman sees some blood, she is not menstruating,219 so let her pray220 and fast, and let her husband make love to her. She may do all of the things that women who are not in their menstrual periods do.’”221

[Muḥammad said], “That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

56. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The pregnant woman always prays222 until she has given birth even if she sees blood, because pregnancy is not menstruation.223 If she makes a bequest, and then has a painful delivery224 and dies, then the bequest comes out of the third [of her property which she may leave as a bequest to people or causes other than her heirs].’”225 226

Muḥammad said, “”We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”
