16. A MAN AND WOMAN DOING GHUSL BECAUSE OF INTERCOURSE FROM A SINGLE VESSEL

48. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from ʿĀ’ishah, the Mother of the Mu’minūn, that the Messenger of Allah would take a ghusl194 with one of his wives195 from a single vessel both of them contending for the water at the same time.’”196 197

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we see no harm198 in the wife taking her ghusl with the husband, whether she begins before him or he begins before her, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

Footnotes

  1. ghusl or ghisl and this latter is the water with which one washes. ↩
  2. It was ʿĀ’ishah , as is in a narration from her, “I used to make ghusl, I and the Prophet ...” ↩
  3. “At the same time” either qualifies “take a ghusl”or “contending for the water.” ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.14, and al-Bukhārī under “ghusl” in the chapter on “whether the person in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations should enter his hand in the vessel before washing them.” Muslim narrated it under “menstruation” in the chapter on “the amount of water that is recommended in washing because of janābah,” and Abū Dāwūd in “purification,” the chapter on “wuḍū’ with water left over by a woman,” and an-Nasā’ī in “purification”, the chapter “in mention of a man and woman taking a ghusl from a single vessel,” and Ibn Mājah under “purification” in the chapter on “a man and a woman taking a ghusl from a single vessel.” ↩
  5. As for the purification of the man and woman from one vessel, it is permissible according to the consensus of the Muslims. As for the purification of the woman with water left over by the man it is also permissible, and vice-versa it is permissible according to the three Imams contrary to Aḥmad. ↩
