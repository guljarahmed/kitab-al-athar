23. GHUSL ON THE DAY OF JUMUʿAH AND ON THE TWO ʿĪDS

68. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the ghusl on the day of Jumuʿah, ‘If you take a ghusl that is good,258 but if you leave it that is good.’”259

69. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I saw Ibrāhīm go out to the two ʿĪds without doing ghusl.’”

Muḥammad said, “If you take a ghusl on the Jumuʿah and the two ʿĪds it is better, but if you leave it there is no harm.”

70. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘We often used to come to the two ʿĪds without having taken a ghusl,’ and he said, ‘If you take a ghusl, it is good.’”

71. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abān narrated to us from Abū Naḍrah from Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Anṣārī from the Prophet that he said, “Whoever takes a ghusl on the day of Jumuʿah has done well, but whoever does not take a ghusl and [suffices himself with wuḍū’] it is fine.”’”260 261

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

ḥadath is the exit of anything from the urinal tract or the anus, including wind. – Trans.

Footnotes

  1. The dominant majority of the ʿulamā’ took the position that it is a recommended sunnah and is not obligatory. That it is obligatory is cited of several of the right-acting first generations. The dominant majority responded concerning the hadith narrated commanding it that it is interpreted as being a recommendation in order to reconcile the hadith. ↩
  2. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.1, p.436. ↩
  3. fabihā wa niʿmat is an expression having a large number of explanations of which Ḥāfiẓ Riyāḍ Aḥmad al-Multānī chooses “that he suffices himself with it, i.e., this action of wuḍū’, and what an excellent feature that is or what an excellent concession.” – Trans. ↩
  4. The hadith is well-known among the people of knowledge, and a large number of the Companions narrated it. It has been narrated from Jābir by different routes. The route from Jābir through Abū’ Naḍrah was narrated by ʿAbd ibn Ḥamīd on p.326. ↩
