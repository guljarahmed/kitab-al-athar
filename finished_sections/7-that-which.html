7. THAT WHICH NOTHING POLLUTES: WATER, THE EARTH, THE PERSON IN NEED OF GHUSL BECAUSE OF SEXUAL RELATIONS,109 ETC.

25. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us, ‘Al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham narrated to us that Ibn ʿAbbās said, “There are four things which nothing110 pollutes: the body, clothing, water and the earth.”’”111

Muḥammad said, “The explanation of that according to us is that when something dirty strikes something112 and one washes it, it113 departs from it,114 and so it doesn’t carry115 the filth.116 With respect to water it only means that when there is a great deal of it or it is flowing, then it doesn’t carry impurity.”117

26. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm118 that the Messenger of Allah used to put his head out of the mosque while he was in iʿtikāf seclusion, and that ʿĀ’ishah would wash it while she was in her menstrual period.”119

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and we see no harm in it, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

27. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm120 that while the Messenger of Allah was walking,121 Ḥudhayfah ibn al-Yamān appeared before him. The Prophet made to shake his hand, but Ḥudhayfah held his hand back. The Prophet said, ‘What is wrong with you?’122 He answered, ‘Messenger of Allah, I am in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations.’ He said, ‘The person who has īmān123 is not impure.’”124

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the hadith of the Messenger of Allah . We see no harm125 in shaking hands with someone in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

28. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning someone who is so sick that he [or she] is unable126 to perform ghusl after sexual relations or for [the end of the] menstrual period, ‘They should do tayammum.’”127

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

29. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that the sick person who is resident128 and living with his family, who is not able [to take a ghusl or do wuḍū’] because of smallpox or a wound and for whom water is to be guarded against, has the status of the traveller who cannot find water, for whom tayammum suffices.’”129

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, to which we adhere.”

30. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man130 taking a ghusl because of sexual relations, ‘He should wipe over the splints.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to that. If he fears the effects of wiping over the splints he will also give that up131 and it suffices him, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

  1. janābah, translated throughout as “being in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations,” covers the condition resulting from ejaculation while asleep or awake, with or without sexual intercourse and sexual intercourse with or without ejaculation. ↩
  2. Nothing pollutes of actual or legal impurity, such as water, the earth and the person in need of ghusl because of sexual relations, because much water and flowing water cannot be rendered impure by impurities falling into them, and similarly the earth, which when it becomes polluted and then dries is then pure without any need for washing. The person in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations is not really impure in a real sense and the impurity in his case is legal, and it is similar with the woman in her menstrual period and the woman who experiences bleeding after childbirth. This is according to how Imam Muḥammad explains it, as comes after the tradition. Shaykh Mahdī Ḥasan said in his commentary, Qalā’id al-azhār, “In my view what is meant by ‘nothing’ is the state of being in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations (janābah), as the narration of ʿAbd ar-Razzāq and ad-Dāraquṭnī make clear, because in it there is, ‘There is no janābah for water, no janābah for the earth, no janābah for clothing and no janābah for the body.’ And there is from Ibn ʿAbbās , ‘There are four things that are not rendered impure: the human being, clothing, water and the earth,’ meaning that a human being does not become in a state of janābah if a person in a state of janābah touches him, nor does clothing if a person in a state of janābah wears it, nor the earth if a person in a state of janābah betakes himself to it [in tayammum], nor water if a person in a state of janābah immerses himself in it, and the judgement of being in a state of janābah does not transfer to these things. These things are only singled out for mention because of the great frequency with which they are needed.” ↩
