21. THE CALL TO PRAYER

58. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “There is no harm231 in the mu’adhdhin making the call to prayer when he is not in wuḍū’.”’”232

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we see no harm in it,233 but we disapprove of him making the call to prayer while in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations.”

59. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that he said about the mu’adhdhin talking during his call to prayer, “I do not tell him to do it234 and I do not forbid him.”’”235

Muḥammad said, “As for us, we think that he should not do it, but that if he does do it, it does not invalidate236 his call to prayer, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

60. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that he [Ḥammād] said, ‘I asked him about tathwīb237 [summoning a second time] and he said, “It is a part of that which people238 have innovated, but it is one of the good things239 they have innovated,” and he mentioned that their tathwīb240 was when the mu’adhdhin finished his call to prayer, “Prayer is better than sleep.”’”

61. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The end of Bilāl’s call to prayer was, “Allah is greater, Allah is greater. There is no god but Allah.”’”241

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

62. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The call to prayer and the iqāmah are [called repeating each phrase] two at a time.’”242

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

63. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ṭalḥah ibn Muṣarrif narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “When the mu’adhdhin says, ‘Hasten to success,’ people ought to stand243 and form rows, and then when the mu’adhdhin says, ‘Prayer is about to begin,’ the imam says the takbīr.”’”244 245

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. But if the imam waits until the mu’adhdhin finishes his iqāmah and then says takbīr, there is also no harm in this, and all of that is good.”

64. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Women do not have246 to call to prayer or [call the] iqāmah.’”247

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
