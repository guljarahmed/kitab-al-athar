4. WUḌŪ'70 BECAUSE OF FOOD THAT HAS BEEN COOKED OVER FIRE

16. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Murrah informed us from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr said, “If I, in the state of wuḍū’, was brought a large bowl71 of bread and meat and I ate till I was satisfied, and a large bowl of camel milk from which I drank until full,72 I would not care73 if I did not touch water. Should I make wuḍū’ because of wholesome good things?”’”74

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it. There is no wuḍū’ because of that which fire has changed [by cooking]. Wuḍū’ is only done because of things that exit76 [the body] not because of things that enter.”77

17. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn Zādhān narrated to us that Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī said, “The Messenger of Allah entered my house, and I brought meat which had been roasted and he tasted some of it. Then he called for water, washed his hands78 and rinsed out his mouth, and later prayed without renewing wuḍū’.”’”79

18. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Shaybah ibn Musāwir narrated to us saying, “I was seated with ʿAdī ibn Arṭāh when he asked al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī, ‘Should I perform wuḍū’81 because of that [food] which fire has touched?’ and he answered, ‘Yes.’82 Then Bakr ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Muznī said, ‘The Prophet went to see his aunt Ṣafiyyah bint ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and she picked out some cold shoulder meat for him which he ate and he did not renew wuḍū’.’”’”83

Muḥammad said, “We adhere84 to the verdict of Bakr ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Muznī, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

19. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Yaḥyā ibn ʿAbdullāh narrated to us that Abū Mājid al-Ḥanafī said about ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd , “Once while we were seated in the mosque85 with Ibn Masʿūd , they came towards us with a bowl and a jug of water from the Door of the Elephant.86 Ibn Masʿūd said, ‘I think that you are going around87 with this [food]?’ One of the men of that party said, ‘Yes, Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān,88 there was a banquet89 in the neighbourhood.’ So it was put down90 and he ate some of it and drank some of the water. Then he poured some over his hands and washed them, wiped his face and arms with his damp hands and said, ‘This is the wuḍū’91 for someone who has not nullified his wuḍū’.’”’”92

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, and we adhere to it. There is no harm93 in washing (wuḍū’) in the mosque if it is not because of something impure.”
