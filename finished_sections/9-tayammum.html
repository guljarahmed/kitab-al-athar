9. TAYAMMUM132

31. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said concerning tayammum, ‘You place133 your palms on clean soil then wipe your face, and then you place them a second time and shake them to remove the dust and wipe your hands and your forearms up to the elbows.’”134

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to that, and we think135 also that one should shake the hands to remove dust each time, before wiping the face and the forearms. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

32. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whenever a man does tayammum he remains in the condition of tayammum as long as he does not find water or do something that voids his tayammum.’”136

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

33. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “It is preferable to us137 that when one does tayammum one does it up to the elbows.”’”138

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to that; tayammum is not valid until one does it up to the elbows, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah &.”

Footnotes

  1. Lexically tayammum means “purpose and intention” and in the Sharīʿah it means to purpose going to pure earth to wipe the face and hands with the intention of becoming in a state in which it is permissible to do the prayer. It is firmly established in the Book and the Sunnah and the consensus of the Ummah. They agree unanimously that it is particularly for the face and the two hands even if the nature of the nullification of the state of purity is major. It is one of the distinguishing marks of this Ummah. ↩

If it is according to the outward apparent meaning then there is an indication in it that wiping up to the elbows is not obligatory in the view of Ibrāhīm but on the contrary recommended (mustaḥabb) as Mālik said, “Wiping up to the wrists is obligatory and up to the elbows is a matter of choice.” If it is understood in the sense of something incumbent (wājib) as it is interpreted in the books of fiqh in some places [in which wājib is less binding than farḍ (obligatory)], then there is no disagreement between us and Ibrāhīm. ↩
