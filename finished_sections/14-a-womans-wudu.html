14. A WOMAN'S WUḌŪ' AND WIPING OVER HER HEAD-COVERING

43. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Women wipe the hair on their heads,175 and it is not enough to wipe over their head-coverings.’”176

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

44. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “It is not enough for a woman to wipe her temples177 alone, she must also wipe her head as men do.”’”

Muḥammad said, “As for us,178 we say that if she wipes the site of her hair and wipes that to the extent of three179 fingers then it suffices her, but we prefer for her to wipe [the entire head] in the same way as men do, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

Footnotes

  1. Because of His words, exalted is He, “and wipe over your heads,” (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 6). It is obvious that the turban and a woman’s head-covering are not the head itself. ↩
  2. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.1, p.31. ↩
  3. The temple (ṣadgh) is that which is between the eye and the ear. ↩
  4. We extend it. ↩
