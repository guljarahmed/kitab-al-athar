177. THE COMPENSATORY PAYMENT FOR THAT OF WHICH A MAN ONLY OWNS ONE

555. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If anything of the tongue is cut off which prevents speech or if it is cut off at its root then a complete compensatory payment is due.’”1567

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

556. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Everything a person has only one of which is destroyed by mistake, there is due a complete compensatory payment: the nose, the penis, the tongue, the spine or the departure of the intellect and the like. Everything which a person has two of, for each of them there is due a half of the compensatory payment [due for fatalities]: female breasts, legs, eyes and the like.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

557. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Any of that which is done intentionally, there is retaliation for it. Anything for which one is not able to retaliate, there is [full] compensatory payment. If it was by mistake, there are due five lots1568 of camels. If it is with quasi-deliberate intent,1569 four lots of camels are due for it, and quasi-deliberate intent in injuries is everything in which someone intended to strike with a weapon or something else, and [for such cases] in which retaliation is not possible, then a severe compensatory payment is due for it.’”

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to all of this,1570 may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, and we ourselves also adhere to it except in one respect: For those things which are of quasi-deliberate intent three lots1571 of camels are due: a lot of three-year old female camels which have entered their fourth year,1572 a lot of five-year old camels,1573 a third lot of camels in between six-years old and [eight-to-nine-years old] that have passed a year after cutting their canine teeth,1574 all of [the forty of] which are pregnant. Abū Ḥanīfah used to say, ‘Four lots of camels: a lot of camels which have entered their second year,1575 a lot of camels which have entered their third year,1576 a lot of camels which have entered their fourth year and a lot of camels which have entered their fifth year.’ As for unintentional homicide our verdict and his verdict are the same: five lots of camels. There should be a lot of male camels which have entered their second year, a lot of female camels which have entered their second year, a lot of female camels which have entered their third year, a lot of female camels which have entered their fourth year, and a lot of female camels which have entered their fifth year, and that is the verdict of ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd . That which we have narrated about quasi-intentional homicide has also been narrated from the Prophet . He said during his address on the day of the Opening of Makkah [to Islam], ‘Certainly, the person who is killed unintentionally, someone killed by the lash or by a staff, then one hundred camels is due from him: thirty females which have entered their fourth year, thirty females which have entered their fifth year, and forty females which are in between six-years old and [eight-to-nine-years old] that have passed a year after cutting their canine teeth, all [forty of] which are pregnant.’”

558. “The same as that has reached us from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb which he ascribed directly [to the Prophet ], ‘Of them there are forty in whose wombs there are their offspring.’ The same as that has reached us from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb , al-Mughīrah ibn Shuʿbah, Abū Mūsā al-Ashʿarī and Zayd ibn Thābit and we adhere to it.”

559. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham from ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib concerning a man who shaves off another man’s beard to such an extent that it will not regrow, that he said, ‘He owes the full compensatory payment.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
