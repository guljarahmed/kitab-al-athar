178. COMPENSATORY PAYMENT FOR TEETH, THE EYELASHES AND FINGERS

560. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The fingers of the hands and [the toes] of the feet are the same, for each digit there is a tenth of the [full] compensatory payment.’”1577

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

561. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Shurayḥ who said, ‘The teeth are all the same:1578 for each tooth there is a twentieth of the [full] compensatory payment.’”1579

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

562. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “For [wounds] that reach to the thin skin above the skull1580 and flesh wounds1581 and the like, whether unintentional or intentional, for which retaliation is impossible, then a just and knowledgeable person must assess a suitable compensation.”’”1582

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

563. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Shurayḥ who said, ‘For wounds that penetrate deep inside1583 there is due a third of the [full] compensatory payment, and for head wounds which lay bare the brain1584 there is a third of the compensatory payment. If a person loses consciousness [and is in a coma], the entire compensatory payment is due. For head wounds that remove some of the bone of the head,1585 there is due a tenth and a twentieth of the compensatory payment, and for wounds that lay bare the bone1586 there is a twentieth of the compensatory payment, and for every other kind of wound a suitable compensation must be assessed by a just and knowledgeable person. The wound that lays bare the bone is only with respect to the face and the head, and a wound that penetrates deep inside is only with respect to the abdomen.’”1587

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. Āmmah comprise every head wound1588 which reaches to the brain. Munaqqilah are those by which bone is moved or removed. Mūḍiḥah wounds comprise those which lay bare the bone and hāshimah wounds are whatever break the bone, the ruling on both of which is that a tenth of the compensatory payment is due, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. Simḥāq wounds [which stop short at the fine layer of skin above the bone] are less severe than those which lay bare the bone; between them and wounds which lay bare the bone there is a fine thin skin. For them a suitable compensation must be assessed by a just and knowledgeable person. It has reached us that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib gave judgement concerning them that four camels were due. Bāḍiʿah flesh wounds are less severe than those wounds which stop short at the thin skin/filament over the bone and are those which cut off some of the flesh, and for that a suitable compensation must be assessed by a just person. A dāmiyah wound which causes bleeding is less severe than a flesh wound and it is the type which splits the skin, and a just and knowledgeable person must assess a suitable compensation. The mutalāḥimah1589 is the wound which blackens or reddens its site without bleeding or removing flesh, and a just and knowledgeable person must assess a suitable compensation. We think that everything of that which is less than a flesh wound is not to be paid by those who pay the compensatory payment on behalf of the man but is due from the property of the man himself, even if it was unintentional.”

564. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘For all the eyelashes1590 the entire compensatory payment is due if they do not regrow, but for each one of them singly a quarter of the full compensatory payment is due. For all the eyelids the entire compensatory payment is due, but for each eyelid a quarter of the full compensatory payment is due. For both lips the full compensatory payment is due, but for each one of them a half of the full compensatory payment is due.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

  9. munaqqilah or munaqqalah is the wound that removes bone after breaking it. ↩
  10. mūḍiḥah is the wound that exposes the bone to view. ↩

  12. shijāj is the plural of shajjah and lexically it is used particularly for wounds to the face or head. Other wounds are called jirāḥ. ↩

  14. ashfār is the plural of shufr and denotes the place where the eyelashes grow and are the borders and edges of the eyes. The hairs that grow there are called eyelashes, so what is meant by ashfār is the eyelashes since it is the hair that grows and not the skin. Since it is obligatory to pay the entire compensatory payment for all of the ashfār, then for each one there is a quarter payment. ↩
