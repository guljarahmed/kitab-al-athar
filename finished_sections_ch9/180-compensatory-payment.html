180. COMPENSATORY PAYMENT FOR AN INVOLUNTARY ACT AND THAT WHICH THE ʿĀQILAH1606 PAY

571. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the compensatory payment for unintentional and quasi-intentional homicide due from the ʿāqilah, ‘It must be paid by the people who use silver [as a currency] over three years, one third each year. Whatever unintentional wounds there are, [then the compensatory payment] is the responsibility of the ʿāqilah, the people of the register (dīwān).1607 If the wound amounts to two-thirds of the full compensatory payment, it is to be paid in two years. If it is a half [of the full compensatory payment], it is to be paid in two years. If it is one third, it is to be paid in one year. All of that is due from the people of the register.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. That is due from the stipends1608 of fighting men and not from the stipends of children and women. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

572. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The ʿāqilah do not pay1609 for anything less than wounds that lay bare the bone.’”1610

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

573. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The ʿāqilah do not pay for deliberate crimes,1611 or something agreed after a dispute1612 nor something confessed.’”1613

574. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whatever is agreed after a dispute, or confessed to, or a deliberate crime, then it is paid from the man’s property [and not by the ʿāqilah].’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

575. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If they testify that he struck him and he was healthy, but remained confined to his bed until he died, their testimony is valid and they are not charged with more than that.’ Ibrāhīm spoke concerning someone who is struck and dies and the witnesses testify1614 that he remained confined to his bed1615 until he died, and he said, ‘I would free him1616 and would accept the compensatory payment from his ʿāqilah if it was unintentional.’”1617

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

576. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The ʿāqilah pay for all unintentional acts except what is less than a wound laying bare the bone, or a tooth, for which there is no known compensatory payment.’”1618

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

577. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm from the Prophet who said, “No retaliation is taken or compensatory payment made (jubār) for a dumb beast,1619 a well, a leg, a mine;1620 and from precious minerals a fifth is due.”’”1621 1622

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. Jubār means hadr1623 (not to be retaliated for and it goes uncompensated). If a man travelled on a mount and it kicked someone with its hoof1624 while travelling and killed or injured a man, then that goes uncompensated and compensation is not due from the ʿāqilah or from anyone else. A dumb beast [in the hadith above] is an animal that has escaped without a driver or a rider and which tramples a man and kills him. That also goes unretaliated and uncompensated. As for a mine or a well [in the hadith above], it is when a man hires another man to dig a well or a mine for him and falls from it and dies, then that goes unretaliated and uncompensated, and there is nothing due from the employer1625 nor from his ʿāqilah.”

  1. The ʿāqilah are those who pay the compensatory payment – [and this term is used] because ʿaql means compensatory payment – and they are the people of the register (dīwān), if the killer is from the people of the dīwān. Whoever is not from the people of the register, then his ʿāqilah are his tribe. When ʿUmar recorded the registers (dīwān) he made the people of the register responsible for compensatory payments. The dīwān is the register in which is recorded the people who receive stipends, and the army. Ash-Shāfiʿī said that one’s ʿāqilah is one’s tribe. ↩
  2. The dīwān register was initiated by Sayyidunā ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and in it was recorded those who received stipends and the members of the army. It is they who pay the compensatory payment if the person who committed the act of violence is also one of the people of the register. Otherwise, if he is not one of the people of the register, his tribe pay the compensatory payment. – Trans. ↩

As for our people, they said that rikāz includes minerals from mines as well as buried treasure on all of which a fifth has to be paid, which is supported by the hadith of Abū Hurayrah which he ascribed to the Prophet [as a marfūʿ], “From precious minerals a fifth is due.” Someone asked, “What are precious minerals, Messenger of Allah?” He said. “What Allah created in the earth on the day it was created.” Al-Bayhaqī mentioned it. ↩
