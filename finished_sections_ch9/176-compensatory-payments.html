COMPENSATORY PAYMENTS AND RETALIATION

176. COMPENSATORY PAYMENTS (DIYĀT)1563 FOR FATALITIES AND WHAT IS DUE FROM PEOPLE WHO USE SILVER [AS CURRENCY] AND THOSE FOR WHOM CATTLE [ARE WEALTH]

554. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham from ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī from ʿUbaydah as-Salmānī from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb who said, ‘For the people who use silver their compensatory payment for a fatality is ten thousand dirhams, and for the people who use gold it is one thousand dinars. For the people [whose wealth is in] cows it is two hundred cows, and for the people [whose wealth is in] camels it is two hundred camels. For the people [whose wealth is in] sheep and goats it is a thousand ewes. For the people [whose wealth is in] garments,1564 it is two hundred sets of garments.’”1565

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this, and Abū Ḥanīfah1566 used to adhere to that of it set in camels, dirhams and dinars.”

  2. Garments held for trade. The set of garments consists of a large cloth wrapped around the lower half of the body (izār) and an over garment for the whole body (ridā). ↩
  3. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.221. ↩
  4. And he said that the measures are only valid with something whose value is known not with something unknown, and the value of these things is unknown [i.e., their value fluctuates, thus, according to him, one should not pay the compensatory payment on them. – Ed.] ↩
