179. THOSE THINGS FOR WHICH ONE IS UNABLE TO RETALIATE

565. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a blind person who puts out1591 the eye of a healthy person, ‘He is responsible for paying the compensatory payment from his own wealth.’”1592 1593

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because retaliation is not possible1594 for that and it only means in cases where it was intentional, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

566. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever strikes with an iron implement or a staff [causing an injury] for which retaliation is not possible must pay the severe compensatory payment from his own property.’”1595

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, and that is for anything less than homicide.”1596

567. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Everything less than homicide of the category considered to have been done with quasi-deliberate intent, [the compensatory payment] is due from the perpetrator’s own property, and it is in every case in which someone hits someone else intentionally in which retaliation is not possible.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

568. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There are three types of homicide: involuntary manslaughter, murder and quasi-intentional homicide. Involuntary1597 manslaughter is when someone intends something [else] but strikes his fellow with a weapon1598 or something else, in which case compensatory payment is due for that, [paid] in cloaks.1599 Murder (intentional homicide) is when someone deliberately intends his fellow and strikes him with a weapon, and for this there is retaliation1600 unless they [the deceased’s heirs] reconcile1601 or pardon.1602 Quasi-intentional homicide is every case in which someone intends to strike someone else without a weapon1603 [with a resultant death], then for this there is a severe compensatory payment [to be paid] by the paternal male relatives [ʿāqilah], due from those who pay the compensatory payment, if that results in death. Quasi-intentional injury is every case in which someone deliberately intends to hit someone else with a weapon or something else, but for which retaliation is impossible, then for that severe compensatory payment must be paid.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this except in one respect: whenever someone strikes someone else without a weapon but with something which takes the place of a weapon1604 or worse, then there is also retaliation for that, and that was Abū Ḥanīfah’s earlier verdict. However, there is no retaliation in his later verdict except for what was done with a weapon.”

569. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham from a man from Abū Bakr aṣ-Ṣiddīq concerning a man who shot another man with an arrow that pierced him,1605 and he decided that he should have to pay two-thirds of the full compensatory payment for it.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. For something which penetrates the abdomen a third of the full compensatory payment is due, but if it pierces him except for the very last part of it, then two thirds of the compensatory payment is due for it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

570. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Everything apart from taking a life, which a person has done deliberately, striking with an iron implement, a staff, a hand, a cane or anything else, then that is deliberate and there is retaliation for it. If retaliation is not possible for it, the person who caused the injury must pay [compensation] for it from his own property. If the person dies from it and it was done with an iron implement or a weapon, then retaliation is due for it. If it was done with something else, then the ʿāqilah [paternal male relatives] must pay the compensatory payment.’”

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to all of this and we also adhere to it except in one respect: if he struck him with something other than a weapon which nevertheless took the place of a weapon then retaliation is due for it, and that is the verdict of Abū Yūsuf and it is our verdict.”
