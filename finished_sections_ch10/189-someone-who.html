189. SOMEONE WHO IS FOUND KILLED IN HIS HOUSE

597. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who comes to another’s house by night and is found dead in the morning, and the owner of the house claims that he fought and overcame him and thus killed him. He said, ‘One looks into the circumstances of the deceased, and if he was a corrupt person1663 suspected of theft, [retaliation] for his death is invalid, but [the owner of the house] owes compensatory payment. If [the deceased] was not suspected of anything like that and was only known for good behaviour, [his killer] is killed [in retaliation] for him.’ If the owner of the house claimed that he found him on his wife’s belly and for that reason killed him, he said, ‘One investigates and if he was a corrupt person suspected of fornication and adultery, retaliation [for his death] is invalid, but he owes compensatory payment. If he was not suspected of anything like that and only good was known of him, this one is killed in retaliation for him.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, with respect to the thief. As for the adulterer, I do not recall that from him.”
