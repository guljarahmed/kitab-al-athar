198. THE PUNISHMENT FOR A SLAVE WOMAN WHO FORNICATES OR COMMITS ADULTERY

618. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Maʿqil ibn Muqarrin al-Muzanī went to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd with a slave woman of his who had fornicated and he said, ‘Flog her fifty times.’1703 So he said, ‘She has not been married.’1704 ʿAbdullāh said, ‘Her being a Muslim is [equivalent] to her marriage (iḥṣān).’ He said, ‘A slave of mine stole from another slave of mine.’ He said, ‘There is no amputation [of his hand] due against him. [Both slaves and the stolen item are] your property; some of it is in the hands of others.’ He said, ‘I have sworn never to sleep on a bed’ meaning as an act of worship. Ibn Masʿūd said, ‘You who have īmān! do not make ḥarām the good things Allah has made ḥalāl for you, and do not overstep the limits. Allah does not love people who overstep the limits.’ (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 87) The man said, ‘If it had not been for this āyah I would not have asked you.’ He ordered him to expiate [the breaking of the oath] by freeing a slave – the man was prosperous – and to sleep in his bed.”1705

Muḥammad said, “This is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict except in one respect: only the ruler executes ḥadd punishments. So if a slave woman or man fornicates it is the ruler who executes the punishment and not the owner.”

Footnotes

  1. Allah, exalted is He, informed that if slave women have become muḥṣan through accepting Islam or marriage they have no more than half of the ḥadd punishment of free women. Since, He laid on them a half of the ḥadd punishment due a free woman if she is muḥṣan, we know that He meant flogging since stoning can’t be half done. ↩
  2. Meaning, “Even if she has not married.” Ibn ʿAbbās said that a slave does not have to be given the ḥadd punishment even if she has become a Muslim unless she marries. It was for this reason that Ibn Masʿūd said, “Her being a Muslim is [equivalent] to her marriage (iḥṣān).” The difference of opinion is based on the recitation of this word [in Sūrat an-Nisā’ 26]. It is narrated of Ibn ʿAbbās that it is recited as uḥṣinna with a ḍammah (u) on the alif meaning “they are married” [the recitation of Ḥafṣ] but from Ibn Masʿūd and others that it is aḥṣanna with a fatḥah (a) meaning “they become Muslims.” It is not impossible that both meanings are intended by this word since both interpretations are possible. ↩
  3. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.5, p.497 in an abridged form. ↩
