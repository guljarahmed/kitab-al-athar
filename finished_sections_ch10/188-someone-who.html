188. SOMEONE WHO KILLS HIS SLAVE OR A MEMBER OF HIS CLOSE FAMILY

595. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘’ʿAbd al-Karīm narrated to us from a man from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb that a desert Arab said to his umm walad, “Go and shepherd these lambs,”1658 and so her son said, “Then I will go, so keep her back, because I am afraid that ignoble baseborn1659 people will alight with her as guests.” He said, “You have reached to this!”1660 and then he struck at him with his sword to kill him and cut off his leg [and he died]. This was raised to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and he ordered that he be killed, but Muʿādh ibn Jabal said, “There is no retaliation between son and father, but compensatory payment from out of his own wealth.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Someone who deliberately kills his own son is not to be killed for it,1661 but he is bound to pay compensatory payment from out of his own wealth over three years, paying each year a third of the compensatory payment. He may not inherit any of the compensatory payment or any of his son’s wealth, but the relatives closest to the son after the father inherit. The father does not preclude anyone else from inheriting1662 and in this it is as if he were dead. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

596. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who deliberately killed his slave, ‘He is given over to his heirs who kill him if they wish or pardon him if they wish.’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this; there is no retaliation between a slave and his master, but the master is beaten painfully and placed in prison, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
