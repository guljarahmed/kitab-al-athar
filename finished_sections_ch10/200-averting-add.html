200. AVERTING ḤADD PUNISHMENTS

621. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, ‘Avert ḥadd punishments from Muslims as much as you are able, since for a ruler to pardon mistakenly is better than for him to punish mistakenly. So if you find some way out for a Muslim, avert [the ḥadd punishment] from him.’”1714

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict.”

622. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm who said, ‘If a man says about his wife whom he had married, “I did not find her a virgin,” there is no ḥadd punishment due upon him.’”

Muḥammad said, “And this is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and it is our verdict.”

623. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘ If a man says to another, “So-and-so was not your mother,” then that is nothing.’”1715

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict, since he had not repudiated his attribution to his father, but only said that his mother did not give birth to him. The repudiation of lineage for which there is a punishment is the statement, ‘Your father is not your real father.’”

624. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham from a man who narrated to him that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb was brought a man who had sexual intercourse with an animal, but he averted the punishment from him and ordered that the beast be burnt.”1716

625. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿĀṣim ibn Abī’n-Najūd from Abū Razīn from Ibn ʿAbbās who said, ‘Whoever has sexual intercourse with an animal is not due a ḥadd punishment.’”1717

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict.” Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad said, “If it was his own animal it is to be slaughtered1718 and then burnt, but it is not to be burnt without being slaughtered because that would be an exemplary punishment.”1719
