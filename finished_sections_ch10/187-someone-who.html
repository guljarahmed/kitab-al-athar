187. SOMEONE WHO KILLS AND THEN ONE OF THE HEIRS PARDONS HIM

593. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb was brought a man who had deliberately killed someone and he gave the command that he should be killed. One of the heirs pardoned1652 but he gave the command that he should be killed. ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘The right to take his life belonged to all of them,1653 so when this one pardoned him he gave life to that person and so is unable to take his share [of the compensatory payment],’ meaning of the one who had not pardoned, ‘and thus take someone else’s share’.1654 He said, ‘What do you think?’ He said, ‘I think that he [the killer] should be made responsible for the compensatory payment1655 from out of his own wealth and that he should not have to pay1656 the portion of the man who pardoned him.’ ʿUmar said, ‘And I too think that.’”1657

“That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

594. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever of those who possess a share [in the inheritance] pardons, then his pardon is pardon.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Whoever pardons, whether his [the deceased’s] wife, mother, brother or anyone else, then that pardon is valid, and [the killer’s] life is spared, and the remainder have their share of the compensatory payment. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
