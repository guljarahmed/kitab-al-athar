191. SOMEONE WHO ACCUSES A WHOLE PEOPLE OF SEXUAL IMPROPRIETY, AND THE ḤADD PUNISHMENT FOR A FREE MAN AND A SLAVE

604. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you fabricate something against a people and you say, “Adulterers!” you only have a single punishment.’”1675 1676

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict.”

605. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who accused a man of sexual impropriety and then accused another, that he said, ‘If he were to accuse the people of the Jumuʿah, accusing all of them, he would still only have a single punishment.’”

Muḥammad said, “And this is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict. He only has a single punishment until the punishment is completed. If he accuses another person after the completion of the punishment he is flogged from the beginning again except that he is held in custody until he has recovered from the first and then flogged for the other.” He said, “The punishment is spread over his limbs if he is being flogged.”

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict concerning all ḥadd punishments [that require flogging] except that we do not strike the head, the face or the private parts. As for in discretionary punishments (taʿzīr)1677 it is not spread over the limbs as it is in ḥadd punishments, but he is flogged in one place which is the severest flogging.1678 He is not to be stripped for ḥadd punishments, nor for discretionary punishments nor for anything else.”

606. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The fornicator is flogged severely with his clothes off. The one who has slanderously accused someone of adultery is flogged with his clothes on. The person who drinks alcohol is flogged in the same way as the slanderer and their flogging is to be less severe than that of the fornicator.’”1679

Muḥammad said, “All of this is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah except in one respect, for he would have the drinker of alcohol stripped as he would have the fornicator stripped.”

607. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a slave or slave woman makes an accusation of adultery against a free person, then their punishment is a half of the punishment for a free person: forty each.’”1680

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict.”

608. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a slave woman of whom a third1681 or two thirds is free and who is then set to work to pay the remainder1682 [and thus become completely free], and later a man accuses her of sexual impropriety. He said, ‘He is not liable for any [punishment] as long as she is working.’”

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. He did not think that the person who accused her was liable for anything because in his view she has the position of a slave woman1683 as long as she is working. As for our verdict she is a freewoman; whenever a part of her is set free, then all of her is set free and the one who accuses her of sexual impropriety must be punished, and Allah knows best.”
