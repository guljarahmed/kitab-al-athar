203. THE PUNISHMENT FOR GRAVEROBBERS

638. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that he said about the graverobber1744 when he rifled the [graves of the] dead and stripped them, “His hand is cut off.”’1745 Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘His hand is not cut off, because these belongings are not guarded, but he is beaten painfully and imprisoned until he becomes reformed in a good way.’”1746

Muḥammad said, “It has reached us that Ibn ʿAbbās gave Marwān ibn al-Ḥakam the judgement1747 that [the graverobber] should not have his hand cut off, and that is our verdict.”

Footnotes

  1. nabbāsh: the person who robs graves and takes the shroud; nabsh: to uncover and to draw out. ↩
  2. And this is the verdict of Abū Yūsuf and ash-Shāfiʿī. ↩
  3. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.5, p.518. ↩
