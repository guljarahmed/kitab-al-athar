202. THE ḤADD PUNISHMENT FOR A HIGHWAY ROBBER OR THIEF

628. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Qāsim ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān narrated to us from his father from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd who said, ‘The hand of a thief is not to be cut off for less than ten dirhams.’”1725

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

629. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The hand of a thief is not to be cut off for less than the price of a leather shield (ḥajafah1726),’ and its price used to be ten dirhams. And he [Ḥammād] said, ‘Ibrāhīm also said, “The hand of a thief is not to be cut off for less than the price of a shield (mijann),” and its price at that time was ten dirhams, “and it is not to be cut off for less than that.”’”1727 1728

630. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham from ash-Shaʿbī who ascribed it directly to the Prophet that he said, ‘The hand of a thief is not cut off for fruit (thamar) or the spadix (kathar)1729 of a palm tree.’”1730

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Fruit is that which is at the tops of date palms and trees, not protected in houses,1731 so there is no cutting off of the hand of someone who steals them. The spadix is the pith,1732 the pith of the date-palm and so there is no cutting off of the hand for stealing it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

631. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Murrah narrated to us from ʿAbdullāh ibn Salamah from ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib who said, “If a man steals, his right hand is cut off. If he repeats it, his left foot is cut off. If he does it again, he is put into prison1733 until he becomes reformed in a good way. I am ashamed before Allah to leave him without a hand with which he can eat and with which he can wash his private parts after the toilet or without a foot with which to walk.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Only a thief’s right hand and left foot are to be cut off, and nothing further is to be done if the thief steals time and time again. However, he is given a discretionary punishment and imprisoned until he becomes reformed in a good way. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

632. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The hand of a thief is cut off and he is responsible [to restore what he stole or its value].’”1734

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this. If the hand of a thief is cut off, then his being responsible [to restore what he stole or its value] is invalidated1735 except where the specific items are found in his possession, in which case they are returned to their owner.1736 That is the verdict of ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī and Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on them both.”

633. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibrāhīm ibn Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir narrated to us from his father from Yazīd ibn Abī Kabshah who said, “Abū’d-Dardā’ was brought a black slave girl who had stolen at the time he was in charge of Damascus,1737 and he said, ‘Salāmah, have you stolen? Say, “No.”’ So she said, ‘No.’ They said, ‘Are you instructing her, Abū’d-Dardā’?’ He said, ‘You have brought me a woman who does not know what is demanded of her, in order that she confess and then I cut off her hand.’”’”

634. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Abū Masʿūd al-Anṣārī was brought a thief and he said, “Have you stolen? Say, ‘No.’” So he said, “No,” and he set him free.’”

Muḥammad said, “As for us, we say1738 that the judge ought not to say to him, ‘Have you stolen?’ but he should be silent until he admits it or denies it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Muḥammad said, “I only think that they1739 said to these two thieves, ‘Say, “No,”’ when they asked them, ‘Have you stolen?’ for fear of them answering ‘Yes,’ when questioned, even though they had not done it.1740 Similarly, Abū Ḥanīfah gave the verdict concerning a witness who testifies to the judge, ‘The judge ought not to say to him, ‘Do you testify such-and-such?’ for fear of him saying, ‘Yes,’ but he should leave him alone until he produces his own testimony. Then if his testimony is decisive he should execute it, but if it is not decisive he should then reject it [the case]. Thus it is with ḥadd punishments.”

635. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man acts as a brigand stealing property and killing, then the ruler can kill him in whatever manner he please. He may kill him by crucifixion1741 if he wishes, and he may kill him without amputation [of limbs] and without crucifixion if he wishes, and he may cut off alternate hands and feet and then have him killed if he wishes. If he stole property and did not kill anyone his alternate hand and foot are cut off. If he did not take property and did not kill anyone then he is given a painful punishment and imprisoned until he becomes reformed in a good way.”

Muḥammad said, “This is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, to which we adhere except in one respect: if he killed someone and took property then he is killed by crucifixion but his hand foot are not cut off. If two ḥadd punishments come together, one overrules the other, which is then averted.”

636. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a thief who stole and was then caught but got away, and then later he stole and was again caught, ‘His hand is cut off.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We think that he is only due to have one limb amputated, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

637. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘A man narrated to us from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, “The hand of a person who takes something stealthily1742 is not to be cut off.”’”1743

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
