201. THE PUNISHMENT FOR SOMEONE WHO IS INTOXICATED1720

626. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd al-Karīm ibn Abī’l-Mukhāriq narrated to us ascribing the hadith to the Prophet that he was brought someone who was intoxicated and told them to beat him with their sandals – and they were at that time forty men – and so each one of them struck him with their sandals. When Abū Bakr took command he was brought someone who was intoxicated and he told them to beat him with their sandals. When ʿUmar took command, he sought to find the correct ruling from people, and he had [drunks] flogged with a lash.”1721

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We think that the punishment for someone who is intoxicated, whether from nabīdh or something else, is eighty lashes1722 with the whip. He is to be taken into custody until he sobers up and the intoxication leaves him and then he is to be flogged as a punishment, the flogging being spread over his limbs, and he is to be stripped. However, he must not be struck on the private parts, the face or the head. His flogging is to be more severe than that for a slanderer, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

627. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Even if a man drinks a single sip1723 of wine he is to be flogged.’ He said, ‘I fear that an intoxicant1724 might be the same as that.’”

Muḥammad said, “One is flogged with the ḥadd punishment for a sip of wine, but as for an intoxicant then one is not to be given a ḥadd punishment unless intoxicated but rather one is given a discretionary punishment and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
