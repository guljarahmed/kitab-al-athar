190. LIʿĀN1664 AND REPUDIATION OF A CHILD

598. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke about a man who repudiated the parentage of his child and engaged in liʿān [with his wife] so that the two of them were separated, and then his father accused the child whom he repudiated of fornication or accused the mother of adultery, and he said, ‘If his father accused the one whom he repudiated as his child of fornication or [his father accused] any other people or accused the mother of adultery, then he is to be flogged.’ Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘Whoever accuses her of adultery is not flogged1665 for accusing the mother of it, because she has a child with no parentage. Whoever accuses the child himself in particular and says to him, “Fornicator!” is flogged for transgression of the ḥadd limits,’ and so says Muḥammad.”.

599. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man accused his wife of adultery and [previously] he had been punished,1666 I would flog him1667 for transgressing the ḥadd limits [by slandering her], or if he accused her of adultery and [previously] she had been flogged, there is to be no liʿān between them nor is there to be any punishment for him,’ and he said, ‘Whoever has no testimony1668 has no right to liʿān.’”

“This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad, may Allah have mercy on them both.”

600. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “If a man accused his wife of adultery and she died before he was able to engage in the process of liʿān with her, he inherits from her and there is no punishment [for his accusing her without sufficient witnesses] and there is no liʿān. It is similar if a man accused someone other than his wife1669 of sexual impropriety [and that person died], there is to be no punishment inflicted on him since the one he accused might well have confirmed his accusation. If her husband accused her and then died, she inherits from him because he had not engaged in liʿān with her.”’”

“This is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad, may Allah have mercy on them both.”

601. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Mujālid ibn Saʿīd from ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb who said, ‘If a man confirms that a child is his even if only for the blink of an eye then he has no right to repudiate him.’”

“This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad, may Allah have mercy on them both.”

602. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Shurayḥ who said, ‘If a man repudiated his parentage of his child and then later claimed him as his own, he may do that1670 and the child is ascribed to him.’”

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict.”

603. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who confirmed that his son is his own and then later repudiated him, ‘He engages in liʿān with her1671 and the child is ascribed to his mother. If he had already divorced her, he is flogged with the ḥadd punishment,1672 even if the child’s mother had already died.’”1673 1674

Muḥammad said, “This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and our verdict except in one respect: if he confirms his son and then later repudiates him while she is his wife, he engages in liʿān with her and the child is attached to his father. If he affirms him one time, he has no right to repudiate him, as ʿUmar said.”
