147. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES AND THEN TAKES HIS WIFE BACK DURING THE ʿIDDAH

483. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that a woman came to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and said, “My husband divorced me1410 and I had two periods and began the third and then my bleeding ceased. I went into my bathroom and took off my clothes, and then he1411 came to me and said, ‘I take you back,’ before I could pour water on myself.” ʿUmar said to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd , “Speak about it.”1412 He said, “Amīr al-Mu’minin, I think he has more right1413 to take her back since she is still menstruating and prayer is not permissible to her.” ʿUmar said, “I think that as well,” and so he returned her to her husband, and said, “A small vessel full of knowledge.”’”1414 1415

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. A man has more right to take his wife back up until she has performed ghusl after her third menstruation. However, if she delays the ghusl until the time of a prayer has passed during which she was able to perform a ghusl before it passed, then the right to take her back has expired, and she becomes permissible to other men, and the prayer [she missed] is obligatory upon her. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
