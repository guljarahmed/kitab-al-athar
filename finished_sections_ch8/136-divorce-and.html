DIVORCE

136. DIVORCE AND THE ʿIDDAH

462. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man wishes to divorce his wife according to the Sunnah,1360 he should leave her until she has her menstrual period1361 and then becomes pure from her menstruation. Then he should divorce her a single time without having sexual intercourse. He should leave her alone until her ʿiddah expires. If he wishes, he may divorce her three times, at each purification [from her menstruation] with a single [pronunciation of] divorce until he has [pronounced] divorce [from] her three times.’”1362

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

463. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar divorced his wife1363 while she was menstruating, but fault was found with him about that and so he took her back1364 and later divorced her in her state of purity.’”1365

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We do not think that he should divorce her in the state of purity after the menstruation during which he [mistakenly] divorced her, but he should divorce her when she becomes pure from a subsequent menstruation.”

464. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man wishes to divorce his wife while she is pregnant, he should divorce her at the sighting of each new moon.’”1366 1367

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to this,1368 may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, but our verdict is that the divorce of a pregnant woman according to the Sunnah is a single divorce which he pronounces at the sighting of the new moon or whenever he wishes. Then he leaves her until she delivers her child. Thus it has reached us from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī and Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh, and thus it has reached us from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd .”
