172. ĪLĀ’1542 [DIVORCE BROUGHT ABOUT BECAUSE THE HUSBAND SWEARS TO ABSTAIN FROM SEXUAL INTERCOURSE WITH HIS WIFE FOR FOUR MONTHS]

537. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man swears to abstain from sexual intercourse with his wife, but then has intercourse with her within the four months, he must pay an expiation [for the broken oath].’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and the īlā’ is invalidated, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

538. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘ʿAbdullāh ibn Anas an-Nakhaʿī swore to abstain from sexual intercourse with his wife and then was away from her for five months. Later he came and had sexual intercourse with her, and came out to his companions with his head dripping from [the ghusl after] intercourse. They said to him, “You had intercourse with so-and-so?” He said, “Yes.” They asked, “Had you not sworn not to have sexual intercourse with her?” He said, “Certainly.” They said, “We are afraid for you that she might have become finally divorced from you.” So they took him to ʿAlqamah but found nothing [concerning the matter] with him. ʿAlqamah took them to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd and he mentioned the matter to him. He told him to go and tell her that she had become finally divorced from him and to propose to her. So he went to her and told her, and proposed to her with [a dowry of] some mithqals of silver.’”1543

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we think that he owes her a dowry for having had sexual intercourse with her before the second marriage, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī and Ḥammād ibn Abī Sulaymān.”

539. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Murrah narrated to us from Abū ʿUbaydah from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd who said, “If a man swears to abstain from sexual intercourse with his wife and four months passes, she becomes finally divorced1544 from him with a single divorce and he is a suitor1545 who can propose to her within her ʿiddah and no one else may propose to her within her ʿiddah.”’”1546

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The divorce takes effect1547 with the completion of the four months. [His] returning [to her] is the act of sexual intercourse within the four months, and the execution [of the divorce] is not stayed1548 after that, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

540. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that a man’s wife gave birth and she said to him, ‘Don’t approach me until I wean this child of mine because I am afraid to become pregnant at the same time as him,’ and so he swore an oath that he would not approach her until she had weaned him. He [Ḥammād] said, ‘So I asked him about that and he said, “I am afraid that it might be īlā’, but I hope that it is not īlā’.”’”

Muḥammad said, “So I asked Abū Ḥanīfah about that and he said, ‘It is īlā’.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this.”

541. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū’l-ʿAṭūf narrated to us from az-Zuhrī that the Prophet swore to abstain from sexual intercourse with his wives for a month. Then when twenty-nine days had passed he sent a message to ʿĀ’ishah , “Come.” She sent a message back to him, “You have sworn to abstain from sexual intercourse with me. I continue to count the days and the nights and one day of the month still remains.” So he sent a message back to her, “Come, because the month [can be both] thirty and twenty-nine [days].”’”1549

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if it is reckoned by new moons. If it is reckoned by any other reckoning than new moons then the month is thirty days, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

542. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who says to his wife, “If I approach you then you are divorced,” and then leaves her for four months. He said, “She becomes finally divorced by īlā’.”’”

“That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
