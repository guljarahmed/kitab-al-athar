150. TAKING BACK A WIFE ONE HAS DIVORCED

488. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife retaining the right to take her back, then she may deck herself1435 out hoping that he will take her back. However, if he has not retained the right to take her back, or in the case of a woman whose husband has died, then she may not deck herself out, nor dress in clothes dyed yellow, and she has to avoid using kohl and perfume except for medicinal purposes.’”1436

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

489. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man fondles1437 his wife out of sexual appetite during her ʿiddah, that is his taking her back, and if he kisses her during her ʿiddah, that is his taking her back.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. i.e., beautify herself for her husband because she is ḥalāl for her husband since the marriage between them still exists, and moreover their returning to each other is recommended, and since her beautifying herself would lead to that then it is a part of the Sharīʿah to do so. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.145. ↩
  3. The act of taking her back would be if he said, “I take you back,” and this is a clear declaration about which there is no difference of opinion, or that he makes love to her, or kisses her or fondles her with passion, and this is the position according to us. Ash-Shāfiʿī said that the act of taking back is only valid by his saying it and being able to do so. ↩
