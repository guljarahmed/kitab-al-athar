169. SOMEONE WHO SAYS TO HIS WIFE, “YOU ARE ḤARĀM TO ME.”

523. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who says to his wife, ‘You are forbidden (ḥarām) to me,’ that if he intended divorce, it is a single one and he retains the right to take her back.’”1511

Muḥammad said, “As for the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, it is that if he intended divorce, it is as he intended. If he intended a single divorce, it is a single final1512 divorce, and if he intended a divorce without specifying a number, it is a single final divorce. If he intended two divorces, it is a single final divorce. If he intended a single divorce in which he retained the right to take her back, it is a single final divorce. If he intended three divorces, it is three and she is not permissible to him until after she has married another man. If he did not intend divorce,1513 it is an oath and he is a man who has sworn to abstain from intercourse with his wife (īlā’).1514 If he leaves her for four months and does not come near her, she becomes finally divorced1515 from him by īlā’. [See hadith 412] If he had no intention, it is still īlā’. If he intended it as a lie, it is of no account,1516 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.

Footnotes

  1. See the traditions about that in the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shaybah, vol.4, pp.98-99. ↩
  2. If he did not intend a particular number of divorces or intended it once or twice, because it is an allusion. ↩
  3. If he said, “I meant to render her ḥarām,” [which is regarded as an oath that has to be expiated but not as divorce. – Trans.] or he said, “I didn’t mean anything by it.” ↩
  4. Because the principle in declaring the ḥalāl to be ḥarām is that it is an oath in our view even if he intended to lie because the woman is ḥalāl for him, so that his saying, “You are ḥarām,” does not correspond to the actual situation and is a lie. ↩
  5. And if he approaches her within four months he must make expiation. ↩
  6. Divorce does not take place because he intended the literal meaning of what he said. Some shaykhs consider the wording of declaring her ḥarām to be a divorce, although unintended, according to customary usage since it is customary among people in this age of ours that they intend divorce by it, and the faqīh, Abū’l-Layth said, “We adhere to that.” ↩
