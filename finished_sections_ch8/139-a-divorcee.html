139. A DIVORCEE, WHOSE WIFE REMARRIES AND LATER RETURNS TO HIM

467. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr who said, ‘I was seated with ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUtbah ibn Masʿūd when a Bedouin Arab came to ask him about a man who had divorced his wife once or twice, and her ʿiddah had expired and she had married another husband who had consummated the marriage with her, and who had then himself died or divorced her. Then her ʿiddah had expired, and the former husband wanted to marry her. [He asked] “How many [divorces] is she now reckoned to have had from him?”’1375 He [Saʿīd ibn Jubayr] said, ‘He [ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUtbah] said to me, “Answer him.” Then he said, “What does Ibn ʿAbbās say about it?”’ He said, ‘So I [Saʿīd ibn Jubayr] said, “It obliterates one, two1376 and three [divorces].” He said, “Have you heard anything about it from Ibn ʿUmar?”’ He [Saʿīd ibn Jubayr] said, ‘I said, “No.” He said, “When you meet him, ask him.”’ He [Saʿīd ibn Jubayr] said, ‘So I met Ibn ʿUmar and asked him about it, and he said the same about it as had Ibn ʿAbbās .’”1377

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah used to adhere to this,1378 may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, but our verdict is that she is on whatever remains of her divorce1379 if anything1380 remains of it.1381 That was the verdict of ʿUmar, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, Muʿādh ibn Jabal, Ubayy ibn Kaʿb, ʿImrān ibn Ḥuṣayn and Abū Hurayrah .”

468. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife and then takes her back, whatever has passed of her ʿiddah is voided, and if he divorces her [again] then the ʿiddah begins again anew.’”1382

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
