142. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES DURING HIS ILLNESS BEFORE OR AFTER CONSUMMATING THE MARRIAGE

471. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a sick person who divorces his wife1388 and dies before her ʿiddah has expired that she inherits from him and observes the ʿiddah of someone whose husband has died.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if it was a divorce in which he retained the right to take back his wife. If the divorce was final, she has to observe as ʿiddah whichever of the two is longer:1389 the three menstruations from the day on which he pronounced the divorce or the four months and ten days from the day on which he died, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”1390

472. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife once, twice or thrice while he is ill, without having consummated the marriage with her, she is owed a half of the dowry but does not inherit and has no ʿiddah.’”1391

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

473. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who divorces his wife once or twice,1392 that they inherit from each other as long as [either dies while] she is in her ʿiddah. Then she begins afresh the ʿiddah of the woman whose husband has died, four months and ten days. If he had divorced her three times while in good health and then died, her ʿiddah is that of a divorced woman, three menstrual periods.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

474. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife three times during ill health, and then dies in that illness of his before her ʿiddah has expired, she inherits and observes the ʿiddah of a woman whose husband has died. If her ʿiddah expires before he dies, she does not inherit from him and she has no ʿiddah to observe1393 [as a widow].’”1394

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this except in one respect: if she inherits, she observes as ʿiddah the longer of the two terms as I described to you [see 471] and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

475. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a woman obtains a divorce from her husband while he is ill for a compensation which she pays him [khulʿ] and he dies from his illness, she has no inheritance.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because she was the one who sought that of her husband, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
