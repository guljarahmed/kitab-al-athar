144. THE ʿIDDAH OF THE DIVORCED WOMAN WHOSE MENSTRUATION HAS CEASED

478. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlqamah divorced his wife one time1397 and that she menstruated one time,1398 and then her menstruation ceased for eighteen months and then she died.1399 He mentioned that to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd and he said, ‘This is a woman whose inheritance Allah kept back for you,1400 so make use of it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. She always observes the ʿiddah according to the menstrual periods until she despairs of menstruating at which point she observes the ʿiddah according to the months. Her husband inherits from her as long as she is in the ʿiddah,1401 and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. With a divorce in which he retained the right to take her back. ↩
  2. And in one narration, “A single menstruation or two.” ↩
  3. She died before the ʿiddah in terms of menstrual cycles had been completed. ↩
  4. He had kept it back by extending the ʿiddah. The upshot of it is that Ibn Masʿūd gave the fatwā that the ʿiddah had not come to an end even if eighteen months had passed from the time of the divorce as long as she had not menstruated. That is because she had not come to despair of menstruating but on the contrary her menstruation had disappeared because of her breastfeeding or some other reason. So she does not come out of the ʿiddah as long as she does not menstruate. ↩
  5. As she would also inherit from him if he died during her ʿiddah. ↩
