173. SOMEONE WHO SWEARS TO ABSTAIN FROM INTERCOURSE WITH HIS WIFE AND THEN DIVORCES HER

543. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man swears to abstain from intercourse with his wife and then later divorces her, the divorce overturns1550 the īlā’.’”1551

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this.”

544. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from ash-Shaʿbī who said, ‘If a man swears to abstain from sexual intercourse with his wife and later divorces her, the two cases are like two horses in a wager:1552 if she has passed the four months and is in any part of her ʿiddah [from the īlā’ divorce] then the divorce due to the īlā’ takes place along with the divorce which has been pronounced. If the ʿiddah [of the divorce] has expired before the time of the four months comes then the īlā’ is dropped.’”1553

Muḥammad said, “So I said to Abū Ḥanīfah, ‘To which of the two verdicts do you adhere?’ He said, ‘The verdict of ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to it.”

  3. rahn: a pledge, that which is deposited with a person as a substitute for something taken from him. [Here it is a wager placed so that whichever horse comes in first takes the wager. – Trans.] It means that if the ʿiddah [of divorce], which is three menstrual periods, comes to an end before his īlā’, which is four months, then she is divorced finally from him by that divorce and she does not have an īlā’ since when the four months came to an end she is no longer married to him. If the four months come to an end and she is still in her ʿiddah [from the divorce] then she becomes finally divorced from him [because of the īlā’] along with that divorce and so they are counted as two divorces. So he regarded them as two horses racing for a wager. ↩
