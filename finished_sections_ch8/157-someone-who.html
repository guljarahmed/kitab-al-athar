157. SOMEONE WHO WRITES ABOUT DIVORCING HIS WIFE

498. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If her husband writes to her about her divorce intending divorce then she is divorced from the moment he writes it.’”

Muḥammad said, “If he had written to her, ‘When this letter of mine reaches you, you are divorced,’ then she is not divorced until the letter reaches her. If he had written, ‘To proceed; you are divorced,’ then she is divorced at the instant he wrote the letter, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

499. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning a man who writes to his wife, ‘When this letter of mine reaches you, you are divorced,’ and he said, ‘If the letter reaches her, she is divorced on the day it reaches her. If the letter goes missing or is erased, then it is nothing [it is not a divorce]. If he wrote to her, ‘To proceed; you are divorced,’ the divorce takes place on the day he wrote it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
