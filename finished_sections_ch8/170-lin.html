170. LIʿĀN1517

524. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Liʿān is a final divorce.’”1518 1519

525. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the two parties to a liʿān divorce, ‘They are to be separated because it is a final divorce.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

526. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “If a man accuses his wife of adultery but then does not engage in the liʿān procedure with her, they remain in their marriage. However, if he engages in the procedure of liʿān with her, she becomes finally divorced from him,1520 and he is never allowed to remarry her unless he himself admits to lying. Then if he himself admits to lying,1521 he may marry her.”’”1522

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, if he himself admits to lying and is flogged for transgressing the limits [concerning slander] and his testimony is invalidated1523 then he may remarry her, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

527. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who accused his wife of adultery and then divorced her three times that he said, “There is to be no procedure of liʿān between them1524 nor is any punishment to be imposed on him for transgressing the limits [concerning slander], since he had accused her of adultery while she was married to him, and the opportunity for liʿān had come about but he did not engage in the procedure of liʿān before divorcing her. So the procedure of liʿān is invalid and there is no punishment for transgressing the limits to be imposed on him.”’”1525

528. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who accused his wife of adultery about which [accusation] she was silent1526 and he divorced her three times, but later she was prepared [to defend herself],1527 that there is to be no procedure of liʿān between them.’”1528

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

529. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man accuses his wife of adultery and one of them engages in the procedure of liʿān, they still may inherit from each other as long as the other person has not engaged in the procedure of liʿān.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. They inherit from each other as long as they have not both engaged in the procedure of liʿān, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

  1. liʿān derives from “cursing” which means expulsion and driving far away, and in the Sharīʿah it is acts of testification emphasised by an oath which takes place between a couple with a curse which stands in place of the punishment for slander with respect to the man and in place of the punishment for adultery with respect to the woman. [“Those who make an accusation against their wives and have no witnesses except themselves, such people should testify four times by Allah that they are telling the truth and a fifth time that Allah’s curse will be upon them if they are lying. And the punishment is removed from her if she testifies four times by Allah that he is lying and a fifth time that Allah’s anger will be upon her if he is telling the truth” Sūrat an-Nūr: 6-9.] ↩

So nothing remains after his admission to lying. As for in reality, then that is obvious. As for in judgement, then when he admitted to lying, he necessitated punishment [for having slandered her] and so his competence as a witness for liʿān was invalidated, and when the competence is invalidated then the judgement is removed, and so they may reunite. ↩
