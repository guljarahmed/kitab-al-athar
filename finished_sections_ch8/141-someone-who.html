141. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES THREE TIMES BEFORE CONSUMMATING THE MARRIAGE

470. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife three times together before consummating marriage with her, she becomes finally divorced from him because of them all together1384 and she is forbidden to him until she marries another husband. So when he separates [from her] she becomes finally divorced by the first [divorce]1385 and the second [divorce] falls upon someone who is not his wife.’”1386 1387

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. All together and not separated. When the three take place all at once they take effect. If he were to separate them by saying, “You are divorced, and divorced, and divorced,” or by repeating it without the conjunction [and], the first in particular takes effect since the “and” is an unqualified conjunction, and there is no precondition or exception at the end of the phrase to alter its beginning. Mālik, and ash-Shāfiʿī in his older school, said that he has thrice divorced. ↩
  2. And she has no ʿiddah. ↩
  3. The second and third divorces do not take effect because she became finally divorced by the first declaration. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.131. ↩
