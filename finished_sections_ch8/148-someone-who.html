148. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES HIS WIFE AND THEN TAKES HER BACK, BUT SHE DOES NOT LEARN OF IT UNTIL AFTER SHE HAS REMARRIED

484. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Abū Kanf1416 divorced his wife once1417 and then absented himself.1418 Then he had his act of taking her back witnessed,1419 but that did not reach her until she had remarried. When he returned she was preparing to be conducted1420 to her [new] husband. He1421 went to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and mentioned that to him, and he wrote to his governor,1422 ‘Go to her and if you find1423 that he has not consummated the marriage,1424 then he1425 has more right to her. However, if you find her and he has already consummated the marriage with her,1426 she is his wife.’”1427 He said, “He discovered her1428 on the night she was to be conducted [to her new husband] and he1429 had sexual intercourse with her. Then he went to ʿUmar’s governor and told him and he realised that the matter was clear.”1430

485. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib used to say, “If a man divorces his wife and then has his taking her back witnessed before the expiry of her ʿiddah but without making her aware of that before the expiry of her ʿiddah and her remarriage, in that case she is to be separated from her second husband, she has the dowry because of his having sexual intercourse with her, and she remains the wife of the former1431 and is returned to him. However, he may not approach her until her ʿiddah from the other man expires.”’”1432

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of ʿAlī , and it is preferable to us than the former verdict. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
