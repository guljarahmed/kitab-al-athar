166. THE ʿIDDAH OF THE UMM WALAD1499

517. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning an umm walad whose master dies, ‘If she is menstruating, [her ʿiddah is] three menstruations, but if she does not menstruate, then three months. It is similar if he sets her free.’”1500

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

518. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning the miscarriage of a child1501 which a slave woman had by her master, that he said, “As long as a finger, eye or mouth cannot be clearly discerned in it, then she is not set free and she does not by that become an umm walad.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this: when anything of its form is clear then on that basis she is an umm walad. If nothing of its form is clear, then on that basis she is not an umm walad, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. An umm walad is a slave woman who is mother of a child by her master. Neither she nor her child may be sold and on the death of her master she automatically becomes free. – Trans. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.145. ↩
  3. If the umm walad miscarries [which is not completely formed] does she become an umm walad or not? ↩
