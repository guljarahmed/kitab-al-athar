158. THE DIVORCE OF THE PERSON SUFFERING FROM PLEURISY, THE INEBRIATED PERSON AND THE PERSON ASLEEP

500. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The divorce of someone suffering pleurisy1467 is not counted as anything until they recover consciousness.’”1468

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if he had not grasped [what he was doing]1469 and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

501. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The divorce of the inebriated person1470 is valid.’”1471

502. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us from ash-Shaʿbī from Shurayḥ who said, “The divorce of the inebriated person is valid.”’”1472

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

503. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The divorce someone [pronounces while] asleep is nothing.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

504. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that he said concerning someone inebriated, ‘His freeing slaves, his divorcing and his sale are valid.’”1473

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
