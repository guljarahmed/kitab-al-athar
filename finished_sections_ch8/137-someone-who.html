137. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES HIS WIFE WHILE SHE IS PREGNANT

465. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning the woman who is divorced,1369 and the woman who is divorced at her own request for a compensation which she pays (khulʿ), and the woman who is divorced because of her husband’s oath to abstain from intercourse for a period longer than four months (īlā’) and his adherence to that oath,1370 ‘If she is pregnant or otherwise then she has maintenance and accommodation until she delivers the baby1371 unless the husband of a woman divorced at her own request for a compensation stipulates that after the khulʿ she will have no maintenance.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. A divorce in which the husband can take her back within the ʿiddah or a final divorce. Ash-Shāfiʿī said that if the husband divorces her thrice or divorces her for compensation then she has no maintenance unless she is pregnant. His proof is that which Fāṭimah bint Qays narrated, when she said, “My husband divorced me three times and the Messenger of Allah did not appoint a dwelling for me nor any maintenance.” The answer to this is that ʿUmar rejected it saying, “We will not abandon the Book of our Lord nor the Sunnah of our Prophet for the word of a woman whom we do not know whether or not she remembered.” ʿĀ’ishah said, “Fāṭimah lived in an isolated place and so one feared for her because of her neighbourhood, and so for that reason the Messenger of Allah gave her special license [not to spend her ʿiddah in the house in which she was divorced].” ↩
  2. Lexically īlā’ means an oath, and in the Sharīʿah it denotes withholding oneself from approaching one’s wife for four months or more if that act of withholding oneself is stressed with an oath. ↩
  3. i.e., if she was pregnant. Otherwise the ʿiddah of a woman divorced such that her husband retains the right to take her back or in a final divorce is three menstrual periods. If she does not menstruate because of her youth or her age, then her ʿiddah is three months. The ʿiddah of the widow is four months and ten days. ↩
