171. THE WOMAN BEING GIVEN THE CHOICE [OF DIVORCE] AND [THE STATEMENT] “YOUR AFFAIR IS IN YOUR OWN HANDS”

530. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man says to his wife, “Your affair is in your own hands,” then she can only choose a single [divorce], but if he says, “That of divorce which is in my hands is now in your hands,” she must decide in that sitting before they separate. If she says that it is one divorce or two divorces, it is whatever she says.’”

Muḥammad said, “As for our verdict, if he says to her, ‘Your affair is in your own hands,’ and she chooses her own self, it is according to what the husband intended. If he intended a single divorce, it is a single final divorce, and if he intended three divorces, it is three,1529 and if he intended two divorces, it is a single final divorce. It will never be anything other than a single final divorce or three divorces if he intended that. If he had not intended divorce and it had come out of anger, he is not believed in judgement [in court], but he is believed in that which is between him and Allah, exalted is He. If it had taken place, but not because of anger, he is believed in all of that, if he swears an oath. All of this is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

531. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning a man who said to his wife, ‘You choose!’ or ‘Your affair is in your own hands!’ and he said, ‘They are the same.’”1530

Muḥammad said, “We say they are the same, but that it is her right1531 as long as she is still sitting [with her husband] and does not take up some other activity. If she takes up some other activity or stands up from sitting with him, her choice is obviated. If she chooses her self, there are two differing judgements.1532 As for his saying, ‘You choose!’ if he had meant divorce, it is a single final divorce in any case whether he meant three divorces or something else, and that is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

532. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man offers his wife the choice [of divorce] but she stands up from that sitting of hers, she [no longer] has a choice.’”

533. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Dīnār narrated to us from Jābir who said, “If a man offers his wife the choice [of divorce] but she stands up from sitting [with him] then she has no choice.”’”1533

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Muḥammad said, “The one from whom he narrated was Jābir ibn Zayd Abū’sh-Shuʿthā’.”

534. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd both used to say about a woman whose husband offers her the choice [of divorce] but she chose to stay with him, that she is his wife. If she chose herself, it is a divorce1534 and her husband has more right to her.”1535 1536

535. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that Zayd ibn Thābit used to say, “If she chose her husband, it is nothing and she is his wife. However, if she chose herself, it is three [divorces]1537 and she is unlawful [ḥarām] to him until she marries another husband.” ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib used to say, “If she chose her husband, it [still counts as] a single divorce and the husband has more right to her. If she chose herself, it is a single [divorce]1538 and she has more right to herself.”’”1539

536. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm from ʿĀ’ishah who said, “The Messenger of Allah gave us [his wives] the choice and we chose him and he did not count that against us as a divorce.”’”1540

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of ʿĀ’ishah which she narrated from the Prophet , and to the verdict of ʿUmar and Ibn Masʿūd : if she chooses her husband, it is nothing. And [we adhere] to the verdict of ʿAlī : if she chooses her self, it is a single [divorce] and she has more right to herself1541 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
