161. SOMEONE WHO SAYS, “IF I MARRY SO-AND-SO THEN SHE IS DIVORCED.”

508. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Muḥammad ibn Qays1477 from Ibrāhīm from ʿĀmir that al-Aswad ibn Yazīd said concerning a woman who was mentioned to him, ‘If I marry her,1478 she is divorced.’ Al-Aswad did not think that it was anything. He asked the people of the Ḥijāz and they did not think that it was anything. So he married her and consummated the marriage with her. That was mentioned to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd and he told him to tell her1479 that she had more right to herself.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd . We think that she has a dowry which is a half of the dowry for which he married her, or the dowry of someone her equal1480 if he consummated the marriage with her, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. And in a narration in the Muṣannaf of ʿAbd ar-Razzāq, “From Muḥammad ibn Qays who said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm and ash-Shaʿbī about divorce [declared] before marriage and they said that al-Aswad named a woman and said that if he married her she was divorced. So he asked Ibn Masʿūd about that and he said, “She has become finally divorced from you.”’” ↩
  2. He connected the divorce to the coming into possession of her [in marriage] itself or because of it. Ash-Shāfiʿī said that this connection is not valid and that divorce will not be executed because of it because of that which Abū Dāwūd and others narrated, “There is no divorce in respect of that [woman] whom one has no authority over,” and in another narration, “There is no divorce before marriage,” to which our companions responded by interpreting it to refer to execution [meaning that a divorce pronounced before marriage is only executed upon marriage]. ↩
  3. And divorce takes place by the simple fact of making the contract [of marriage]. ↩
  4. The “dowry of someone her equal” means the dowry that women in her family normally receive, such as her sisters, cousins, etc. ↩
