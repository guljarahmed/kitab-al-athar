168. THE WOMAN DIVORCED AT HER OWN REQUEST FOR COMPENSATION, WHICH SHE PAYS (KHULʿ)

520. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb who said, ‘If she were to obtain divorce by paying in compensation the string with which she ties her hair, that would be valid.’”1504

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Whatever she pays to obtain her divorce, even if it were her entire wealth,1505 that is acceptable in settlement.”1506

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

521. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If the wrongdoing is on the part of the woman, it is permissible for you to accept the ransom. However, if it is on the part of the man, it is not permissible for him to take the ransom.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. It is not desirable for him1507 to take any more than what he gave her, but if he does do that, it is valid in settlement.”

522. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿUmārah or ʿAmmār or Abū ʿAmmār1508 (the doubt was on the part of Muḥammad) from his father from ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib who said, ‘Only take from her in a khulʿ divorce what you have given her, because there is no good1509 in more [than that].’”1510
