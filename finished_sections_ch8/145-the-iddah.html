145. THE ʿIDDAH OF THE DIVORCED WOMAN WHO IS PREGNANT

479. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd who said, ‘The shorter Sūrat an-Nisā’1402 abrogates1403 every other ʿiddah in the Qur’ān: “The time for women who are pregnant is when they give birth.”’” (Sūrat aṭ-Ṭalāq: 4)’”1404

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If she is divorced or her husband dies and she gives birth a day or less or more after that, her ʿiddah has expired and she is permissible to men [to marry] at that moment even if she is bleeding in the after birth state, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”

480. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife and she then has a miscarriage, her ʿiddah has expired.’”1405

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. A miscarriage1406 is not counted as such by us until some of its form is clear, whether hair or nails etc. If she delivers something whose form is unclear, her ʿiddah does not come to an end by means of it. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”
