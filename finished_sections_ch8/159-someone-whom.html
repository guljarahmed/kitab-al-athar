159. SOMEONE WHOM THE RULER FORCES TO PRONOUNCE DIVORCE OR TO FREE A SLAVE

505. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man whom the ruler forces to pronounce divorce or to free a slave and then he pronounces divorce or sets a slave free against his will, ‘It is valid for him,1474 and if Allah had wished He would have tested him with something worse than that.’ And he said, ‘It takes effect however it was.’”1475

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Meaning that it is executed. The three Imams said that the divorce and the act of setting a slave free are not executed when the person has been coerced to do so. As for our position, it is valid, which is deduced from the analogous situation of someone doing so in jest. The basic position with us (Ḥanafīs) which cannot be interpreted to be invalid is that coercion does not prevent its being executed, and that similarly everything which when undertaken in jest would be executed is also executed when undertaken because of coercion. ↩
  2. See the traditions about that in the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shaybah, vol.4, pp.85-86. ↩
