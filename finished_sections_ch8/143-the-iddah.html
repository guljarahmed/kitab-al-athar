143. THE ʿIDDAH OF A DIVORCED WOMAN WHO DESPAIRS OF MENSTRUAL PERIODS

476. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man divorces his wife and she despairs of having menstrual periods, she observes the ʿiddah by months. Then if after that she menstruates, she must reckon up1395 whatever has already passed of her first menstruations.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

477. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm, ‘If a man divorced his wife and she observed the ʿiddah for a month or two, and menstruated once or twice, and then came to despair [of menstruating again], she must begin with the months afresh. If she menstruates after that, she observes the ʿiddah based on what menstruations there have already been.’”1396

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. She annuls what of her ʿiddah she discharged in months, and has to start the ʿiddah again according to menstrual periods because in the āyah the monthly reckoning is a substitute for the reckoning according to menstrual periods, and one does not take into account the substitute when there is the possibility of doing the original. So when she sees blood, then she knows that her having despaired of going according to the original ruling has not been realised, and the precondition is that one despair of it right up until death, just as in the case of a decrepit old man paying a ransom [instead of fasting]. ↩
