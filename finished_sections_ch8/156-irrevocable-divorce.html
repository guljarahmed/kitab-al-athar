156. IRREVOCABLE DIVORCE

496. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the [man who uses terms such as] khaliyyah (a woman set free),1455 bariyyah1456 (a woman from whom one has declared oneself quit), bā’in (in a state of final separation) and battah (irrevocable),1457 ‘If he intended divorce, it is what he intended. If he intended three,1458 three it is. If he intended one divorce, it is one final (bā’in) divorce and he may be a suitor [because it is not irrevocable if bā’in]. If he did not intend a divorce, it is nothing.’”1459

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

497. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUrwah ibn al-Mughīrah was tried by it1460 when he was amir of Kufa and so he sent a message to Shurayḥ1461 saying,1462 ‘Give your verdict on a man who said to his wife, “You are irrevocably divorced (battah).”’ He said,1463 ‘ʿUmar said about it that it is a single divorce and that he retains the right to take her back.’ He said, ‘ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said that it is three [divorces].’ He said, ‘You give your own verdict.’ He said, ‘Both of them have already given their verdicts on it.’1464 He said, ‘I demand that you give your verdict on it.’ Shurayḥ said, ‘I think that his saying, “You are divorced,” is a divorce which has been expedited, and I think that his saying ‘irrevocably’ is an innovation and that one should consider this innovation,1465 and if he intended three [divorces], three it is, and if he intended a single [divorce], it is a single final [divorce], and he is a suitor [if he wishes].’”1466

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
