163. THE ʿIDDAH OF THE DIVORCED WOMAN AND THE WOMAN WHOSE HUSBAND HAS DIED

510. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib moved1484 Umm Kulthūm bint ʿAlī (the wife of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb ) while she was in her ʿiddah after the death of her husband ʿUmar , because she had been in the house of the amirate.’”1485 1486

511. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A woman whose husband has died observes the ʿiddah from the day when her husband died,1487 and a divorced woman from the day he divorced her.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

512. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that a woman whose husband has died does not come out of her house except for some unavoidable exigency.1488 She must only spend the night in her house, as ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd returned those who had come from Najaf to perform the Hajj during their ʿiddah.’”1489

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

513. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that a divorced woman may not leave her house whether for something true and worthy or for something false until her ʿiddah has expired, and that a woman whose husband has died may leave for unavoidable exigencies, but she must not spend the night anywhere but in her own house.”1490

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, because a divorced woman’s maintenance is obligatory upon her husband so she has no need to go out. As for a woman whose husband has died, she has no maintenance, and she must necessarily go out to seek Allah’s bounty. However, she may not spend the night anywhere but in her own house. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
