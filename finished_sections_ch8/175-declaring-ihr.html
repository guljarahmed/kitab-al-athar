175. DECLARING ẒIHĀR FROM A SLAVE WOMAN

553. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ẓihār is expedited against a slave woman if her husband declares ẓihār from her.”

Muḥammad said, “Ẓihar is expedited against her if her husband declares ẓihār from her. However, ẓihār is not expedited against her if her owner declares ẓihār from her because Allah, exalted is He, says, ‘Those who divorce their wives by equating them with their mothers,’ (Sūrat al-Mujādilah: 3) and the slave woman is not a wife against whom ẓihār can be expedited. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab, Mujāhid and ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on them.”

[If her first husband had divorced her twice already, then if they marry again and subsequently divorce, it is irrevocable and they may not remarry except after a new marriage to a new husband that is consummated. – Trans.]
