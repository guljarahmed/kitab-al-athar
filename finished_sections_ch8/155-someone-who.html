155. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES AS A JOKE

495. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Ibn Masʿūd said, ‘Marrying, whether in jest or in earnest, is all the same, just as divorce, whether in jest or in earnest,1453 is all the same.’”1454

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him: ‘There are four things which if they are done with serious intent it is serious intent and if they are done in jest it is [still] serious intent: divorce, marriage, taking a divorced woman back, and settings slaves free.’”

Footnotes

  1. jidd: earnestness, is the opposite of hazl: jest. Jest is that one intends by something other than what it was intended for without any relationship between them. Earnestness is that one intends by something that for which it was intended or that for which it is appropriate metaphorically. Thus someone who marries, divorces, takes his divorced wife back or sets slaves free and says, “I was only joking and did not really intend it in that sense,” then his statement is not to be reckoned with, and the divorce is expedited, marriage is contracted and is binding, and his act of taking his divorced wife back is ratified and his slave is really set free. ↩
  2. See the traditions about that in the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shaybah, vol.4, p.119. ↩
