149. SOMEONE WHO DIVORCES THREE TIMES OR DIVORCES ONCE BUT INTENDING THREE TIMES

486. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn Abī Ḥusayn from ʿAmr ibn Dīnār from ʿAṭā’ from Ibn ʿAbbās . He [ʿAṭā’] said, ‘A man came to him [Ibn ʿAbbās] and said, “I divorced my wife three times.” He said, “One of you goes and dirties himself with something putrid and then comes to us! Go away, for you have disobeyed your Lord,1433 and your wife is forbidden to you, and is not permissible to you until she has married a husband other than you.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, and of people in general, about which there is no disagreement.”1434

487. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a person who divorces once intending it as three times or divorces three times intending once, ‘If he says once then it is once, and his intention is nothing. If he says it three times it is three times, and his intention is nothing.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. By pronouncing three divorces all at one time. ↩
  2. The dominant majority of the ʿulamā’ have no disagreement about that, and whoever disagrees with them is an isolated individual who disagrees with the People of the Sunnah, and that is only attached to those who are people of innovation and those to whom one pays no attention because of their being isolated individuals separated from the main body. ↩
