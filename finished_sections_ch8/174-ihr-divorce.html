174. ẒIHĀR [DIVORCE BY THE MAN PRONOUNCING “TO ME YOU ARE AS MY MOTHER’S BACK”1554

545. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man declares ẓihār from four women, he has to make four expiations1555 [if he wishes to retract the ẓihār].’”1556

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

546. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who says to his wife, ‘To me you are as my mother’s back! To me you are as my mother’s back!’ meaning by that [repetition] to be hard on her, that he has to make two expiations. He said, ‘It is similar with two oaths. If he meant the first, then it is a single one.’”1557

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

547. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who declares ẓihār from his wife and later divorces her, and later remarries her after the expiry of the ʿiddah, that he said, ‘The ẓihār remains is as it was; he must not approach her until he has made expiation.’”1558

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

548. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man declares ẓihār from his wife, he may not approach her until he has set free a slave. If he does not find that, then he must fast two consecutive months. If he is not able to do that then he must feed sixty poor people. If he does not find [that with which to do that, still] he may not approach her until he has expiated with one of these expiations.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Īlā does not enter into that even if it becomes lengthy,1559 and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

549. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who declares ẓihār from his wife and then approaches her [sexually] before having made expiation, that he said, ‘He has done wrong1560 and must not repeat it.’”1561

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. He must not repeat it until he has made expiation, but only a single expiation is incumbent upon him, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

550. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Ẓihār does not take place when a man declares ẓihār from his wife except by his likening her to someone who is a maḥram.’”1562

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

551. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who declares ẓihār from his wife and later has sexual intercourse with her at night while fasting [in expiation of his ẓihār] that he said, ‘He must resume his fasting from the beginning again.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because Allah, exalted is He, says, ‘... must fast for two consecutive months before the two of them may touch one another again’ (Sūrat al-Mujādilah: 4). Therefore if he has sexual intercourse with her during the time when he is fasting, his fasting is invalidated and he must begin the two consecutive months again from the beginning. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

552. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who says to his wife, ‘If I approach you then you are to me as my mother’s back,’ that he said, ‘If he leaves her alone for four months she becomes finally divorced from him by īlā’. If he has sexual intercourse with her within the four months then the expiation for ẓihār is due from him.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
