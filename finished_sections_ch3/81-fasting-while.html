FASTING

81. FASTING WHILE TRAVELLING AND BREAKING THE FAST

284. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibrāhīm ibn Muslim narrated to us that a man of Banī Sawā’ah said, ‘I set out meaning to go to Makkah and met two parties among one of which was Ḥudhayfah and in the other of which was Abū Mūsā .’ He said, ‘I was one of Ḥudhayfah’s companions.’ He said, ‘So Ḥudhayfah and his companions fasted881 as did Abū Mūsā and his companions. Ḥudhayfah used to hasten to break the fast882 but he would delay the predawn meal, and Abū Mūsā would delay breaking the fast and would hasten to take the pre-dawn meal at an early time.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of Ḥudhayfah , and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

285. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and his companions broke the fast883 on a cloudy day thinking that the sun had set.’ He said,884 ‘Then the sun appeared, and so ʿUmar said, “We didn’t deliberately do something wrong.885 We will complete this day of ours, and make up by fasting a day in place of it.”’”886

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If a man breaks his fast while travelling during Ramaḍān, or a menstruating woman breaks her fast and then becomes pure during a part of the day, or a traveller arrives in his city during the daytime, then they complete what remains887 of their day without eating or drinking, and then make up by fasting a day in place of it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
