83. WHAT BREAKS THE FAST

290. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man washing out his mouth and nose [in wuḍū’] while fasting, such that in his haste the water reached his throat, ‘He should complete his fasting and then later make it up by fasting another day in place of it.’”896

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, if he had remembered that he was fasting.897 However, if he had forgotten that he was fasting, he does not have to make it up, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

291. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning vomit, ‘He does not have to make [the fast] up unless he did it deliberately,898 in which case he should complete his fast and then later make it up899 [by fasting another day in its place].’”900

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

292. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who has intercourse with his wife901 while fasting in the month of Ramaḍān, ‘He should complete his fast, make up by fasting a day in place of the one in which he broke his fast, and seek to draw closer to Allah,902 exalted is He, with whatever of good actions he is able. If the imam [the ruler] comes to know about it, he should give him a discretionary punishment (taʿzīr903).’”904

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we think that along with that he owes expiation:905 freeing a slave, but if he does not have the means906 then fasting two consecutive months, and if he is not able then he should feed sixty poor people giving each of them a half of a ṣāʿ907 of wheat, or a ṣāʿ of dates or barley, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
