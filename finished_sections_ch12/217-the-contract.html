217. THE CONTRACT WRITTEN WITH THE SLAVE TO PURCHASE HIS FREEDOM

677. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said concerning the contract written for a slave to purchase his freedom, ‘That much of him for which he has paid is set free and that much of him for which he has not paid is still enslaved.’”1803

678. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said concerning a slave who writes a contract to purchase his freedom, ‘If he pays the price of his slavehood then he is a debtor.’”1804

679. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Zayd ibn Thābit said concerning a slave who writes a contract to purchase his freedom, ‘He is a slave1805 as long as there remains anything of his contract unpaid.’”1806

Muḥammad said, “And the verdict of Zayd ibn Thābit is preferable to me and to Abū Ḥanīfah with respect to a slave who writes a contract to purchase his freedom than the verdict of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib and ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd . Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘It is the verdict of ʿĀ’ishah in that which has reached us.’ And we adhere to it.”

680. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib and ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd and Shurayḥ all used to say, ‘If a slave who has written a contract to purchase his freedom dies and leaves enough property [to pay off his contract], what remains of his contracted sum is taken from it1807 and paid to his owner and whatever is left over1808 goes to the heirs of the slave.’”1809

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

681. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning the saying of Allah, exalted is He, ‘...write it [the contract to purchase their freedom] for them if you know of good in them,’ (Sūrat an-Nur: 33) and that he said, ‘If you know they can pay.’”

682. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man writes a contract with two slaves of his to purchase their freedom for a sum of a thousand dirhams in a single contract and he made its instalments a single one,’ then he said, ‘If they both pay, they are both free. If they are both incapable, they are both returned to slavery.’ Ibrāhīm said, ‘They are not freed until they pay all of the thousand.’”1810

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

683. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who writes a contract with two slaves of his to purchase their freedom for a thousand dirhams and then one of them dies, that if he had said, ‘If you both pay the thousand, you are free, but if not, you are slaves,’ and one of them dies, then the one who is still alive takes on the entire thousand. If he had written a contract with them for a thousand without stipulating, he may not take anything but the proportionate share: half of the first,1811 and the price of the remaining one.”1812

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this in all of the hadith. If he did not stipulate anything and one of the two dies, the contract is divided between them in proportion to their prices, and the share of the price of the deceased is removed from the contract, and the other one who is still alive is obligated to pay [what is proportionate to] his price, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
