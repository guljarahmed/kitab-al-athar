212. THE MERIT OF SETTING SLAVES FREE

664. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿImrān ibn ʿUmayr from his father that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd set a slave of his free and said to him, ‘Even though your property1786 belongs to me, I will leave it to you.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If someone sets a slave free or writes a contract with him for him to purchase his own freedom, then his property belongs to his owner, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

665. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever frees a soul,1787 then for every limb of his [the slave’s] Allah will free a limb of his [the owner’s] from the Fire,1788 so much so that if it is a man it is recommended that he free a man because of the perfection of his limbs, and that a woman free a woman because of the perfection of her limbs.’”

Footnotes

  1. The ascription of ownership to the slave is not as ownership but taking into account what is in his hands. ↩
  2. nasmah is a spirit (rūḥ) meaning a being possessing a spirit. ↩
  3. Abū Hurayrah narrated it as a marfūʿ hadith in the collection of al-Bukhārī under “expiations of oaths” in the chapter “on the saying of Allah, exalted is He, ‘or free a slave’” (Sūrat al-Mā’idah:89), and Muslim under “freeing slaves” in the chapter on “the merit of freeing slaves.” ↩
