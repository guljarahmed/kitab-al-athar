207. WHAT TESTIMONY OF WOMEN IS VALID OR INVALID

646. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Testimony of women along with men is valid in everything apart from ḥadd punishments.’”1760 1761

Muḥammad said, “And we say apart from ḥadd punishments and retaliation.”

647. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm that he used to consider women’s testimony valid on whether a child was born alive or not.”1762

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if she is a Muslim woman of acceptable testimony. Abū Ḥanīfah used to say, ‘Only the testimony of two men or one man and two women is accepted with respect to whether a child was born alive or not. As for birth from a wife then the testimony of a woman is accepted if she is a Muslim woman of acceptable testimony.’ But as for us, then these two are the same.”

Footnotes

  1. Because in women’s testimony there is the doubtful circumstance of badaliyyah for the discharge of women’s testimony along with men’s testimony, so that it is not accepted in those matters which are averted because of dubious circumstances. ↩
  2. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.5, p.528. ↩
  3. The sound of the baby’s voice at birth [among whose implications are matters of inheritance etc. – Trans.] ↩
