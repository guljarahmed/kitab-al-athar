205. THE TESTIMONY OF SOMEONE WHO HAS RECEIVED A ḤADD PUNISHMENT

640. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm concerning a Christian who accuses a Muslim woman of unsubstantiated sexual impropriety and is then flogged as a punishment, but later accepts Islam, that his testimony is valid.’”1750 1751

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, because he was not flogged as a punishment in Islam.”

641. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “If someone who slanders others by accusing them of unsubstantiated sexual impropriety is flogged his testimony is never valid,” and he spoke about the words of Allah, exalted is He, “...except for those who after that make tawbah and put things right,” (Sūrat an-Nūr: 5) and said, “The appellation of being a deviator1752 is lifted from him, but his testimony is never again valid.”’”1753

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

642. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham narrated to us from ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī who said, “I regard the testimony of someone who accuses of others of unsubstantiated sexual impropriety as being valid1754 if he has turned in tawbah.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this.”

643. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us from ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī from Shurayḥ who said that a man from Banī Asad whose hand was amputated [for theft] had come to him and asked, “Is my testimony acceptable?” – and he was one of their best people – and he said, “Yes, I regard you as fitting for that.”’”1755

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Every person who has had a ḥadd punishment whether as a thief or a fornicator or anything else, if they turn in tawbah their testimony is accepted, except for the person who has had the punishment for accusing others of unsubstantiated sexual impropriety in particular because of Allah’s words, exalted is He, ‘and never again accept them as witnesses,’ (Sūrat an-Nūr: 4).”
