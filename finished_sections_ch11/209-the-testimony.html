209. THE TESTIMONY OF SMALL CHILDREN

650. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from Shurayḥ who said, ‘Hishām wrote to Ibn Hubayrah to ask him about five things: testimony of small children, compensatory payments for injuries caused by men and women to each other, compensatory payments for fingers, [the compensatory payment for] an animal’s eye, and a man who claims the paternity of a child of his on his deathbed. He wrote back to him, “Small children’s testimony on behalf of each other is valid if they are all agreed.1764 The compensatory payments for injuries to men and women are the same in the cases of teeth and wounds that lay bare the bone but in all other cases they are different. The compensatory payments for fingers and toes are the same. For the eye of an animal there is due a quarter of its value. As for a man claiming the paternity of his child, then the time of death is when he is in the most truthful condition.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this except in two respects: the testimony of small children in our view is invalid whether they agree or differ because Allah, exalted is He, says in His Book, ‘Call two upright men from among yourselves1765 as witnesses’ (Sūrat aṭ-Ṭalāq: 2), and ‘Two men among you should act as witnesses. But if there are not two men, then a man and two women with whom you are satisfied as witnesses’ (Sūrat al-Baqarah: 281), and small children are not among those described as upright and they are not among those witnesses with whom one is satisfied. The other matter is that injuries to women are [compensated] at the rate of a half of those of men in [injuries] to teeth and wounds which lay bare the bone and other things, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

651. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “There are four things with respect to which the testimony of women is not valid: adultery and fornication, accusations of sexual impropriety, drinking wine, and intoxicants.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. This was Mālik’s verdict. ↩
  2. Literally “from among your men (rijāl)” and the child is neither a man nor a woman and so his testimony is not accepted. ↩
