TESTIMONY

204. TESTIMONY OF PEOPLE OF THE DHIMMAH1748 AGAINST MUSLIMS

639. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke concerning His words, exalted is He, ‘...when one of you is near to death and makes a will, two just men from among you should act as witnesses; or, ... two men who are strangers to you,...’ up until the end of it (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 106). He said, ‘It is abrogated.’”1749

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah. He only means by this, testimony about a will on a journey when death occurs when there are no Muslims, in which case the testimony of the people of the dhimmah is valid in a Muslim’s will. However, that is abrogated, so only Muslims are valid [witnesses] in a Muslim’s will or in anything else of their affairs, and Allah knows best.”

Footnotes

  1. ahl adh-dhimmah are Jews and Christians living under Muslim governance by a treaty with the Muslims. – Trans. ↩
