293. MAINTENANCE OF A FOUNDLING

890. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whatever you spend on a foundling2199 by which you intend Allah, he owes nothing of it to you. What you spend on him intending that he should owe it to you, he owes it to you.’”

Muḥammad said, “This is all voluntary, and it is not referred back2200 to the foundling in any way, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Lexically laqīṭ is whatever is picked up from the ground, and then later it has been used in the main for abandoned children because they are likely to be picked up accidentally. ↩
  2. Unless the qāḍī orders that in which case it is referred back to the foundling whenever that is easy. ↩
