284. JOINING TIES OF KINSHIP AND TREATING ONE’S PARENTS WELL

873. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Nāṣiḥ from Yaḥyā ibn Abī Kathīr al-Yamānī from Abū Salamah from Abū Hurayrah that the Prophet said, ‘There is no action in which Allah is obeyed that has a more magnificent reward than joining ties of kinship. There is no action in which Allah is disobeyed that has a swifter punishment2173 than fornication and adultery.2174 A false oath leaves homes barren2175’”

874. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Muḥammad ibn Sūqah, ‘A man2176 came to the Prophet and said, “I have come to you to wage jihad alongside you and I have left my parents weeping.” He said, “Go and make them laugh just as you have made them weep.”’”2177

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. It is not right without the permission of his parents, as long as the Muslims are not pressed by great need, but when they are under great pressure and in great need of it then there is no harm.2178 That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  3. Wasteland on which there is nothing, meaning that the one who swears a false oath will become impoverished and what provision is in his house will depart. ↩
  4. He was Jāhimah ibn al-ʿAbbās. ↩
  5. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on pp.199-200. ↩
  6. Meaning, [that he go on jihad] without his parents’ permission. ↩
