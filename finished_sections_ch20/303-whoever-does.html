303. WHOEVER DOES AN ACTION WHICH HE CONCEALS, ALLAH WILL CLOTHE HIM IN ITS OUTER WRAP; AND SHOW MERCY TO THE TWO WEAK BEINGS: WOMEN AND CHILDREN

912. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Conceal whatever you wish and make public whatever you wish. Any slave who conceals something, Allah, exalted is He, will clothe him in its outer wrap.’”2244

913. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘A Shaykh of ours narrated to us, ascribing it directly to the Prophet as a marfūʿ hadith, that he said, “Show mercy to the two weak beings: women and children.”’”

Footnotes

  1. The hadith has two possible senses: first, in the case where the outer robe refers to the action’s outer robe: if someone does a good action in secret, Allah will robe him in that action’s noble outer robe, but if someone does a wrong action in secret, Allah will robe him in that action’s ignoble outer robe. This sense is that of the hadith transmitted by aṭ-Ṭabarānī in al-Kabīr (172): “Whenever a slave conceals a secret, Allah will publicly dress him in its robe which if it (the secret) is good then it will be good, but which if it (the secret) is evil, then it will be evil.” – Trans. ↩
