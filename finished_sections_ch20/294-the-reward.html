294. THE REWARD FOR A RUNAWAY SLAVE

891. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Saʿīd ibn al-Marzubān from Abū ʿUmar or Ibn ʿUmar2201 (Muḥammad was unsure) that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd made the reward for [capturing] a runaway slave forty dirhams when someone caught him outside the city.”2202

892. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibn Abī Ribāḥ also narrated to us from ʿAbdullāh [Ibn Masʿūd] the same as that concerning the reward for capturing a runaway slave.’”2203

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this when the place in which he captured him is three days journey away, in which case the reward is forty. If it is less than that, he is given a lesser reward according to the measure of his travel.2204 That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.166. ↩
  3. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.166. ↩
  4. This is legal preference (istiḥsān) but analogical deduction gives the result that he gets nothing unless it has been stipulated, and that is the verdict of ash-Shāfiʿī , but our position is that the Companions agreed upon the obligatory nature of the basic reward except that some of them regarded forty as obligatory and some of them less than that. So in order to reconcile these, we regard forty as obligatory for going so far as to be a traveller and the lesser amount for going a lesser distance than would make one a traveller. ↩
