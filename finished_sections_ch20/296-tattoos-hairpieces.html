296. TATTOOS,2209 HAIRPIECES, REMOVING FACIAL HAIR, AND ONE WHO RENDERS ḤALĀL

895. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A woman who lengthens her hair with a hair-piece and someone who does it for her, a man who makes a divorced woman ḥalāl for her original husband who divorced her and the man for whom that is done,2210 and a female tattooist and the woman who asks her to tattoo her, all these are accursed.’”2211

Muḥammad said, “As for ‘a woman who lengthens her hair with a hair-piece’ that is the woman who ties hair to her own hair, and that is abhorent in our view, but there is no harm if it is done with wool.2212 As for ‘a man who renders the divorced woman ḥalāl for her original husband who divorced her and the man for whom that is done,’ that is when a man divorces his wife three times and later asks another man to marry her [and then divorce her] in order to make her ḥalāl for him again. This is neither right for the man who asks for it or the man who is asked to do it. As for the tattooist it is a woman who tattoos the palms and the face, which ought not to be done.”

896. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us from Umm Thawr that Ibn ʿAbbās said, “There is no harm in tying up the hair of the head if it is done with wool [and not with human hair].”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
