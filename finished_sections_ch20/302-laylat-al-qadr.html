302. LAYLAT AL-QADR – THE NIGHT OF THE DECREE

911. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿĀṣim ibn Abī’n-Najūd narrated to us from Zirr ibn Ḥubaysh that Ubayy ibn Kaʿb said, “Laylat al-Qadr is the night of the twenty-seventh,2240 and that2241 is because the sun rises on the morning of that day without rays as if it were a copper wash-basin rotating and coming and going.”’”2242 2243

Footnotes

  1. Most probably. ↩
  2. Its sign. ↩
  3. Indicating the appearance of its motion upon rising, because it is seen to have an imagined motion because of its closeness to the horizon, contrary to when it rises and is high. ↩
  4. Muslim narrated in the book on “fasting” in the chapter on “the merit of Laylat al-Qadr.” ↩
