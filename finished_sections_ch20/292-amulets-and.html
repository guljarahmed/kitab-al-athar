292. AMULETS AND INCANTATIONS2193 USED AS PROTECTION AGAINST THE [EVIL] EYE, AND CAUTERISATION

888. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, “Nāfiʿ informed us that Ibn ʿUmar utilised cauterisation,2194 trimmed his beard2195 and used amulets and incantations2196 as treatments for fever.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and there is no harm in it. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

889. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿUbaydullāh ibn Abī Ziyād narrated to us from Abū Najīḥ from ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar that Asmā’ bint ʿUmays came to the Prophet , and she had a son from Abū Bakr and a son from Jaʿfar and she said, “Messenger of Allah, I fear ‘the eye’ for your two nephews;2197 should I use amulets and incantations to protect them?” He said, “Yes, for if anything could outstrip the Decree ‘the eye’ would outstrip it.”’”2198

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if it consists of dhikr of Allah or is from the Book of Allah. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

The permissibility of using charms and spells has been narrated in some hadith but in some others there is narrated its prohibition. The way that these can be reconciled is that using charms and spells in languages other than Arabic and with means other than the names of Allah and His attributes and His speech from His revealed books is deplored as is to believe that charms and spells are inevitably effective so that one relies on them. It is not disapproved to use anything else such as to seek refuge by means of the Qur’ān and the names of Allah, exalted is He, and those charms that are transmitted. It is for that reason that he said to the man who charmed by means of the Qur’ān and took a wage for it, “You took [a wage] for a true charm.” [Note that Muḥammad ibn ʿAbd al-Wahhāb and his followers confused this matter by choosing only hadith on forbidden talismans. – Trans.] ↩
