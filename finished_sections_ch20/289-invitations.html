289. INVITATIONS

880. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Muḥammad ibn Qays narrated to us that Abū’l-ʿAwjā’ al-ʿIshār was a friend of Masrūq and he [Abū’l-ʿAwjā’ al-ʿIshār] would invite [Masrūq] and [Masrūq] would eat of his food and drink of his drink without asking him [about the source of the food or whether it was ḥalāl or ḥarām].’”2187

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. There is no harm in it as long as one does not know of a specific wrong.2188 That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

881. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whenever you go to see a man, eat of his food and drink of his drink and do not ask about it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this as long as one does not fall into doubt about it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

882. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘It used to be said, “When you enter a Muslim man’s house, eat of his food and drink of his drink and do not ask2189 about anything.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this as long as one does not fall into doubt about it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

883. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿĀṣim ibn Kulayb that one of the men of the Companions of Muḥammad said, ‘One of the men of the Companions of Muḥammad made food and invited him, so the Prophet stood and we stood up with him. When the food was set, he ate some and we ate with him. The Prophet took a morsel and chewed it in his mouth for a long time, unable to eat it. So he took it out of his mouth and withheld himself from the food. The Prophet called for the owner of the food and said, “Tell me about this meat of yours; where is it from?” He said, “Messenger of Allah, it is a ewe which belonged to a friend of ours, but we did not have that with which to buy it from him. We hurried and slaughtered it and made it for you before its owner could come and we could give him its price.” The Prophet told him to remove the food and feed the prisoners with it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If the meat had been in its original state, the Prophet would not have ordered him to feed the prisoners with it, but he saw that it had gone from the ownership of the original person. He abhored eating it because [the host] had not stood surety for its price to the ewe’s owner from whom it was taken. Whoever is responsible for something and it becomes his through wrongful expropriation, we prefer that he give it away as ṣadaqah and not eat it, and equally its profit. Prisoners in our view mean needy inhabitants of prisons. All of this is analogically derived from the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”
