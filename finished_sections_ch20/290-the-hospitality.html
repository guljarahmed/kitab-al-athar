290. THE HOSPITALITY OF GOVERNORS

884. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ibrāhīm and Dharr al-Hamdānī went to Zuhayr ibn ʿAbdullāh al-Azdī, who was the governor of Ḥulwān, and asked him for the hospitality2190 due them, and he gave them their hospitality.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this as long as he does not know of anything in itself ḥarām,2191 and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

885. Muḥammad said, “Al-ʿAlā’ ibn Zuhayr informed us, ‘I saw Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī come to my father,2192 while he was the governor of Ḥulwān, and ask him for his hospitality, and he gave it to him.’”

886. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no harm in the hospitality of governors.’ He [Ḥammād] said, ‘I asked, “If it is the tithe of the tenth or the like?” He said, “If what he gives you is not something he specifically expropriated from a Muslim or someone [of the People of the Book] under a treaty, accept it.”’”
