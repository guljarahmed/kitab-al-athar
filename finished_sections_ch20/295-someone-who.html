295. SOMEONE WHO FINDS LOST PROPERTY WHICH HE MAKES KNOWN

893. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Isḥāq informed us from a man2205 that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, “One makes a piece of lost property known2206 for a year.2207 Then if its owner comes [he has the right to it], but if not one should give it away as ṣadaqah, or sell it and give its price away as ṣadaqah, except that its owner has the choice: if he wishes he can make him liable for it, and if he wishes he can abandon it.”’”2208

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

894. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning lost property, ‘I prefer that one give it away as ṣadaqah rather than eating it, but if you are in need and eat it, there is no harm in it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Whose name was ʿĀṣim ibn Ḍamrah as is narrated by al-Bayhaqī. ↩
  2. The way to make it known is to announce it at the place in which he found it and in the marketplaces, the streets and the mosques. ↩
  3. Making no distinction between a great deal or a little. Some say that if it is ten dirhams or more it must be made known for a year, but if less then just for a few days. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.168. ↩
