MISCELLANEOUS

282. THE MERITS OF THE COMPANIONS, AND THOSE OF THE COMPANIONS OF THE PROPHET WHO USED TO CONFER WITH EACH OTHER ON FIQH

866. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham that ash-Shaʿbī said, ‘There were six of the Companions of Muḥammad who would confer with each other on fiqh: ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, Ubayy [ibn Kaʿb], Abū Mūsā [al-Ashʿarī] on one hand, and ʿUmar [ibn al-Khaṭṭāb], Zayd [ibn Thābit], and Ibn Masʿūd .’”2164

867. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar touched the Prophet when he was feverish. ʿUmar said, ‘Does it affect you thus, even though you are the Messenger of Allah ?’ He said, ‘When [fever] affects me, it does so very severely. The most severe of this community in affliction is their Prophet, and then the best, and then the best. It was thus with the prophets and the communities before you.’”2165

868. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAlī ibn al-Aqmar who said, ‘ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb used to feed people in Madīnah. He would go round and about among them with a staff in his hand. He passed a man eating with his left hand. He said, “Slave of Allah, eat with your right hand.” He answered, “Slave of Allah, it is otherwise occupied.” He went on his way, but later passed by him and he was again eating with his left hand. So he said, “Slave of Allah, eat with your right hand,” and he answered three times, “Slave of Allah, it is otherwise occupied.” He asked, “What is occupying it?” He said, “It was injured on the day of Mūtah.” ʿUmar sat with him weeping and began to say to him, “Who helps you in doing wuḍū’? Who washes your hair and your clothes? Who does such-and-such and such-and-such?” He called for a servant for him, and ordered a she-camel as a mount for him and food, and things that would be of use to him and appropriate for him, to such an extent that the Companions of Muḥammad raised their voices supplicating Allah for ʿUmar because of his kindness and compassion for the man which they had seen, and his concern for the affairs of the Muslims.’”2166

869. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Jaʿfar Muḥammad ibn ʿAlī narrated to us, “ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib came to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb when he had been stabbed and said, ‘May Allah be merciful to you. There is no one on the earth with whose record2167 I would rather meet Allah than yours.’”’”2168
