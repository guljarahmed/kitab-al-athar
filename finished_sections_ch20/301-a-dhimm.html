301. A DHIMMĪ2235 GREETING A MUSLIM WHO RETURNS THE GREETING

910. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham narrated to us that Ibn Masʿūd kept the company of a man from among the people of the dhimmah, and that when he intended to leave him, [the dhimmī] said, “As-salāmu ʿalayka,” and [ʿAbdullāh] said, “Wa ʿalayka as-salām.”’”2236

Muḥammad said, “We abhor that a Muslim should initiate greeting2237 a mushrik with the [Sharīʿah] greeting of peace, but there is no harm in returning his greeting.2238 2239 That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. A dhimmī is a person of the People of the Book under the contract of the dhimmah, which grants them protection and freedom of worship and exemption from military service in return for payment of jizyah. – Trans. ↩
  2. The like of it is narrated of other Companions of the Prophet ; see the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shaybah, vol.5, pp251-2. ↩
  3. Because initiating the greeting is to show honour and it is not permitted to show honour to them. ↩
  4. Meaning with the wording “wa ʿalaykum” (and upon you (plural)) as is narrated in the hadith. ↩
  5. By returning his greeting saying “wa ʿalaykum” (and upon you (singular)) as narrated in hadith. ↩
