283. TRUTHFULNESS, LYING, BACKBITING AND SLANDER

870. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Maʿn ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān narrated to us that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, “I have not lied since I accepted Islam except for one single lie.” Someone asked, “What was that, Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān?” He said, “I used to saddle the camel for the Messenger of Allah , but someone brought him a man from Ṭā’if to saddle up for him who2169 asked, ‘Who used to saddle up for the Messenger of Allah ?’ They said, ‘Ibn Umm ʿAbd.’2170 So he came to me and asked me, ‘Which mount is preferable to the Messenger of Allah ?’ and I said, ‘The Makkan Ṭā’ifan one.’2171 So he saddled it for the Messenger of Allah who mounted it, and it was one of the most hateful mounts to the Messenger of Allah , and so he said, ‘Who saddled up this one?’ They said, ‘The Ṭā’ifī man.’ The Messenger of Allah said, ‘Tell Ibn Umm ʿAbd to saddle up for us.’ So [the position of saddling] the mount was returned to me.”’”

871. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ibrāhīm ibn Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir from his father from Masrūq that when he narrated from ʿĀ’ishah he would say, ‘The Ṣiddīqah [the utterly truthful one], who was the beloved of the Beloved of Allah and the daughter of the Ṣiddīq [Abū Bakr] narrated to us.’”

872. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm, ‘If you say about a man that which is genuinely in him, it is backbiting, but if you say that which is not in him, you have slandered him.’”2172

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. The man from Ṭā’if. ↩
  2. Ibn Umm ʿAbd is the kunyah of ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd . ↩
  3. Saddling as it was done in Makkah and Ṭā’if, and the Messenger of Allah only loved the Madīnan mount because of his love of Madīnah’s people. ↩
  4. It is a marfūʿ hadith which Abū Hurayrah narrated and which at-Tirmidhī published under “complete goodness (birr) and joining [ties of kinship],” in the chapter on “that which has been narrated on backbiting.” ↩
