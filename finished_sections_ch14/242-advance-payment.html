242. ADVANCE PAYMENT FOR ANIMALS

744. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm who said, “ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd paid to Zayd ibn Khuwaylidah al-Bakrī1931 some money as a profit-sharing loan (muḍārabah) and Zayd paid an advance payment to ʿItrīs ibn ʿUrqūb ash-Shaybānī for some young female camels. When the term fell due, he took1932 some and some remained. That caused problems for ʿItrīs and then it reached him that the money belonged to ʿAbdullāh and he came to him asking him to help him.1933 ʿAbdullāh asked, ‘Did Zayd do that?’ He said, ‘Yes.’ So he sent for him and asked him. ʿAbdullāh said to him, ‘Return what [camels] you took and take your capital sum and do not give our money in advance payments for anything in the nature of animals.’”’”1934

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. Advance payment is not permissible for anything in the nature of animals, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Al-Bakrī is a mistake for al-Yashkurī which is the correct name as is mentioned by al-Bukhārī in his Tārīkh. ↩
  2. Zayd took some of what he had paid in advance for, i.e., the young female camels, from ʿItrīs. ↩
  3. Asking him to be kind to him and to grant him a delay. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on pp.186-7. ↩
