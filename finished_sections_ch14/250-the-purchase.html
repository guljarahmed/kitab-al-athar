250. THE PURCHASE OF GOLD AND SILVER SET IN SOMETHING OF GOOD APPEARANCE (SIBR), AND JEWELS

757. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a ring is silver and there is a gem in it, then buy it for whatever you wish; a little if you wish or a lot if you wish.’”

“And we do not adhere to this, and we do not permit the sale until it is known that the price is more than the silver in the ring and that the extra price is for the gem, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

758. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Walīd ibn Sarīʿ narrated to us from Anas ibn Mālik who said, “A Khusrāwī1964 vessel of silver of very strong workmanship was sent to ʿUmar and he told the messenger to sell it. The messenger returned and said, ‘I was given more than its value in silver.’ ʿUmar said, ‘No, because the extra amount is usury.’”’”

“And we adhere to this, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”1965

Footnotes

  1. So named for Khusraw Shāh, one of the kings of Persia. – Trans. ↩
