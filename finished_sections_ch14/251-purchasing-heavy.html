251. PURCHASING HEAVY DIRHAMS WITH LIGHT ONES, AND USURY

759. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Marzūq narrated to us from Abū Jabalah1966 from Ibn ʿUmar and he [Abū Jabalah] said, “I said to him [Ibn ʿUmar], ‘We are coming to a land with weighty silver in piles and we have light silver which is in much demand; may we sell our silver for theirs?’ He said, ‘No, but sell your silver for dinars and then buy their silver, and do not part from your customer (ṣāḥib) for even a span until you take it from him in full. If he climbs up1967 above the house, then climb there with him, and if he jumps, jump with him.’”’”1968

“We adhere to this and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

760. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAṭiyyah al-ʿAwfī narrated to us from Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī from the Prophet that he said, “Gold for gold, like for like and any excess is usury. Silver for silver, like for like, and any excess is usury. Wheat for wheat, like for like and any excess is usury. Barley for barley, like for like and any excess is usury. Dates for dates, like for like and any excess is usury. Salt for salt, like for like, and any excess is usury.”’”1969

“We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Abū Jabalah is a mistake for Jabalah ibn Suḥaym at-Taymī (Iʿlā’ as-sunan). ↩
  2. Showing that exchange before separating in monetary exchanges is a right, even if standing without parting does not prevent the continuance of the contract. ↩
  3. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on pp.184-5. ↩
  4. Muslim narrated it under “date-crop sharing” in the chapter on “selling food, like for like.” ↩
