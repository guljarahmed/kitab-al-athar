262. WHOEVER DOES SOMETHING OUTSIDE OF HIS FORECOURT2023 IS RESPONSIBLE FOR IT

787. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who puts a rock in his wall, and by it concealing a load-bearing camel2024 or he makes a lavatory2025 empty on to a pathway, ‘If what you mention happens, he is responsible for everything, because he produced something outside of his property, and without possessing its forecourt,2026 and so he is responsible for what happens.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. finā’ is the forecourt, the open space in front of a house, and sometimes to the sides of a house that belong to the house. ↩
  2. Those beasts on which people carry burdens whether or not they have loads or not such as riding beasts. ↩
  3. kanīf is a lavatory. This tradition shows that killing through some causative means necessarily requires liability unless he stipulates about it that it should be as an offence against the law such as digging a well on someone else’s property. ↩
  4. It is samā’ in the original and the samā’ of anything is the highest part of it, but it is probably finā’ which is the open space in front of a house. ↩
