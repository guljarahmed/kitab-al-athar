232. THE CHOICE OF [THE FORM OF] EXPIATION, AND SOMEONE WHO DEDICATES HIS WEALTH TO THE VERY POOR

721. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Wherever in the Qur’ān there is His saying, “or,” one has a choice; one may do whichever of it one wishes,’ meaning in expiation.”1881

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. An example of it is His words, exalted is He, respecting expiation of an oath, ‘Feed ten poor people with the average amount you feed your family, or clothe them, or free a slave,’ (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 89) so that whatever one uses in expiation of his oath it is valid, and it is not valid to fast if one has any of these things, because Allah, exalted is He, says, ‘Anyone without the means to do so should fast three days’ (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 89) without giving the choice of fasting as He gave the choice in the other matters. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

722. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man dedicates his wealth1882 to the very poor as an act of ṣadaqah, he must examine1883 what is sufficient for him1884 and for his dependants and retain that, giving the excess as ṣadaqah. If it is easy he gives in ṣadaqah the same as what he has retained.’”1885

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
