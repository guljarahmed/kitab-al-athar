OATHS AND VOWS

228. OATHS1862 AND THEIR EXPIATIONS

709. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘”I swear (uqsim),” “I swear by Allah,” “I witness,”1863 “I witness by Allah,” “I swear (aḥlif),” “I swear by Allah,” “Allah’s contract (ʿahd)1864 is binding on me,” “Allah’s covenant (dhimmah) is binding on me,” “the vow (nadhr) is binding on me,”1865 “the vow to Allah is binding on me,” “he is a Jew,”1866 “he is a Christian,” “he is a Zoroastrian,” and “he is quit of Islam,” all of these are oaths which are to be expiated when they are retracted.’”1867

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

710. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning the expiation of an oath [that it is], ‘To feed ten destitute people feeding each with half a ṣāʿ of wheat, or to clothe (with a robe) or to free a slave, and whoever does not have [any of the above] should fast three days.’”1868

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. The three days must be consecutive and it is not valid to separate them, because in the recitation of Ibn Masʿūd it is, ‘... fast three consecutive days,’ (Sūrat al-Mā’idah: 89) and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

711. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you intend to feed [someone] for the expiation of an oath, it must be breakfast1869 and dinner.’”1870

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
