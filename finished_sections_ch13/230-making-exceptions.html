230. MAKING EXCEPTIONS IN OATHS

713. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Qāsim ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān from his father that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘Whoever swears an oath and says, “Inshā’Allāh,” has made an exception.’”1875

714. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever swears an oath and says, “Inshā’Allāh,” has got out of his oath.’”

715. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿUbaydullāh narrated to us from Saʿīd ibn Jamīl from Ibn ʿUmar who said, “Whoever swears an oath and says, ‘Inshā’Allāh,’ is not regarded as having broken his oath.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah concerning all oaths when one’s saying ‘inshā’Allāh’ is connected to one’s statement before or after it.”

716. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If making an exception is connected [to one’s oath, it is valid], but if not, it is nothing.’”

Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm, ‘If he moves his lips in [making] an exception, then he has made an exception [even if he does not say it aloud].”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

718. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning a man who said to his wife, ‘You are divorced, inshā’Allāh,’ that he said, ‘It is nothing and her divorce does not take place.’”1876

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if his making an exception is connected at the beginning or at the end to his oath, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Meaning that the exception he makes is valid and the oath does not take effect [such that he would have to expiate it if he did not fulfil it]. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.138. ↩
