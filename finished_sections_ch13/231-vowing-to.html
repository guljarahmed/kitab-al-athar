231. VOWING TO BE DISOBEDIENT

719. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Muḥammad ibn az-Zubayr narrated to us from al-Ḥasan from ʿImrān ibn al-Ḥuṣayn that the Prophet said, “One must not vow acts of disobedience,1877 and the expiation of it is the expiation of an oath.”’”1878

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

720. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘I heard ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī saying, “One must not vow acts of disobedience. Whoever swears an oath to do an act of disobedience must cancel it and he is not due any expiation.”’”1879

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this but we adhere to the first hadith. An example of that is a man who swears an oath not to talk to his father or mother or that he will not perform the Hajj or pay zakāh (taṣaddaqa)1880 and similar types of good action, and so he must do the thing that he swore he would not do and he must make expiation for his oath. Do you not see that Allah, blessed is He and exalted, regards ẓihār as ‘wrong and a slanderous lie,’ (Sūrat al-Mujādilah: 2), and appointed an expiation for it? It is the same here. This is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. It does not mean that it does not originally take effect since that does not accord with his statement and his expiation, but on the contrary it means that one does not fulfil it as is clearly stated in some narrations, because in them there is, “There is no fulfilment of a vow of disobedience.” ↩
  2. Muslim narrated it in the book of “vows” in the chapter that “one does not fulfil a vow to disobey Allah,” and Abū Dāwūd under “oaths and vows” in the chapter on “vows concerning that which the son of Ādam does not possess.” ↩
  3. The verdict of ash-Shaʿbī is contrary to hadith and is not acted upon. ↩
  4. taṣaddaqa I have here read to refer to the obligatory zakāh, since nothing else is obligatorily due from a Muslim than it, and it would not technically be an act of disobedience to swear not to give optional ṣadaqah. In the context of the other oaths it is clearly an oath to abstain from an obligation that is intended here, even though lexically to the modern Muslim, ṣadaqah almost invariably means optional generosity to the poor. However, in the language of fiqh and Qur’ān it regularly refers to the zakāh. – Trans. ↩
