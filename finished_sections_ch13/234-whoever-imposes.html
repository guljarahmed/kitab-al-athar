234. WHOEVER IMPOSES ON HIMSELF TO SACRIFICE HIS SON OR TO SACRIFICE HIMSELF

724. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that a man who imposes on himself1888 to sacrifice his son owes a hundred camels1889 which he must sacrifice.”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but we adhere to the verdict of Ibn ʿAbbās and Masrūq ibn al-Ajdaʿ.”

725. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Simāk ibn Ḥarb narrated to us from Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir who said, “A man came to Ibn ʿAbbās and said, ‘I have dedicated1890 my son as a sacrificial offering’ (i.e., ‘I have vowed to sacrifice my son’), while Masrūq ibn al-Ajdaʿ was sitting in the mosque, and so Ibn ʿAbbās said to him, ‘Go to that Shaykh and ask him and then come back and tell me what he says.’ So he went to him and asked him. Masrūq said, ‘If the person is a mu’min he hastens to the Garden and if a kāfir you hasten him to the Fire; sacrifice a ram and that will suffice you.’ He went to Ibn ʿAbbās and told him what Masrūq had said and he said, ‘And I order you to do what Masrūq ordered you to do.’”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

726. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Simāk ibn Ḥarb narrated to us from Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir that Ibn ʿAbbās said concerning a man who imposes on himself to sacrifice himself,1891 “A ram or a ewe or goat [which he sacrifices].”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this.”

Footnotes

  3. Meaning that his vow is valid but he is required to sacrifice a hady offering, which is to sacrifice a camel or a sheep, and that is only done during the days of tashrīq (the drying of the meat) whether or not he is in the ḥaram. This is legal preference (istiḥsān) and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad. Analogical deduction gives that his vow is not valid and that is the verdict of Abū Yūsuf, Zufar and ash-Shāfiʿī, may Allah have mercy on them (al-Badā’iʿ). ↩
  4. naṣībahu: his portion. It is thus in the original and it is possible that it should be nafsahu (himself) instead of naṣībahu. ↩
