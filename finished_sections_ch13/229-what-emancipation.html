229. WHAT EMANCIPATION [OF SLAVES] SUFFICES IN EXPIATION OF AN OATH

712. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘It does not suffice1871 for any expiation [to set free] a slave with whom a contract has been written to purchase his freedom (mukātab),1872 nor an umm walad, nor a slave who is to be set free as a bequest upon the death of his owner (mudabbar). But [setting free] an infant child and a kāfir1873 suffice in the case of [expiation for] ẓihār [divorce].’”1874

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this except in one respect: if a [mukātab] slave who has contracted to purchase his freedom has not paid anything towards purchasing his freedom before his master sets him free, then that is valid, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Because of their earning their freedom. ↩
  2. If he has not paid anything it is valid, because his condition of slavehood still exists in every respect. ↩
  3. According to us a kāfir is valid for liberation contrary to ash-Shāfiʿī, and we say that the noun “slave” (raqabah) is used [in the Qur’ān] for both Muslims and kuffār. ↩
  4. See the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shaybah, vol.3, pp.78-80. ↩
