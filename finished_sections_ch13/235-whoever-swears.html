235. WHOEVER SWEARS AN OATH WHEN HE HAS BEEN WRONGED

727. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘In the case where a man has been wronged and he is asked to swear an oath, the oath is according to what he intended but which the one requiring him to swear the oath has not intended.1892 In the case where he is the wrongdoer, the oath is according to the intention of the one who requires him to swear the oath.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The oath, in that which is between him and his Lord,1893 is according to that, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

728. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There are two types of oath: there is an oath which must be expiated and an oath for which one seeks forgiveness. The oath which must be expiated is when a man says,1894 “By Allah! I will do it!” The oath for which one seeks forgiveness1895 is when someone says, “By Allah! I have done it!”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

729. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿĀ’ishah, the Mother of the Mu’minūn , said concerning an inadvertent [oath],1896 ‘It is everything which a man says, connecting it to his speech but not meaning an oath by it [such as] “No, by Allah,” and “Of course, by Allah,” and that which his heart has not resolved upon.’”1897

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. An example of inadvertent [oaths] is a man who swears about something thinking that it is as he said but it turns out to be otherwise. This is also a kind of inadvertent [oath], and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
