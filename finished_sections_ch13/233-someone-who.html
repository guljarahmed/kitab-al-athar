233. SOMEONE WHO IMPOSES ON HIMSELF TO WALK [ON HAJJ OR ʿUMRAH]

723. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm spoke about someone who imposes on himself to walk1886 [on Hajj or ʿUmrah] and then walks for a part and rides for another part, and he said, ‘He should return and walk the part that he rode.’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but we adhere to the verdict of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib , ‘If he rides, he must sacrifice a hady offering or a sheep or goat and it will suffice him, sacrificing it and giving it away as ṣadaqah and not eating any of it, and then performing an ʿUmrah or Hajj. There will be nothing further due from him,’1887 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Meaning someone who said, “It is obligatory on me to walk to the House of Allah,” or “to the Kaʿbah” must perform Hajj or ʿUmrah walking. If he wishes, he may mount and then slaughter an animal [in expiation]. ↩
  2. Aṭ-Ṭaḥāwī mentioned that he has to sacrifice a hady sacrifice for giving up walking and make expiation for breaking his oath. ↩
