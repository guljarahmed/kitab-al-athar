57. THE PRAYER OF THE TWO ʿĪDS

201. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us saying, “I asked Ibrāhīm about a man who goes out to the place of prayer (muṣallā)632 and finds that the imam has already prayed, ‘Should he pray by himself?’ He said, ‘He doesn’t have to pray,633 but if he wishes he may pray.’ I said, ‘If he doesn’t go out to the place of prayer, should he pray in his house in the same way as the imam does it?’ He said, ‘No.’”’”634

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The ʿĪd is only with the imam, so if you miss it with the imam, then there is no prayer [to be done],635 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

202. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd was seated in the mosque of Kufa, and along with him were Ḥudhayfah ibn al-Yamān and Abū Mūsā al-Ashʿarī . Al-Walīd ibn ʿUqbah ibn Abī Muʿayṭ came up to them, and he was the amir of Kufa at that time. He said, ‘Tomorrow is your ʿĪd, so what should I do?’ The two of them said, ‘Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān,636 tell him what he should do.’ So ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd told him to pray without the call to prayer or the iqāmah,637 and to say the takbīr five times638 in the first [rakʿah] and four times639 in the second and to do the two recitations consecutively,640 and to deliver the khuṭbah641 on his mount after the prayer.”642

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but there is no harm if he does the khuṭbah standing even if he is not on his mount. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

203. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The prayer on the two ʿĪds is always done before the khuṭbah. Then the imam pauses on his mount after the prayer. He supplicates [gives the khuṭbah]643 and prays, without a call to prayer or iqāmah.’”
