76. THE NEWBORN BABY CRYING OUT AT BIRTH, AND SAYING THE PRAYER OVER IT [IF IT DIES]

262. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the miscarried foetus,817 ‘If it had cried out,818 then the prayer is prayed over it, and it inherits,819 but if it had not cried out then the prayer is not prayed over it and it does not inherit.’”820

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and its crying out at birth is [proof of] its emerging alive,821 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

263. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a completely formed but stillborn child, ‘It does not prevent [someone else inheriting],822 it does not inherit, and the prayer is not said over it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but it is given a ghusl, shrouded and buried, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. The child that is miscarried before being completely formed. ↩
  2. If it raised its voice in crying at birth, the intention being to see what signs of life come from it, meaning that if it called out, cried, sneezed or moved one of its limbs. ↩
  3. The child inherits if most of it comes out alive and then it dies, but if less than the majority of it emerges alive and then it dies, it does not inherit. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.80. ↩
  5. He explained it with its Sharīʿah meaning. ↩
  6. Here he uses a technical term which means lexically “to prevent” and technically means to prevent a specific person from inheriting all or part of the inheritance, and there are two types of it: the prevention of a specific person from inheriting a specific obligatory share such that he inherits a smaller obligatory share, and prevention of a specific person from inheriting entirely because of the presence of another person who, if it had not been for him, he would have inherited. The miscarried foetus does not prevent inheritance in either case, i.e., either completely or partially, because he was dead. ↩
