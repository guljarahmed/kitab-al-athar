47. PROLONGED INTENSE LAUGHTER IN THE PRAYER, AND WHAT IS DISLIKED IN PRAYER

160. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There is no harm511 in a man covering his head during prayer as long as he does not cover his mouth, and it is disapproved for him to cover his mouth.’”512 513

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and we also disapprove of him covering his nose, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

161. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who, while praying ʿAṣr, remembers during the prayer that he hadn’t prayed Ẓuhr, ‘This prayer of his is invalid and he must begin with Ẓuhr then pray ʿAṣr.’”514

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this515 except in one respect:516 if he fears that he will miss ʿAṣr entirely if he begins again with Ẓuhr, he must continue with ʿAṣr and then pray Ẓuhr when the sun has set. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

162. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said of a man praying on a cloudy day and then the sun appeared while some of his prayer remained unfinished and he [found that he] had not been praying towards the qiblah, ‘He should turn towards the qiblah, calculate what he has done already and pray what remains of his prayer.’”517

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

163. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Manṣūr ibn Zādhān narrated to us from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī518 that when the Prophet was in prayer, a blind man came from the direction of the qiblah intending to join the prayer while the people were praying Fajr, and the man fell into a pitfall.519 Some of the people laughed so much that it became intense and prolonged. When the Messenger of Allah finished he said, “Whoever of you laughed intensely and prolongedly should repeat wuḍū’ and the prayer.”’”520 521

164. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who laughs intensely and prolongedly522 in prayer, ‘He should repeat wuḍū’ and the prayer and seek forgiveness of his Lord, because it is the most extreme type of factor that nullifies wuḍū’.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
