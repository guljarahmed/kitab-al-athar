61. THE PROSTRATION660 IN [SŪRAH] ṢĀD

209. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm did not use to prostrate in [Sūrah] Ṣād, and that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd did not use to prostrate during it.”661

Muḥammad said, “However, we do think that one should prostrate during it, and we adhere to the hadith narrated from the Messenger of Allah .”

210. Muḥammad said, “ʿUmar ibn Dharr al-Hamdānī informed us from his father from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr from Ibn ʿAbbās that the Prophet said concerning the prostration in Ṣād, ‘Dāwūd prostrated it662 in tawbah, and we prostrate it out of gratitude.’”663

“That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Know that there are two positions about the prostrations in the Qur’ān: they are either sunnah or meritorious acts both of which are well-known verdicts with the Mālikīs. According to the Shāfiʿīs they are a strongly confirmed (mu’akkadah) sunnah, and the Ḥanafīs say that they are incumbent (wājib) because of His words, exalted is He, “Prostrate to Allah,” (Sūrah Fuṣṣilat: 36) and the unqualified command is for something which is incumbent (wājib). There are fourteen prostrations which we and the Shāfiʿīs agree on except that according to him (ash-Shāfiʿī) there are two prostrations in as-Sajdah and in his view none in Ṣād of those which are obligatory. The people of Madīnah say that there are no prostrations in the [shorter] sūrahs [known as] Mufaṣṣal. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.40. ↩
  3. And we are commanded to emulate them because of His words, exalted is He, “They are the ones Allah has guided, so be guided by their guidance.” (Sūrat al-Anʿām: 91) ↩
  4. The fact that it is out of gratitude does not negate it being incumbent, because all obligations are only incumbent as gratitude for the uninterrupted and continuous succession of blessings. ↩
