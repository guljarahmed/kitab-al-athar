60. SAYING TAKBĪR656 DURING THE DAYS OF TASHRĪQ657

208. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib used to say the takbīr from the prayer of Fajr on the day of ʿArafah until the prayer of ʿAṣr on the last of the days of tashrīq.”658

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but Abū Ḥanīfah did not. On the contrary he used to adhere to the verdict of Ibn Masʿūd659 that one says the takbīr from the prayer of Fajr on the day of ʿArafah until the prayer of ʿAṣr on the day of Sacrifice, saying the takbīr at ʿAṣr and then stopping.”

Footnotes

  1. It is incumbent in the well-known position of the madhhab. ↩
  2. The days of tashrīq are the three days known as the days of drying the meat in which the meat of sacrifice can be dried, after the first day of the ʿĪd known as the day of slaughter: an-naḥr. – Trans. ↩
  3. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.60. ↩
  4. He adhered to his verdict adhering to the lesser because saying the takbīr aloud is an innovation, so adhering to the verdict of ʿAlī is adhering to the more numerous since that is being on the safe side with respect to acts of worship. ↩
