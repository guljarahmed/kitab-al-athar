46. A MAN EXPERIENCING MOISTURE DURING THE PRAYER

158. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us from Ibrāhīm from Abū Zurʿah ibn ʿAmr ibn Jarīr ibn ʿAbdullāh that Abū Hurayrah said concerning the man who experiences moisture502 at the end of his penis503 while he is in prayer, “He should place his palms on the ground504 and on the pebbles and then wipe his face and hands [i.e., do tayammum] and then he should pray.”’ Ḥammād said, ‘So I asked Ibrāhīm, “So how do you yourself act?” He said, “When I experience that, I repeat the prayer,505 which is safer and surer for my self.”’”

Muḥammad said, “As for us, we think he should continue in his prayer and not repeat it, nor should he strike his hands on the ground nor wipe his face and hands [in tayammum] unless he is certain506 that it came out of him after he had done wuḍū’. If he is certain that it has, then let him repeat his wuḍū’. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

159. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr that Ibn ʿAbbās said, ‘If you experience anything of moisture then sprinkle it507 with water. Then later if you experience508 it, say, “It is from the water.”’”509

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if it happens a great deal510 to a person, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
