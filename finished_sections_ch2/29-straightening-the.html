29. STRAIGHTENING THE ROWS AND THE MERIT OF THE FIRST ROW

89. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm used to say, ‘Make your rows straight, and make your shoulders level.310 Press closely together or shayṭān will enter in the gaps311 between you like lambs of small black Yemeni sheep or goats. Allah and His angels send blessings on those who straighten the rows.’”312

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. A row ought not to be left with a gap in it without them rectifying it. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

90. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm whether the first row has any merit over and above the second and he said, “They only used to say,313 ‘Do not stand in the row – meaning the second – until the first row is complete.’”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. When the first row is complete, one ought not to try and crowd into it because that314 is the cause of annoyance [to others], and so to stand in the second row315 is better than the first.”

Footnotes

  1. i.e., stick close together and pack closely together until you are adjoining each other. ↩
  2. The conjunction “or” is used here because of the possibility of either one of two matters: either the straightness of the rows or shayṭān inserting himself between them, meaning that shayṭān will enter in the gaps in your rows and whisper in your breasts so that your hearts will come to differ. ↩
  3. Abū Yūsuf narrated it from Ibrāhīm from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb on pp.31-32. ↩
  4. i.e., in the time of the Companions. There is no negation of the superiority of the first row, but on the contrary there is affirmation of it from another point of view, by prohibiting standing in the second row until the first row is complete. ↩
  5. i.e., standing in the first row when it is already complete. ↩
  6. Causing annoyance is not permissible, so then to stand in the first row is not recommended, but on the contrary it is disapproved. ↩
