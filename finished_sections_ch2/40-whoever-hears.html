40. WHOEVER HEARS THE IQĀMAH WHILE IN THE MOSQUE

125. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the man who is praying an obligatory prayer in the mosque and the mu’adhdhin calls the iqāmah while he is in the middle of a rakʿah,402 ‘He completes it with another rakʿah,’ and then he joins403 people in prayer [saying] the takbīr. When the imam has prayed two rakʿahs, sits down and does the tashahhud, the man should say the salām404 to his right and to his left but within himself,405 then stand406 and say the takbīr407 and pray what is left of his prayer as a supererogatory prayer, only joining the people in prayer for the even [number of rakʿahs]408 of his prayer.’ ʿĀmir ash-Shaʿbī said, ‘He should join another rakʿah to it,409 finish that and then join the people.’”

Muḥammad said, “The verdict of ash-Shaʿbī is preferable to us, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. The first rakʿah. ↩
  2. Without saying the salām. ↩
  3. Because his prayer is completed. However, we say that this contradicts the hadith, “The imam has only been appointed to be taken as a model,” so that if one says the salām in the middle one is no longer taking him as a model, and because in this there are two prayers together: the obligatory and an optional prayer at the same time, and a half of it is obligatory and a half optional, and there is nothing else like that in the Sharīʿah. ↩
  4. i.e., secretly. ↩
  5. i.e., after the interruption of the prayer. The two rakʿahs he prays by himself and the two he prays with the imam become obligatory and what remains of the two rakʿahs he prays with the imam become optional for him. ↩
  6. A second time for his entrance in the prayer along with the imam with the intention of an optional prayer. ↩
  7. Because the first even number of rakʿahs was of the obligatory prayer which he prayed individually. ↩
  8. If he had reached to doing a prostration in the first rakʿah to safeguard what has already been discharged from being void, but if he has not done a prostration then he interrupts what he is doing and begins afresh with the imam then that will be correct. ↩
