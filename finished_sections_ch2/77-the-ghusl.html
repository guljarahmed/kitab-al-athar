77. THE GHUSL OF THE SHAHĪD823

264. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who dies as a shahīd and who dies at the place where824 he received the mortal blow, ‘His leather socks825 and his cap are taken off him and he is buried in the clothes which he had on.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. They also take off him all leather and weapons, and they can add826 whatever shrouding they wish. He is not to be given a ghusl but the prayer is to be said over him, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

265. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who is killed on the battlefield, ‘He is not to be given a ghusl,’827 but about the person who was struck and carried [alive] to his family [among whom he later died] he [Ḥammād] said, ‘He is given a ghusl.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. And also if he is carried alive on men’s hands and then dies, he is to be given a ghusl. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

266. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Sālim al-Afṭas narrated to us saying, “There is no prophet who has not fled from his people to the Kaʿbah to worship its Lord, and around it there are the graves828 of three hundred prophets.”’”829

267. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAṭā’ ibn as-Sā’ib830 narrated to us saying, “The graves of Hūd, Ṣāliḥ and Shuʿayb, peace be upon them, are in the Masjid al-Ḥarām.”’”

268. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ziyād ibn ʿIlāqah narrated to us from ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Ḥārith that Abū Mūsā al-Ashʿarī said, “The Messenger of Allah said, ‘The perishing of my community831 will be by means of the thrusts of spears and plague.’ Someone said, ‘Messenger of Allah, we know the thrusting of spears, but what is the plague?’ He said, ‘The stabbing832 of your enemies among the jinn. Among both cases there will be shahīds.’”’”833
