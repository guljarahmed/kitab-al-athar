27. RECITING, “IN THE NAME OF ALLAH, THE ALL-MERCIFUL, THE MOST MERCIFUL” ALOUD

81. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Sufyān narrated to us from Yazīd ibn ʿAbdullāh that his father288 said that he prayed behind an imam who recited ‘In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful’ aloud and when he finished he said289 to him, “Slave of Allah!290 dispense with291 these words of yours, because I prayed behind the Messenger of Allah , behind Abū Bakr, behind ʿUmar, and behind ʿUthmān , and I did not hear these [words] from them.”’”292

82. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Ibn Masʿūd said concerning a man who recited “In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful” aloud [in the prayer], that it is the practice of the desert Arabs293 and that he did not use to recite it aloud nor did any of his companions.’”294

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

83. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There are four things with which the imam lowers his voice,295 “Glorious are You, O Allah and with Your praise, [and blessed is Your name, exalted is Your majesty and there is no god other than You,]” the seeking refuge from shayṭān, “In the name of Allah, the All-Merciful, the Most Merciful” and the āmīn.’”296

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
