31. SOMEONE WHO PRAYED THE OBLIGATORY PRAYER

97. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham narrated to us ascribing it to the Prophet that two men333 of the Companions of the Prophet prayed Ẓuhr in their homes thinking that the prayer had been prayed, and then they came334 while the Prophet was in prayer. They sat down and did not join [the prayer]. When the Prophet finished he called for them and they came with their flanks335 quaking, fearful that something [revelation] might have newly come about them. He said to them, “What prevented you from praying?” They said, “Messenger of Allah, we thought that the prayer had been done and we prayed in our homes. Then we came here and found you praying, but we thought that it would be unacceptable for us to pray again.” He said, “When it is like that, then join the prayer, and regard336 the first as the obligatory prayer and this one337 as supererogatory.”’”338

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. But one may not repeat Fajr, ʿAṣr and Maghrib.”

98. Muḥammad said, “Mālik ibn Anas, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, informed us from Nāfiʿ that Ibn ʿUmar said, ‘If you have prayed Fajr and Maghrib and then you reach them339 do not repeat them with more than what you have already prayed.’”

Muḥammad said, “As for Fajr and ʿAṣr one ought not to pray supererogatory prayers after them because of the saying of the Messenger of Allah , ‘There is no prayer340 after ʿAṣr until the sun sets, nor is there any prayer after Fajr until the sun rises.’ As for Maghrib it is an odd number [of rakʿahs] and praying supererogatory prayers with an odd number [of rakʿahs] is disapproved.341 So if a man joins them as supererogation, when the imam says the salām, let him add342 a fourth rakʿah to it, and say the tashahhud and the salām. This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
