42. SOMEONE WHO PRAYS IN HIS HOUSE WITHOUT AN ADHĀN

132. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Ibn Masʿūd led his companions428 in prayer in his house without an adhān or iqāmah and said, ‘The imam’s iqāmah [in the mosque] suffices.’”429

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this if the man prays by himself, but if he prays in the group it is preferable to us430 that he call the adhān and the iqāmah. However, if he calls the iqāmah and leaves out the adhān there is no harm in that.”

Footnotes

  1. He led them in prayer without adhān or iqāmah because the adhān and iqāmah of the local mosque are the same as his adhān and iqāmah. ↩
  2. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated it in the Muṣannaf, vol.1, p.199. ↩
  3. Meaning that it is recommended in our view in order that it should be done in the most complete and perfect manner that the performance should be done in the manner of the group (jamāʿah). ↩
