45. WHAT OF THE PRAYER IS REPEATED AND WHAT OF THAT IS DISAPPROVED

145. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm about prayer before Maghrib459 and he forbade me to do it. He said, “The Prophet , Abū Bakr and ʿUmar did not pray it.”’”460

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this.461 When the sun sets there is to be no prayer over the dead nor anything else before the prayer of Maghrib, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah”

146. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If the blood and the urine and other things are the size of a dirham then repeat your prayer. If it is less than the size of a dirham462 then conclude your prayer.’”463

And Muḥammad said, “The prayer is valid until the point when that is larger than the size of a big mithqāl464 dirham at which point one’s prayer is not valid, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

147. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAlī ibn al-Aqmar narrated to us that the Prophet passed by a man who had draped his garment over his head and shoulders allowing the rest of it to trail down over his body during the prayer and so he folded it up for him.’”465

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Draping one’s garment loosely over the head and shoulders and over the shirt or anything else is disapproved because it resembles the actions of the People of the Book, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

148. Muḥammad said, “ʿAbd al-Malik466 ibn ʿUmayr narrated to us from Qazaʿah from Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī that the Prophet said, ‘There is to be no prayer467 after the morning prayer468 until the sun has risen,469 nor is there to be any prayer after the ʿAṣr prayer until the sun has set. Nor are these two days to be fasted: al-Fiṭr470 and al-Aḍḥā471. Nor should one make journeys472 except to three mosques: the sacred mosque [in Makkah], my mosque and the al-Aqṣā mosque. A woman must not travel473 except in the company of a maḥram [male relative whom she is unable to marry in any circumstances].’”474 475

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this, and a woman ought not476 to travel except in the company of her husband or with a male relative [whom she is unable to marry in any circumstances], and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

149. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm disapproved of cracking the joints of the fingers in the prayer or of casting477 the large outer garment over the shoulders, or placing the hands on the flanks,478 or burying479 the larger pebbles, or sitting back with the buttocks on the ground and setting the thighs erect while laying one’s arms along the ground (iqʿā’),480 or fiddling with one’s beard.”481

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because it is trifling with the prayer which distracts one from it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

150. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm, “Sadl [draping the garment over head and shoulders allowing the rest of it to trail down over the body] is disapproved in the prayer; do not emulate the Jews.”482

151. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb led his companions483 in Maghrib without reciting484 any of the Qur’ān in it the whole time until he finished. His companions said to him, ‘What prevented you from reciting, Amīr al-Mu’minin?’ He said, ‘Did I not do that? I was equipping an army485 to go from Madīnah to Shām and I continued to ride with it stage by stage until it reached Shām,’ and so he and his companions repeated the prayer.”486

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

152. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr narrated to us from Abū Ghādiyah487 that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb used to beat people488 for praying after ʿAṣr.’”489

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We do not think that anyone should pray supererogatory prayers490 after ʿAṣr under any circumstances. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

153. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you join people in prayer without intending491 the same prayer as they are doing then it is not valid for you. If the imam intends one prayer and those behind him intend another it is valid for him but not for them.’”492

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

154. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘I would not be pleased493 with the prayer of a man when the sun reddens [even if he were to give me] two copper494 coins.’”495

Muḥammad said, “Prayer is disliked (makrūh)496 at that time (unless one has missed the ʿAṣr prayer of that day, so that one should then pray it at that time). But as for other obligatory and supererogatory prayers one ought not to do them. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

155. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘When the blood on your body or on your garment is of the size of a dirham then you must repeat your prayer [after washing off the blood and repeating wuḍū’], but if it is less than that then you may carry on with your prayer.’”

Muḥammad said, “It is the same whether it is blood on one’s garment or body. If it is more than the size of a large mithqāl dirham497 then repeat the prayer. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

156. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿĀṣim ibn Abī’n-Najūd narrated to us from Abū Razīn that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd took a louse during the prayer and buried it. Then later he said, ‘Did We not make the earth a receptacle498 for the living and the dead?’ (Sūrat al-Mursalāt: 25-26)”499

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We see no harm500 in killing a louse and burying it during the prayer. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

157.501 Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm about a man who slaughters a sheep while in wuḍū’ and some blood gets on his hand. He said, “He should wash off what has got on to him but he does not need to repeat wuḍū’.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
