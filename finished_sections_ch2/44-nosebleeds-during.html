44. NOSEBLEEDS DURING THE PRAYER AND BREAKING WIND

142. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr narrated to us from Maʿbad ibn Ṣubayḥ450 that a man451 of the Companions of the Messenger of Allah prayed behind ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān . The man broke wind452 and so left the prayer. He did not speak until he had done wuḍū’ and then returned453 saying, “and they do not knowingly454 persist in what455 they were doing” (Sūrah Āl ʿImrān: 135). So he calculated what he had prayed and prayed what remained.’”456

143. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ibrāhīm said, ‘That suffices him,457 but I prefer him to start again from the beginning.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of Ibrāhīm. That is sufficient for him, but if he talks and begins it again that is better. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

144. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who has a nosebleed during the prayer or who breaks wind, ‘Let him leave without speaking except for remembrance of Allah,458 then do wuḍū’ and return to his place and make up what remains of his prayer reckoning whatever he has already prayed. However, if he talks then let him start from the beginning.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to his. To speak and then start from the beginning is better, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
