54. THE PRAYER OF SOMEONE WHO FEARS HYPOCRISY619

197. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Jawāb at-Taymī narrated to us from Abū Mūsā al-Ashʿarī that a man came to him and said, “I fear hypocrisy for myself.” So Abū Mūsā said, “Have you never prayed where no one but Allah saw you?”620 He said, “Of course I have.” He said, “The hypocrite does not pray where no one but Allah sees him.”’”

Footnotes

  1. Because the believer encounters in his action that which blemishes him and which is contrary to pure sincerity, but it does not necessarily follow from his fear that he has really fallen out of ikhlāṣ but rather that is a heightened state of scrupulousness and taqwā. ↩
  2. “Because the hypocrite acts for desire of reputation and for showing off, not desiring the Face of Allah, so you are not a hypocrite since you have prayed where no one saw you but Allah, and that is the sign of the believer.” Abū Mūsā probably meant by this answer to cut off the chain of whisperings which arise in the breast. ↩
