32. SUPEREROGATORY PRAYERS

99. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Sufyān343 narrated to us from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī that the Messenger of Allah used to pray while sitting in iḥtibā’344 in supererogatory prayers.’”345

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and see no harm in it. Then when one reaches the prostration one unties346 the cloth347 and prostrates. This is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

100. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Jaʿfar348 narrated to us saying, “The Messenger of Allah used to pray thirteen rakʿahs between the prayer of ʿIshā’349 and the prayer of Fajr: eight supererogatory rakʿahs, three rakʿahs350 of witr and the two rakʿahs of Fajr.”’”351 352

101. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥuṣayn ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān said, ‘ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar used to pray supererogatory prayers on his mount in whatever direction it would turn with him. Then when it was the obligatory prayer or the witr353 he would dismount and pray.’”354

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

102. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who joined people in prayer without intending [the obligatory prayer], ‘It is a supererogatory prayer.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. He only meant by that if someone had already prayed the prayer in his house and then came and joined the people in their prayer, that his prayer along with them is supererogatory. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
