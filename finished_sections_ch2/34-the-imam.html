34. THE IMAM SAYING THE SALĀM AND HIS SITTING

104. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘When the imam says the salām do not move away358 until the imam gets up from his place,359 unless the imam has no fiqh.’”360

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this because one does not know361 whether or not he362 may have to do the two prostrations of forgetfulness. If the imam is someone who does not know the fiqh363 of the prayer there is no harm in getting up and leaving. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”

105. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Abū’ḍ-Ḍuḥā from Masrūq that when Abū Bakr aṣ-Ṣiddīq said the salām after the prayer he used to be as if he were sitting on hot stones364 until he had moved from his place.”365

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

106. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who prays in a narrow place such that he is unable366 to sit on his left side, or in the case where he is ailing, ‘Then let him sit on his right side, and if he is able let him sit on his left side.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

107. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If a man is ailing let him sit in the prayer however he wants.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, if his illness prevents him from the sitting posture in the prayer that is commanded.367 That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

108. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The salām is what creates a break368 between two prayers.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
