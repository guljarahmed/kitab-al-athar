53. THE PRAYER OF FEAR605

194. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning the prayer of fear,606 ‘When the imam prays607 with his companions let a party of them stand with the imam and a party stand facing the enemy. The imam should pray a single rakʿah608 with the party with him and then the party who have prayed with the imam should go609 without speaking and take the place of their companions,610 and the other party should come and pray the other rakʿah with the imam, and then leave without talking and take the place of their companions who should come and individually pray a rakʿah611 and then go and take their companions’ places who should come and individually complete612 the rakʿah that remains.’”613

195. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Ḥārith ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān narrated the like of that to us from ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās .’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this. As for the first party they complete their rakʿah without recitation of the Qur’ān because they caught the beginning of the prayer614 with the imam and the imam’s recitation of the Qur’ān is their own. As for the other party, they complete their rakʿah with recitation of the Qur’ān615 because they missed it with the imam. This is all the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

196. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said concerning the man who prays the prayer of fear by himself, “He prays standing facing the qiblah, then if he is not able then mounted facing the qiblah, then if he is not able he should make the motions616 in whatever direction he is facing, while not prostrating on anything. He indicates the prayer by motions, making the [motion indicating] his prostration lower than [that indicating] his bowing, but he must not give up wuḍū’ or recitation of the Qur’ān617 in the two rakʿahs.”’”618

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
