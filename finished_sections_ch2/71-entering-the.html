71. ENTERING THE DECEASED INTO THE GRAVE

243. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm, “From where does one enter the deceased into the grave?” He said, “The side of the qiblah770 from where one prays over him.”’”771 Ibrāhīm said, “Someone narrated to me who had seen the People of Madīnah entering their dead in the earliest epoch from the direction of the qiblah and that ‘drawing in’772 is something which the People of Madīnah did773 after that.”’”774

Muḥammad said, “One enters [the deceased] from the direction of the qiblah and one does not draw him forth from the direction of the legs. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

244. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If they wish, those who enter the grave [with the corpse to bury it] can be an even number775 or, if they wish, an odd number. All of that is good.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Because the side of the qiblah is exalted and so it is more appropriate to enter him from this side. ↩
  2. Clarification of “from the side of the qiblah” i.e., from the direction of the place in which the prayer is being done facing the qiblah. ↩
  3. Sall is to draw something out of something by pulling it and removing it, such as drawing a sword out of a scabbard. The form of “drawing in” is that the body is placed to the right of the qiblah with the feet pointing lengthwise towards the grave and then the body is drawn into the grave feet first and placed in the grave with the head being placed last. ↩
  4. Done because of the weakness of their salty marshland so that the laḥd [niche in which the body is placed] should not collapse when the body is placed on it. ↩
  5. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.82 and p.84. ↩
  6. An even or odd number of men may enter the grave with the deceased according to necessity. ↩
