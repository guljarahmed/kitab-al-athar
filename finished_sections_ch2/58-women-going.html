58. WOMEN GOING OUT TO ATTEND THE TWO ʿĪDS, AND SIGHTING THE NEW MOON

204. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAbd al-Karīm ibn Abī’l-Mukhāriq that Umm ʿAṭiyyah said, ‘Women used to be granted as a concession644 to go out to attend the two ʿĪds: al-Fiṭr and al-Aḍḥā.’”645

Muḥammad said, “Their going out to attend the ʿĪd does not please us,646 except for old women beyond child-bearing age. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

205. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm concerning people who testified that they had seen the new moon of Shawwāl. Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm about that and he said, “If they come in the morning, then they should break the fast647 and go out [to the place of prayer],648 but if they come in the evening, they should not go out [to the place of prayer] nor should they break the fast until the next day.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this except in one respect:649 they break the fast650 and go out [to the place of prayer]651 the day after if they come [as witnesses] in the evening, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
