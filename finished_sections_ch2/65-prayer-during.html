65. PRAYER DURING THE SOLAR ECLIPSE697

222. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The sun was eclipsed during the lifetime of the Messenger of Allah on the day in which Ibrāhīm the son of the Messenger of Allah died, and so people said, “The sun is eclipsed for the death of Ibrāhīm.” That reached the Messenger of Allah , so he delivered an address to the people698 saying, “The sun and the moon are two of the signs699 of Allah and they are not eclipsed for the death of anyone (nor for anyone’s life).” Then he prayed two rakʿahs,700 and after that there was supplication until it was uncovered.’”701

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We do not think that there is any more than one bowing702 in each rakʿah and two prostrations as is usual in people’s other prayers. We think that they should pray in a group for the solar eclipse,703 but that no one should lead them in jamāʿah except for the imam704 who leads them in prayer for the Jumuʿah. As for praying in mosques used for the daily prayers705 [but not the Jumuʿah] in jamāʿah, then no. As for recitation aloud, it has not reached us that the Prophet recited aloud706 in it, but it has reached us that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib recited aloud in it at Kufa, but it is preferable to us707 that one not recite aloud in it. As for lunar eclipses, people only pray individually708 and they do not pray in a group, not with the imam nor with anyone else, and it is thus with all fearful events. When the sun is eclipsed at a time in which one does not pray, such as at sunrise, the middle of the day or after ʿAṣr, then there is no prayer709 at these times, but supplication until it is uncovered710 or until prayer become permissible, in which case one prays if some part of the eclipse still remains.”

  1. kusūf: eclipse, means lexically to alter and become black, so that the eclipse of the sun means that it becomes black and its rays disappear. What is best known among the people of fiqh is to choose kusūf for solar eclipse, and khusūf for lunar eclipse, and al-Jawharī claimed that it is the most tasteful usage. ↩
  2. In order to repel the delusion that it was because of the death of Ibrāhīm. Thus the khuṭbah was from a chance occurrence and not by way of any Sharīʿah purpose, and it is for this reason that in our view and that of Mālik and Aḥmad there is no khuṭbah during an eclipse. Ash-Shāfiʿī said that a khuṭbah is recommended. ↩
  3. Signs indicating the oneness of Allah and His tremendous power, and in this there is refutation of that which the people of Jāhiliyyah used to believe that the stars cause things on the earth. So the Prophet informed us that this is a spurious belief, and that the sun and moon are two creations subject to Allah, neither of which has authority over anything else and no power to repel anything from themselves. ↩
  4. With the form of ordinary supererogatory prayers in every rakʿah of which there is a single bowing and two prostrations.   5. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.55, and al-Bukhārī under “the eclipse” in the chapter on “the prayer during the eclipse of the moon,” and an-Nasā’ī in the book on “the eclipse,” the chapter on “a type of eclipse prayer.” ↩
  6. rakʿah in the sense of rukūʿ: bowing. ↩
