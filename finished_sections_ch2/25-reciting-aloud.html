25. RECITING ALOUD

77. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Someone who prayed274 beside ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd informed me that he had been eager to hear his voice but could only hear275 him saying, “My Lord increase me in knowledge,” which he repeated over and over again,276 and so the man thought he was reciting Ṭā Hā.’”277

Muḥammad said, “This is in the prayer during the daytime, and so we see no harm in it if a man remains on some portion of the Qur’ān such as this, supplicating for himself in optional extra rakʿahs. But as for the obligatory prayer, then no [we do not think he should do so].”

Footnotes

  1. It was ʿAlqamah ibn Qays. ↩
  2. Thus it is clearly established that the recitation in the daytime is only done secretly, and there has to be recitation in the prayer of Ẓuhr and ʿAṣr. ↩
  3. It is disapproved to repeat a sūrah within a single rakʿah but there is no harm in that in optional prayers, because it has not been established of the Prophet that he voluntarily repeated a single āyah in the obligatory prayers. As for the action of Ibn Masʿūd perhaps it was for the purpose of supplication and that he was overcome in seeking increase in knowledge. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.30. ↩
