THE PRAYER

24. BEGINNING THE PRAYER, RAISING THE HANDS AND PROSTRATING ON THE TURBAN

72. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm262 that some of the people of Basra came to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb only for the purpose of asking him about the beginning of the prayer. He said, ‘So ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb stood263 and began the prayer with them behind him, and then he recited aloud264 saying, “Glorious are You, O Allah and with Your praise, and blessed is Your name, exalted is Your majesty and there is no god other than You.”’”265

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this266 in the beginning of the prayer, but we do not think that the imam should recite aloud nor those behind him. ʿUmar only recited aloud in order to teach them that which they had asked him.”

73. Similarly it has reached us that Ibrāhīm said, “Do not raise267 your hands in any part of the prayer after the first time.”268

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

74. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever does not say takbīr269 in the beginning of the prayer is not in prayer.’”270

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this except if he says takbīr for going into the bowing posture but says it while standing erect271 and intends by it to enter into the prayer in which case it suffices him, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

75. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿUthmān ibn ʿAbdullāh ibn Mawhab narrated to us that he prayed behind Abū Hurayrah who used to say takbīr every time he prostrated and every time he lifted himself up.’”272

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

76. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “There is no harm273 in prostrating on the turban.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We see no harm in it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

  6. One raises one’s hands, in our view, level with the ears until one’s thumbs are level with the earlobes and the tips of the fingers level with the tips of the ears. Similarly, the dominant majority agreed that it is not recommended apart from in three places, i.e., in between the two prostrations, after two rakʿahs and in every lowering oneself down and raising oneself up, even if there are narrations concerning that. They differed on raising the hands upon bowing and after it. Abū Ḥanīfah and his companions gave the verdict that one leaves out raising the hands at these two places, and this is narrated by Ibn al-Qāsim of Mālik and thus the Mālikis chose that as their position. Ash-Shāfiʿī and Aḥmad gave the verdict that one should raise them in both places, and this is also narrated of Mālik. Our proof is that which Muslim narrated from Jābir ibn Samurah who said, “The Messenger of Allah came out to us and said, ‘What is it that I see you raising your hands as if they were the tails of headstrong horses. Be still in the prayer.’” There is that which Abū Dāwūd and at-Tirmidhī narrated of ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd that he said, “Should I not lead you in prayer with the prayer of the Messenger of Allah ?” and he prayed without raising his hands the first time. ↩
