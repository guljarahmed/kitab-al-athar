41. SOMEONE WHO MISSES410 PART OF THE PRAYER

126. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘When someone enters the mosque and the people are bowing, let him bow411 without doing it in haste [and then advance to the row].’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this, but rather he should walk with stillness and dignity412 until he reaches the row, then pray what he catches and make up what he misses.”

127. Muḥammad from al-Mubārak ibn Faḍālah from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī that Abū Bakrah bowed before reaching the row and walked until he joined the row. So he mentioned that to the Messenger of Allah who said, “May Allah increase you in your earnestness,413 but do not do it again.”414

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We regard it as sufficing him but we are not pleased that he do it. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

128. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said concerning a man who comes to the mosque on the day of Jumuʿah415 when the imam is seated at the end of his prayer,416 ‘He says a single takbīr417 and joins them in their prayer, and then he says takbīr418 and sits down with them and says the tashahhud. When the imam says the salām, he stands up and prays two rakʿahs.’”

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this.419 Whoever catches a rakʿah of the Jumuʿah adds another to it, but if one catches up on them while they are seated, one prays four rakʿahs. Traditions from more than one person are reported to that effect.”

129. Muḥammad said, “Saʿīd ibn Abī ʿArūbah informed us from Qatādah that Anas ibn Mālik , al-Ḥasan, Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab and Khilās ibn ʿAmr said, ‘Whoever catches a rakʿah of the Jumuʿah, adds another to it, but whoever catches them while they are seated prays four rakʿahs.’ Thus it has also reached us from ʿAlqamah ibn Qays and al-Aswad ibn Yazīd, and it is the verdict of Sufyān ath-Thawrī and Zufar ibn al-Hudhayl, and to it we adhere.”420 421

130. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that Masrūq and Jundub joined the imam’s prayer of Maghrib catching a rakʿah with him before which he had previously performed two rakʿahs, so they prayed a rakʿah with him and then stood to make up what they had missed. As for Masrūq, he sat after the first rakʿah422 that he was making up, but Jundub stood after the first rakʿah and sat in the second. When they had finished, each one turned to the other.423 They went together424 to ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd and told him the story. He said, ‘Both of you have done well, but that I should pray as Masrūq did is preferable to me.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of Ibn Masʿūd . The person sits in both of the two rakʿahs that he missed. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

131. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that when Ibrāhīm was asked concerning a man whom the imam had preceded with any part of the prayer, ‘Should he say the tashahhud425 every time the imam sits,’ he said, ‘Yes.’ He asked,426 ‘Should he return the salām when the imam says the salām?’ He said,427 ‘When he finishes his prayer, he should return the salām.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
