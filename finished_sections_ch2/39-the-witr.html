39. THE WITR AND WHAT ONE RECITES IN IT

122. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Zubayd al-Yāmī narrated to us from Dharr al-Hamdānī395 (from Saʿīd ibn ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn Abzā that his father said, “The Messenger of Allah used to recite396) in the witr, in the first rakʿah, ‘Glorify the Name of your Lord, the Most High...’ (Sūrat al-Aʿlā), and in the second, ‘Say to the ones who are kāfir...’ meaning, ‘Say: “Kāfirūn!...”’ (Sūrat al-Kāfirūn) which is thus in the recitation of Ibn Masʿūd , and in the third, ‘Say: He is Allah, Absolute Oneness,...’ (Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ).”’”397

Muḥammad said, “If you recite this then that is good. Whatever of the Qur’ān you recite in the witr along with the Fātiḥah of the Book is also good, if you recite three āyāt or more along with the Fātiḥah of the Book. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

123. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb said, ‘I would not like to398 leave off doing the witr with three [rakʿahs] even if I were to have red camels.’”399

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The witr is three rakʿahs without separating them by a salām. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

124. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If one wakes up in the morning without having done the witr then there is no witr.’”400

Muḥammad said, “We do not adhere to this. One does the witr in any case401 except at that time at which prayer is abhorent: when the sun rises, in the middle of the day up until the declination, or at the redness of sunset until it has set. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

Footnotes

  1. The passage in brackets is omitted in some copies, and apparently it was dropped by the scribe, and so we have added it back in from other books. ↩
  2. By way of being recommended. It is obvious that he used to do the witr with three rakʿahs with a single salām and two tashahhuds. An-Nasā’ī narrated it and added, “And one does not say the salām except at the end of them.” The Sunnah is the three sūrahs. However, there is in an-Nihāyah that specifically choosing to persevere in them leads to some people believing that it is incumbent (wājib) which is not acceptable. Thus, if one sometimes recited that which the traditions narrate without persisting in it, it would be good. ↩

  5. Ibn Abī Shaybah narrated the like of it in the Muṣannaf, vol.2, p.91. ↩
  6. i.e., One does not pray it afterwards nor make up for it. ↩
