33. PRAYER IN THE MIḤRĀB

103. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm used to lead them in prayer standing to the left of the miḥrāb355 or to its right.”

Muḥammad said, “As for us, we see no harm in standing in front of356 the miḥrāb as long as one does not enter it, if one stands outside it357 and prostrates within it. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. So that there should not necessarily be any resemblance with the People of the Book since they used to pray in the miḥrabs of their places of worship, and aṭ-ṭāq is the miḥrāb. ↩
  2. i.e., level with it because the sunnah is that the man should stand at the midpoint of the row, and if he stands on either side of it he will not be at the midpoint. ↩
  3. Because the feet are what are taken into account, so that if the imam stands in the miḥrāb it is disapproved, the reason for which is that he is distinguished from the others in terms of place, since the miḥrāb is in a sense another building, and that is the practice of the People of the Book. ↩
