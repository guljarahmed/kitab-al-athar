80. RECITATION OF THE QUR’ĀN IN THE PUBLIC BATHS, AND BY THE PERSON IN NEED OF A GHUSL BECAUSE OF SEXUAL RELATIONS

277. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm and from Saʿīd ibn Jubayr866 that any of the Companions867 of Muḥammad would recite their portion (juz’) of the Qur’ān while not in wuḍū’.”868

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this,869 and we see no harm in it, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

278. Muḥammad said, “Shuʿbah ibn al-Ḥajjāj informed us from ʿAmr ibn Murrah al-Jamalī that ʿAbdullāh ibn Salamah said, ‘I and a man870 from Banī Asad, I think,871 went to visit ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib . He wanted to send us on an errand for him, so he said to us, “You are two capable sturdy men, so strive872 on behalf of your dīn.”’ He said, ‘Then he went to the toilet and came back, took some water and wiped his face and hands, and then returned to reciting the Qur’ān. It was as if we disapproved of that and so he said, “The Messenger of Allah used to recite the Qur’ān and nothing would prevent him from doing that,” or he may have said, “nothing would bar873 him from doing that except being in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations.”’”874

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and we see no harm in the recitation of the Qur’ān in any state whatsoever except when one is in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

279. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād said, ‘I asked Ibrāhīm875 about recitation in the public baths and he said, “They have not been built for that.”’”

Muḥammad said, “But if you wish, recite.”876

280. It has reached us that aḍ-Ḍaḥḥāk ibn Mazāḥim recited in the public baths.

281. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘There are four who do not recite the Qur’ān except for an āyah877 or so: the person in need of a ghusl because of sexual relations, the menstruating woman, the person making love to his wife, and in the public bath.’”878

282. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Remember Allah879 in every state, in the public bath and elsewhere, when you sneeze.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

283. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Praise Allah in whatever state you are, whether in the toilet880 or elsewhere.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

He is wrong since in Ḥanafī fiqh prompting the imam is considered to go against the stillness and humility required of those who follow the imam, but by the imam not taking any of the options outlined the imam has forced someone to prompt him.
