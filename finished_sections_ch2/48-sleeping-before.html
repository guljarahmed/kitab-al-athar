48. SLEEPING BEFORE PRAYER AND BREAKING ONE’S WUḌŪ’ BY DOING SO

165. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘The Messenger of Allah performed wuḍū’ and went out to the mosque. He found that the mu’adhdhin had already called the adhān.523 He laid himself down524 and slept so deeply that it could be recognised that he was asleep – and his sleeping could [ordinarily] be recognised [because] he would breath through his mouth525 when he slept. Then he stood and prayed without [repeating] wuḍū’.’526 Ibrāhīm said, ‘The Prophet was not like others.’”527

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to Ibrāhīm’s verdict. It has reached us that the Prophet said, ‘My eyes sleep, but my heart does not sleep.’528 So the Prophet in this is not like others. As for anyone other than him,529 if he lays himself down and sleeps, then he has to do wuḍū’, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

166. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you fall asleep while seated, standing, bowing or in prostration you do not have to do wuḍū’.’”530

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but when someone lays himself down and sleeps, he has to do wuḍū’, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

167. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ismāʿīl ibn ʿAbd al-Malik narrated to us from Mujāhid. He [Ismāʿīl] said, “I asked him about sleep before ʿIshā’, and he said, ‘I would prefer to pray it by myself531 than to sleep before it and then pray it in the group (jamāʿah).’”’”532

Muḥammad said, “And we533 disapprove of sleep before the prayer of ʿIshā’, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

168. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘[While on a journey] The Messenger of Allah alighted534 one night to sleep and said, “Who will guard535 us tonight?” A young man of the Anṣār said, “I will, Messenger of Allah, I will guard you.” So he stood guard over them until it was the morning when his eye overcame him and they only woke up because of the heat of the sun. The Messenger of Allah rose and did wuḍū’ and his companions did wuḍū’. He told the mu’adhdhin536 to call the adhān, and then he prayed two rakʿahs. Then the iqāmah for the prayer was called and he led his Companions in prayer reciting aloud537 in it as he would ordinarily do when praying at the right time.’”538

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
