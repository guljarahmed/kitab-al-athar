52. LIGHTENING THE PRAYER

185. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘A man578 from among the Companions of the Prophet led some people in prayer and he lengthened the prayer with them. That reached the Prophet and he said, “What is wrong with people who scare people away from this dīn? Whoever leads a people in prayer should lighten it,579 because among them are the sick, the elderly and those with pressing needs.”’”580

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this,581 but he must complete the bowing and prostration fully, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

186. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Maymūn ibn Siyāh narrated to me from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī that someone asked him, “Should I recite five hundred āyāt in a rakʿah?”582 and he was astonished583 and said, “Glorious is Allah! Who is able to do this?” The man said, “I am able to do this.” He said, “The most beloved prayer to Allah is [that in which there is] long continuance of standing in obedience and humble stillness (qunūt584).”’”

Muḥammad said, “Long continuance of standing in supererogatory prayers is preferable to us to much bowing and prostrating,585 but all of that is good, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

187. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb led his companions in the dawn prayer and recited for them586 in the first rakʿah, ‘Say: “Kāfirūn!...”’ (Sūrat al-Kāfirūn) and in the second, ‘In acknowledgment of the established tradition of Quraysh...’ (Sūrah Quraysh).”587 588

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and we regard it as valid,589 but we prefer that when the imam prays the dawn prayer while being resident590 he should lengthen the recitation in it and he should recite in each rakʿah a sūrah of twenty or more āyāt apart from the Fātiḥah of the Book, and that he should make the first [rakʿah] longer than the second. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
