56. THE PRAYER ON THE DAY OF JUMUʿAH AND THE KHUṬBAH

199. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ghaylān and Ayyūb ibn ʿĀ’idh aṭ-Ṭā’ī narrated to us from Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī that the Prophet said, “There are four people who are not obliged to attend the Jumuʿah: women, slaves, travellers and those who are sick.”’ Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘If they do it, it is valid for them.’”624 625

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this.”

200. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd that a man asked him about the khuṭbah626 on the day of Jumuʿah, and he said, ‘Don’t you read Sūrat al-Jumuʿah?’627 He said, ‘Yes, of course, but I do not know how it is.’ He said, ‘“But when they see a chance of trade or entertainment they scatter off to it and leave you standing there,” (Sūrat al-Jumuʿah: 11) so the khuṭbah is done standing on the day of Jumuʿah.’”628

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this,629 except that there are two khuṭbahs630 in between which there is a short sitting,631 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
