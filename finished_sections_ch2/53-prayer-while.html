53. PRAYER WHILE TRAVELLING

188. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād591 said, ‘Mūsā ibn Muslim narrated to us from Mujāhid that ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar said, “When you are travelling and you make up your mind to reside for fifteen days, do the full prayer, but if you do not know592 [whether or not you will reside for fifteen days], shorten it.”’”593

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

189. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb led people in prayer594 in Makkah with two rakʿahs and then finished the prayer. He said, ‘People of Makkah, we are travellers, so those who are from the people of the city should complete the prayer.’ So the people of the city completed it.’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. When someone who is resident joins the prayer of a traveller and the traveller finishes the prayer, the person who is resident must stand up and complete the prayer, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

190. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘When the traveller joins the prayer of the resident person, he completes the prayer.’”595

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. When the traveller joins with the resident person he must do the prayer of the resident person,596 i.e., four rakʿahs, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

191. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd said, ‘Let this absence from your ordinary abode597 not deceive you about your prayer; i.e., when a man is absent on his estates598 and thus shortens the prayer saying, “I am a traveller.”’”599

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If one is at a distance less than the travel of three days and nights one completes the prayer, but if one is at a distance of the travel of three days and nights or more, having no wife and family (ahl)600 601 there and not having decided on staying for fifteen days, then one shortens the prayer. If one decides on staying for fifteen days then one prays the full prayer as long as one is on one’s estate, but then when one sets out again to return to one’s family one shortens the prayer. The distance of three days and nights travel is with the meaning of camels’ travelling or walking on foot, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

192. Muḥammad said, “Saʿīd ibn ʿUbayd aṭ-Ṭā’ī informed us that ʿAlī ibn Rabīʿah al-Wālibī said, ‘I asked ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar , “At what point do you shorten the prayer?602” He said, “Do you know as-Suwaydā’?603” I said, “No, but I have heard of it.” He said, “It is three easy604 nights’ journey. When we go out to it, we shorten the prayer.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”

193. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ḥammād narrated to us that Ibrāhīm said, “When a person who is resident joins someone who is travelling in prayer let him pray two rakʿahs with him and then let him stand up and complete his prayer.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah.”
