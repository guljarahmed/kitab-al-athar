109. A PERSON IN IḤRĀM CONTRACTING A MARRIAGE

370. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham that the Messenger of Allah married Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith at ʿAsfān while he was in iḥrām.”1102 1103

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to it, and we see no harm in that. However, one must not kiss, fondle or embrace1104 until one is finished with iḥrām, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Our people argue by reason of this hadith saying that there is no harm in a person in iḥrām contracting a marriage, but he may not consummate it until he has come out of iḥrām. Mālik, ash-Shāfiʿī and Aḥmad said that it is not permissible for a person in iḥrām to get married himself or to marry off someone else, and that if he does that the marriage is invalid. ↩
  2. Al-Bukhārī narrated it under “marriage” in the chapter on “marriage of someone in iḥrām,” and Muslim under “marriage,” in the chapter on “prohibition of a person in iḥrām marrying or proposing,” and at-Tirmidhī under “Hajj” in the chapter on “what is narrated granting concession concerning that,” and Ibn Mājah under “marriage” in the chapter on “a person in iḥrām marrying.” ↩
  3. Because kissing, touching and the likes of that are causes of sexual intercourse which along with its cause is forbidden to the person in iḥrām. ↩
