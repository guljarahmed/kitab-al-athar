107. WHAT CLOTHING AND SCENT ARE PERMISSIBLE FOR THE PERSON IN IḤRĀM

365. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Khārijah ibn ʿAbdullāh said, ‘I asked Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab about the purse1090 which people in iḥrām wear and he said, “There is no harm in it.”’”1091

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah .”

366. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAṭā’ ibn as-Sā’ib narrated to us that Kathīr ibn Jumhān said, “While ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar was doing saʿy wearing two cloths of harawī1092 yellow a man came up to him and said, ‘Do you wear these pieces of dyed cloth while in iḥrām?’ He answered, ‘We only dyed them with odourless red clay.’”’”1093 1094

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. We see no harm in it because it is not scented nor is it saffron hued, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

367. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ibrāhīm ibn Muḥammad ibn al-Muntashir narrated to us that his father said, “I asked ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar about a man’s being scented while in iḥrām and he said, ‘I would rather rise in the morning and sprinkle myself with tar1095 than rise and sprinkle myself with scent.’”’”1096

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The person in iḥrām ought not to perfume himself with any scent after entering iḥrām.”1097

Footnotes

  1. himyān: a purse in which one’s spending money is put which is tied around one’s middle. ↩
  2. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.96. ↩
  3. harawī is a yellow colour and derives from Herat a city in Khurasan [today in Afghanistan] whose cloths were of a yellow colour. ↩
  4. madar is a red clay without any scent such as violet, jasmine, basil and rose. ↩
  5. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.97. ↩
  6. qaṭīrān is the resin of the juniper, indicating His words, exalted is He, “wearing shirts of qaṭirān” (Sūrah Ibrāhīm: 52). [It may also mean tar or liquid pitch. – Trans.] ↩
  7. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.97. ↩
  8. As for putting on scent before entering iḥrām, the dominant majority take the position that it is recommended when one intends iḥrām, and that its colour and scent enduring does no harm, and it is only prohibited for the person in iḥrām to initiate it. Mālik said that it is not permissible to put on a perfume whose scent lingers afterwards. ↩
