96. THE QIRĀN [HAJJ] AND THE MERIT OF IḤRĀM

325. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Manṣūr ibn al-Muʿtamir narrated to us from Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī from Abū Naṣr as-Sulamī that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, “Whenever you enter upon Hajj and ʿUmrah together then perform two sets of ṭawāf1006 for them and two sets of saʿy between Ṣafā and Marwah.” Manṣūr said, “I met Mujāhid who has giving the fatwā1007 that whoever performed the qirān form of Hajj [coupling it with ʿUmrah in the same iḥrām] should perform a single set of ṭawāf and so I narrated this hadith to him. He said, ‘If I had heard, I would only have given the fatwā that one should perform two sets of ṭawāf, and after today I will only pass such a fatwā.’”’”1008

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

326. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ṭāwus said, ‘Even if I were to perform a thousand Hajjs I would not give up1009 qirān, so much so that we used to call it the Greater Hajj and the Lesser Hajj, and we thought that the Hajj of the person who did not do qirān was not complete.’”1010

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. In our view, qirān is better than others, but they are all beautiful and good, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

327. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb only forbade ifrād1011 but not coupling Hajj with ʿUmrah (qirān). By saying, ‘he forbade ifrād,’ he meant doing only ʿUmrah.”1012 1013

328. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAmr ibn Murrah narrated to us from ʿAbdullāh ibn Salamah that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib said, “The perfection of Hajj and ʿUmrah is that you enter iḥrām for both of them inside your house.”’”1014 1015

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If you hasten iḥrām it is better, if you have mastery over yourself,1016 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

329. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘A shaykh from Rabīʿah narrated to us that Muʿāwiyah ibn Isḥāq al-Qurashī said “The person who goes on Hajj is forgiven as is the person for whom he seeks forgiveness, until the end of Muḥarram.”’”1017

330. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Ayyūb ibn ʿĀ’idh aṭ-Ṭā’ī narrated to us that Mujāhid said, “The person performing the Hajj to the House of Allah, the person performing ʿUmrah and the person waging jihad in the way of Allah are ambassadors for Allah; He called them and they responded to Him, and He gives them what they ask Him for.”’”1018

331. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Muḥammad ibn Mālik al-Hamdānī narrated to us that his father said, “We went out in a party1019 intending to go to Makkah. When we reached ar-Rabdhah1020 a tent appeared before us, and there was Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī in it. We went to him and greeted him, and he lifted a side of the tent1021 and returned our greeting. He said, ‘From where have the people come?’ and we said, ‘from the distant road.’1022 He asked, ‘Where are you going?’ They said, ‘The Ancient House.’ He said, ‘By Allah, He Whom there is no god but Him! Nothing but the Hajj moved you to go forth?’ And he repeated that to us several times, and we swore to it, so he said, ‘Depart to your rites1023 and then turn to face work.’”’”1024
