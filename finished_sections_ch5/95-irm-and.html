THE BOOK OF THE RITES (OF HAJJ)

95. IḤRĀM AND CALLING OUT LABBAYK

323. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Saʿīd ibn Jubayr said when his camel rose to set off with him, ‘At Your service O Allah, at Your service, You have no partner, at Your service. Praise and blessing are Yours and the kingdom, You have no partner. At Your service, God of the Truth, at Your service, Repeatedly Forgiving of wrong actions, at Your service.’”999

Muḥammad said, “If the man wishes he may enter iḥrām1000 when his camel rises to set off with him, or if he wishes after his prayer.1001 The well-known form of the labbayk is up until his words, ‘and the kingdom, You have no partner,’ and what is over that1002 is good. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

324. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿUbaydullāh ibn ʿUmar narrated to us from Nāfiʿ that a man said to Ibn ʿUmar , “Abū ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān, I saw you doing four things.” He said, “What are they?” He said, “I saw that when you intended to enter iḥrām you got up on your mount, then you faced the qiblah and then you entered iḥrām when your camel set off with you. I saw that when you went around the House in ṭawāf, you didn’t pass the Yemeni corner1003 until you had saluted it.1004 I saw you had dyed your beard with yellow, and I saw you performing wuḍū’ in a pair of soft hairless ox-leather sandals.”1005 He said, “I saw the Messenger of Allah doing all of those things and so I have done them.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to all of this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Footnotes

  1. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.94. ↩
  2. iḥrām is the state in which actions not befitting the Hajj or ʿUmrah become ḥarām, and it is also used to denote the male pilgrim’s garments of two pieces of cloth. ↩
  3. And this is the best, and when he says the talbiyah he has entered into iḥrām, meaning if he has intended it. ↩
  4. Indicating that one does not decrease the aforementioned talbiyah transmitted from the Prophet but that anything increased in it is valid. ↩
  5. Contrary to the Shāmī (Syrian) corner. The Yemeni corner is ascribed to the Yemen which is thus named because it is the land to the right (yamīn) of the Kaʿbah. ↩
  6. istalama means to take hold of the stone with the hand, or kiss it, or wipe it with the palm of the hand, and derives from salimah: stones. This act is good according to the outward of the narration and it is narrated of Muḥammad that it is sunnah. ↩
  7. sibt is a hide of oxen tanned with qaraẓ (possibly a type of mimosa) from which sandals are made, named thus because its hair had been shaved off (subita). It means that he did wuḍū’ and donned them while his feet were moist. ↩
