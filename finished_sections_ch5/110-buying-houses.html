110. BUYING HOUSES IN MAKKAH AND RENTING THEM

371. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī Ziyād from Ibn Abī Najīḥ from ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr that the Prophet said, ‘Whoever eats anything from the rents of the houses of Makkah is simply eating fire.’ Abū Ḥanīfah used to deplore its houses being rented out during the Ḥajj1105 and to someone who performs ʿUmrah and then goes home. As for residents and those who come to stay for purposes of study or ʿibādah,1106 he saw no harm in taking rent from them for that.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to that.”

372. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī Ziyād narrated to us from Ibn Abī Najīḥ from ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr that the Prophet said, “Allah made Makkah a Ḥaram, so sale of its houses (ribāʿ)1107 and consuming the money from that is forbidden.”’”1108

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. The land ought not to be sold, but as for [selling] buildings there is no harm in it.”

  2. mujāwir is defined thus by Lane drawing on Tāj al-ʿArūs. – Trans. ↩

Al-Jawharī said, “Ribiʿ is a house itself wherever it is.” Abū Ḥanīfah and Muḥammad said, “It is not permissible to sell the land of Makkah nor to rent it out.” Abū Yūsuf, ash-Shāfiʿī and Aḥmad said that there is no harm in selling its land or in renting it out, and they regarded it in that respect as being the same as all other lands, and today fatwā is given on that basis. ↩
