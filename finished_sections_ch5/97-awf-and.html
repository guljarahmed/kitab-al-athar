97. ṬAWĀF AND RECITATION AT THE KAʿBAH

332. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād from Ibrāhīm that the Messenger of Allah walked briskly1025 from the Stone to the Stone.”1026

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

333. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from a man that ʿAṭā’ ibn Abī Rabāḥ said, ‘The Messenger of Allah walked briskly from the Stone to the Stone.’”1027

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. Walking briskly takes place in the first three circuits from the Black Stone when one begins the ṭawāf until one ends at it with three complete circuits, walking the last four at one’s ordinary pace,1028 and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

334. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us that Ḥammād would perform the saʿy between Ṣafā and Marwah with ʿIkrimah, and Ḥammād would ascend Ṣafā but ʿIkrimah would not, and Ḥammād would ascend Marwah but ʿIkrimah would not. He [Ḥammād] said, ‘I said, “Abū ʿAbdullāh, do you not ascend Ṣafā and Marwah?” So he said, “Thus was the manner of walking of the Messenger of Allah .”’ Ḥammād said, ‘I met Saʿīd ibn Jubayr and mentioned that to him and he said, “The Messenger of Allah only performed ṭawāf mounted while complaining of a sickness, and he touched the corners with a crooked staff.1029 He went between Ṣafā and Marwah mounted, and for that reason did not ascend.”’”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to the verdict of Saʿīd ibn Jubayr. A man ought to ascend Ṣafā and Marwah, and then face towards the Kaʿbah where he can see it and supplicate, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

335. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Saʿīd ibn Jubayr recited the Qur’ān at the Kaʿbah in the first rakʿah and in the second rakʿah, ‘Say: He is Allah, Absolute Oneness....’ (Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ).”1030

Muḥammad said, “We see no harm in this if he understands what he is saying, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”
