103. SOMEONE WHO IS CUPPED WHILE IN IḤRĀM AND SHAVING THE HEAD

351. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū’s-Sawwār narrated to us from Abū Ḥāḍir that the Messenger of Allah was cupped while he was fasting in iḥrām.’”1067

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this, but the person in iḥrām ought not to have any hair shaven when he is cupped, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

352. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘Whoever trims1068 [the hair of] the head of women, that is better, but shaving is better for men,’ meaning in iḥrām.”

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him, but it is preferable that a woman does not trim less than the top finger joint from the sides of her head.”

Footnotes

  1. Al-Bukhārī narrated it as a marfūʿ hadith under “fasting” in the chapter on “cupping and vomiting for the fasting person,” and Abū Dāwūd under “fasting” in the chapter on “the concession granted for that,” and at-Tirmidhī under “fasting” in the chapter “what has been narrated concerning the concession granted for that.” ↩
  2. Shortening is that one takes from the extremities of the hair to the measure of a fingertip, and nothing is permitted women except to shorten their hair. ↩
