105. HUNTING IN IḤRĀM

357. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If you embark upon both of them together, ʿUmrah and Hajj, and then you catch game, you have to pay two requitals, but if you embark upon ʿUmrah alone, you owe a single requital and if you embark upon Hajj alone, you owe a single requital.’”1073 1074

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

358. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Muḥammad ibn al-Munkadir narrated to us that Abū Qatādah said, “I set out in a body1075 of the Companions of the Messenger of Allah and every single one of them was in iḥrām apart from me. I saw a herd of wild asses and so I sprang to my horse and mounted it. I had been too hasty, leaving behind my horsewhip, so I said to them, ‘Pass it up to me,’1076 but they refused1077 and so I dismounted and got my horsewhip, remounted and went in search of the herd of wild asses, and caught one of them. I ate and they ate with me.”’”

359. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Abū Salamah narrated to us from a man that Abū Hurayrah said, “I passed through Baḥrayn and they asked me whether the meat of game animals caught by someone not in iḥrām is permissible for someone in iḥrām to eat, and I gave them the fatwā to eat it, but there remained something [of doubt] in my self. Then I went to see ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and mentioned what I had said to them, and he said, ‘Had you said anything else to them, you would not have given fatwā between any two people as long as you remained alive.’”’”1078 1079

360. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Hishām ibn ʿUrwah narrated to us from his father that his grandfather az-Zubayr ibn al-ʿAwwām said, “We used to carry dried strips1080 of game meat which we would take as travelling provision1081 and eat from while in iḥrām along with the Messenger of Allah .”’”1082

361. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Muḥammad ibn al-Munkadir from ʿUthmān ibn Muḥammad that Ṭalḥah ibn ʿUbaydullāh said, ‘While the Prophet was asleep we were discussing game meat which the person in iḥrām eats, and our voices became louder. So the Prophet awoke and asked, “What are you arguing about?” We said, “Game meat that a person in iḥrām eats,” and he told us to eat it.’”1083

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If a person who is not in iḥrām slaughters the game, there is no harm for the person in iḥrām eating it even if the slaughter was for his [the former’s] sake, and that is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.”

Muḥammad said, “I think that in this hadith they were quarrelling about the fiqh and so their voices rose, and then the Prophet awoke because of that, but he did not find fault with them about it.”

362. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us from Ḥammād that Ibrāhīm said, ‘If people who are in iḥrām share in hunting game, each one of them owes its requital.’”1084

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. Do you not see that if people unpremeditatedly kill a man, doing it together, then each one owes the expiation of freeing a believing slave, and if they do not find that, then [they owe] to fast two consecutive months?”

363. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Al-Haytham ibn Abī’l-Haytham narrated to us from aṣ-Ṣalt ibn Ḥunayn that while in iḥrām ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar was presented two gazelles and some ostrich eggs as a gift but he refused to accept them and said, “Why did you not slaughter these two before bringing them to me?”’”1085

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this. If any game is brought alive into the Ḥaram it is not ḥalāl to slaughter it nor to sell it and one must let it go. That is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him.””
