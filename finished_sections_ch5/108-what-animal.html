108. WHAT ANIMAL LIFE THE PERSON IN IḤRĀM MAY KILL

368. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Nāfiʿ narrated to us that Ibn ʿUmar said, “The person in iḥrām may kill1098 rats and mice, snakes, mad dogs,1099 kites and scorpions.”’”1100

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this and it is the verdict of Abū Ḥanīfah, may Allah, exalted is He, have mercy on him. Any beast of prey which attacks you and which you kill, is not held against you.”

369. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Sālim al-Afṭas narrated to us that Saʿīd ibn Jubayr said, “I accompanied Ibn ʿUmar and he saw a kite behind his camel and took a bow and shot it while in iḥrām.”’”1101

Footnotes

  1. No wrong action will be recorded for the person in iḥrām for killing the above mentioned, and the dominant majority take the position that the ruling is universal with respect to everything that causes harm. ↩
  2. A mad dog which bites. [Al-kalb al-ʿaqūr, literally, “the wounding dog,” but has been applied to any animal of prey such as lions, leopards, lynxes and wolves. – Trans.] ↩
