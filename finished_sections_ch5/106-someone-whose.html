106. SOMEONE WHOSE HADY OFFERING DIES ON THE JOURNEY

364. Muḥammad said, “Abū Ḥanīfah informed us saying, ‘Manṣūr ibn al-Muʿtamir narrated to us from Ibrāhīm an-Nakhaʿī from ʿĀ’ishah, the Mother of the Mu’minūn , “She said, [when] I asked her about what I should do with the hady offering which died1086 on the journey? ‘It is preferable to me to eat it rather than leaving it for beasts of prey.’””’ Abū Ḥanīfah said, ‘If it had been an obligatory [sacrifice], do whatever you like with it, but you have to [sacrifice] in its place. If it was a voluntary [sacrifice], give it as ṣadaqah to the needy.1087 If that is in a place where there are no needy people, then slaughter it, plunge its sandal1088 in its blood and strike its side with it, and leave it for people to eat. If you eat anything of it, you have to give in its place [as a sacrifice] the equivalent of what you have eaten. If you wish you may do whatever you wish with it, and then [sacrifice another animal] in its place.’”1089

Muḥammad said, “We adhere to this.”

Footnotes

  1. i.e., which perished, and what is meant by it is that it drew close to perishing [and was then sacrificed before dying naturally]. ↩
  2. And he himself and others who are without need should not eat it. ↩
  3. What is meant by sandal is the garland on its neck because in the main they are pieces of sandal. The point of that is that people should know that it has been sacrificed and so that the needy should eat from it but not those without need. ↩
  4. Abū Yūsuf narrated it on p.112 in which there is, “From Manṣūr from Ibrāhīm from ʿĀ’ishah that her husband sacrificed a hady offering...” ↩
